## Status Values

Grouped by what they imply about canon (see the Canon Index above for evidence tier). No status above `theorem_backed`; nothing in this table is a proven general theorem.

**Active / in progress**

- `open` - under active formalization or challenge; no settled verdict.
- `open_formal_target` - admitted as a research target only, with explicit
  definitions, tests, and failure conditions.
- `candidate` - a proposed formal connection not yet established (qualifiers like
  "re-scoped" belong in the body, not the status token).

**Supported (finite-witness; not a general theorem)**

- `partially_supported` - finite tests support a narrowed form, while major
  physical or generalization conditions remain open.
- `proto_independent` - a formal kernel has a typed executable interface and
  cross-domain positive cases, but lacks a representation theorem or enough
  external-domain evidence to stand as a separate theory.
- `active` - currently used as a guardrail, analogy, or literature bridge rather
  than a standalone supported claim.

**Downgraded / qualified**

- `revised` - changed substantially from an earlier version.
- `weakened` - still useful but no longer supports its original ambition.
- `weakened_conditional` - survives only under an explicitly named added
  condition (e.g. H7's D1-monotone / resource condition).
- `requires_added_assumption` - a specific route holds only under a stated extra
  assumption; the broader program stays open (S1 finite-colimit route).
- `demoted` - no longer a single supported claim; survives only as a
  navigation/umbrella label until a child branch clears its gate (Q1).

**Branch disposition (Q1 children)**

- `bookkeeping_only` - survives only as accounting discipline over already-formed
  records, with no independent finality content (Q1A).
- `externally_blocked` - an admissibility protocol that cannot be scored until an
  external pre-registered / real event-level packet is supplied (Q1B).
- `dormant` - reinstatement-only; null until a concrete platform clears the named
  gate (Q1C).
- `guardrail_only` - constrains language only; not a new theorem, repair, or
  operational prediction (Q1D).

**Terminal**

- `rejected` - failed a test or was superseded. (Currently unused; retained as
  the terminal verdict.)

### 2026-06-19 - Causal Reduction of CHSH Holonomy (T65)

- H1-Sheaf remains `partially_supported`: T65 reduces the CHSH holonomy = -1

**What is proved:**

  causal mechanisms. This is Bell's theorem as a holonomy statement.

  temporal (R's finalization witnesses are in R's causal future), T65 is spatial
  (joint measurement information is in neither party's causal region). Both give

- The biconditional `locally_causal(s) <=> holonomy(s) = +1` is FALSE.
  128 of 256 sections have holonomy = +1 but only 16 are locally causal.
  Holonomy (product of 4 binary transition functions) = +1 when there are 0, 2,
  or 4 negative transitions (even parity), not only when all transitions = +1.
  A coboundary in the abstract topological sense (H^1(4-cycle, Z/2Z) = Z/2Z)
  is not the same as a globally consistent physical section. The forward
  direction (LC => holonomy = +1) is sufficient for Bell's theorem.

**General principle (conjectured, forward direction supported):**
`H^1(cover, Z/2Z) != 0 when globally consistent assignment requires evidence
inaccessible from any bounded causal region in the cover.`
