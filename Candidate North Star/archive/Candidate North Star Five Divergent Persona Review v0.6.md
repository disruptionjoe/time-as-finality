# Candidate North Star Five Divergent Persona Review v0.6

## Status

This is a review and patch synthesis for:

```text
Candidate North Star v0.6.md
```

It is not a replacement draft. It records five deliberately divergent review
lenses, then extracts the small set of updates worth applying before sharing
v0.6 with an outside friend for pushback.

## Executive Synthesis

Verdict:

```text
v0.6 is an upgradeable, friend-sendable candidate.
```

It should not be conceptually rebuilt before external pushback. The posture is
now coherent:

- non-factorization is old;
- the proposed value is the audit discipline;
- prior-art absorption is expected and useful;
- physics enters only through known physics inducing typed `Cap`;
- canonical residue remains a high bar, not the default expectation.

The most useful pre-send updates are not new examples or stronger claims. They
are reader-orientation patches:

1. Add a short "what to push on" section near the top.
2. Define the same-neighbor-data condition inside the note.
3. Clarify that the formal core is the exact/equivalence-valued case, with
   native-comparison variants treated as audit regimes rather than one
   universal quotient theorem.
4. Add a compact generic witness template, not a long worked example.
5. Make the final formulation more explicitly friend-facing and falsifiable.

Defer:

- citations in the main note;
- long domain examples;
- a stronger physics narrative;
- moving dark matter or dark energy back into the main text;
- a paper-style theorem program.

## Persona 1: Hostile Formalist

Posture:

```text
I do not care whether the prose feels right. Is the math well typed?
```

Findings:

- The codomain repair to `K / ~=_K` is the right move.
- Adding `A subset Y` is good; it handles admissible domains, partial witness
  constraints, and finite-budget cases.
- The fiber-constancy statement is now properly about visible-equivalence
  fibers, not only literal fibers.
- The document still needs one sentence saying the formal core is the
  equivalence-valued case. The native-comparison table is not a single theorem
  schema unless each comparison structure supplies its own morphisms,
  thresholds, or reflections.
- "Minimal" remains controlled by the capability-kernel wording. Keep warning
  that minimality depends on the allowed morphism class.

Recommended patch:

```text
Add one scope sentence before or inside Native Capability Comparison:
"The exact formal core is the equivalence-valued case; other rows are audit
regimes that must supply their own native comparison and preservation notion."
```

## Persona 2: Prior-Art Absorber

Posture:

```text
Assume every interesting claim is already owned until proven otherwise.
```

Findings:

- The absorption-first posture is strong.
- Native state completion and native theorem absorption are named correctly.
- The note still uses "same-neighbor data" in the residue ladder without
  defining it for a new reader.
- A friend will likely ask: "How do I know you did not choose the impoverished
  projection just to manufacture failure?" The answer is same-neighbor-data
  testing.
- The domain calibration table is useful and compact.

Recommended patch:

```text
Define same-neighbor-data:
"Before assigning residue, grant the neighboring theory all state variables,
operations, equivalences, access structures, and comparison criteria it
normally treats as legitimate under the same observer/task/horizon/boundary."
```

## Persona 3: Physics Firewall

Posture:

```text
No free physics. No reverse inference. No local/global access smuggling.
```

Findings:

- The no-free-physics section is one of the strongest parts of v0.6.
- The QRT ledger is correctly demoted to translation residue.
- GR, thermodynamics, detector provenance, topological response, and EFT/RG
  are framed as anchors, stress tests, or absorbers rather than proof.
- Dark matter and dark energy remain properly demoted.
- "Capability trajectories along physical time" is safe because it preserves
  physical time rather than replacing it.

Recommended patch:

```text
No conceptual physics patch needed.
Keep the current firewall intact.
```

## Persona 4: Systems And Witness Auditor

Posture:

```text
Can I run this audit on a concrete system without guessing the missing fields?
```

Findings:

- The reviewer checklist is useful but long.
- The witness ledger demonstrates the audit's behavior, especially through the
  ANN demotion.
- A friend could still ask, "What would a proposed witness look like in one
  page?"
- A compact generic witness template would increase usability without adding a
  long example section.

Recommended patch:

```text
Add a generic witness template:
Domain, neighboring absorber, O/T/h/B, A, pi, X, ~=_X, Cap, K, R_K,
positive control, negative fixture, enrichments tested, absorber result,
residue label, demotion condition.
```

next move is to invite the friend to attack the audit's typing, absorber
discipline, physics boundary, and actual residue rather than ask whether the
