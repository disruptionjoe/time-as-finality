## 1. The Past Is What Has Become Hard To Undo

`[CORE CLAIM]` [The past is not merely what happened earlier.](claims/C1-experienced-time-as-record-finality.md) For an embedded observer, the past is what has become finalized into durable records.

That sentence is the heart of the essay.

The ordinary picture says time flows, events happen, and some events leave records. I want to reverse the emphasis. We do not first experience an abstract river called time and then notice records floating in it. We experience a world in which some things have become settled enough that later things must deal with them. A scar, a photograph, a fossil, a memory, a detector reading, a crater, a database entry, a broken cup, a body hitting a wall: these are not merely things that are "in the past." They are the past becoming difficult to reorganize.

`[CORE CLAIM]` [Time, as experienced by embedded observers, is the order assigned to the accumulation of such finalized records.](claims/C1-experienced-time-as-record-finality.md)

This is not yet a physics theory. It is a claim about the architecture of experience and classical fact formation. But it is a claim with teeth. It suggests that the future is not simply "later." The future is what has not yet become finalized into the relevant record network. The present is not a universal spotlight moving across the cosmos. The present is the local edge where candidate states become settled enough to build on.

`[TEST RESULT]` T1 does not establish that full sentence. It establishes the
narrower result that accessible stabilization frontiers can induce a temporal
partial order for a reconciler. Causally independent finalized records remain
unordered, and the model contains no phenomenal-experience bridge.

In the shortest possible plain-language form:

`[CORE CLAIM]` [Reality is not only what exists. Classical reality is what has become hard to undo.](claims/C1-experienced-time-as-record-finality.md)

## 2. Which "Time" Are We Talking About?

`[DEFINITION]` The word "time" names several different things that should not be collapsed.

There is coordinate time: a parameter inside a model. There is proper time: the invariant clock measure along a timelike worldline in relativity. There is the thermodynamic arrow: the entropy-asymmetric direction in our universe. There is phenomenological time: the felt order of experience. There is metaphysical becoming: the question of whether the future is genuinely open or whether the whole spacetime structure is equally real.

`[NOT CLAIMED]` [This essay does not replace coordinate time, proper time, thermodynamics, or the equations of relativity.](guardrails/G2-not-a-replacement-theory.md)

The target is narrower:

`[CORE CLAIM]` [The essay concerns experienced temporal order and classical fact formation: how embedded systems come to live inside a world of settled records.](claims/C1-experienced-time-as-record-finality.md)

That narrower target matters. If the essay tried to prove that spacetime geometry is "really" consensus in one jump, it would deserve suspicion. If it tried to solve the measurement problem by changing vocabulary, it would be too easy. If it claimed that clocks tick because people agree that they tick, it would be nonsense.

The claim is different:

`[CORE CLAIM]` Whatever time is at the deepest physical level, an embedded observer encounters time through the local integration of records that have become stable enough to constrain future experience.

This is why the claim can coexist with conservative physics. One could hold a block-universe view and still say that beings inside the block experience time through the record structure along their worldlines. One could hold a more radical view and say that the block itself emerges from a deeper source. The core essay does not need to settle that. It only needs to ask whether temporal experience is inseparable from record finality.

## 3. Finality Without Circularity

The phrase "finality" comes from distributed systems, but if we define finality as "the moment something becomes final," we have already smuggled time into the definition. That would be circular.

So the physics-facing definition must be different.

`[DEFINITION]` [Physical finality v0.1 is an observer-indexed componentwise
preorder among proposition-value pairs.](claims/D1-physical-finality-definition.md)
The executable profile keeps accessible support, distinct-holder redundancy,
causal branch support, and graph reversal count separate rather than combining
them into a weighted score.

The four parts matter.

First, redundant encoding. A record becomes more final when it is copied or correlated across many independent systems. One private memory is fragile. A measurement recorded by multiple instruments, environmental traces, written logs, and later consequences is harder to erase.

Second, causal accessibility. A record matters for an observer only if it can, in principle, enter that observer's causal history. Records outside causal reach may be real in some other domain, but they are not part of that observer's settled world.

Third, robustness. A record is more final when later compatible observations keep reinforcing it rather than destabilizing it. A rumor is not robust. A spectral line confirmed by independent labs is more robust. A footprint in wet sand is less robust than a geological stratum, not because one is real and the other is not, but because their record-stability differs.

Fourth, reversal cost. A state is more final when undoing it would require reorganizing many correlated traces. A quantum state isolated in a lab may be reversible in principle. A broken vase scattered across the floor, photographed, remembered, and entered into an insurance form is not logically irreversible, but reversing it would mean reversing a world of correlated records.

`[NOT CLAIMED]` Finality is not absolute metaphysical impossibility. It is practical, physical, and domain-relative stability.

This lets us speak about degrees of finality without pretending reality is binary. Ideas can be real but low-finality. Institutions can be high-finality in human social systems while remaining physically revisable. A wave in water is real but transient. A mountain is real and high-finality at human timescales, but not eternal. A particle track in a detector is a record; how final it is depends on how it propagates into other records.

`[CORE CLAIM, WEAKENED]` Accessible record-stabilization frontiers can induce
an observer-relative temporal partial order. Whether conscious experience
turns that partial order into felt succession remains an open bridge problem.

This is still only a conceptual frame. But now it avoids the easiest circularity. We are not defining finality by time. We are defining finality by record-stability, then asking whether experienced time is reconstructed from the ordering of such stabilization.

`[NOT CLAIMED]` This does not mean every observer's accessible record set only grows. Memories fail, archives burn, signals are lost, and local access can degrade. The claim is about the physical stabilization of record relations, not about perfect personal recall.

`[NOT CLAIMED]` This essay does not derive the thermodynamic arrow. It assumes that our universe supports durable records because of its thermodynamic boundary conditions, then asks how embedded systems experience that record-supporting world as time.

## 4. The Distributed-Systems Analogy

`[ANALOGY]` [In distributed systems, finality is the difference between a candidate state and a committed state.](claims/A1-distributed-systems-finality-analogy.md)

A network may contain many local views. Messages can arrive in different orders. Some nodes have seen one transaction; others have not. A fork may exist. A write may be pending. A quorum may not yet be formed. Until finality, the system has activity and structure, but later operations cannot safely treat the candidate state as settled.

This language is useful because it distinguishes sequence from commitment.

A log can have sequence without safety. A rumor can spread before it is verified. A possible event can have probability before it becomes a record. A computation can explore branches before one branch is committed to the durable state. The important transition is not just "after." It is "safe to build on."

`[ANALOGY]` Classical reality resembles a high-finality ledger, but the validators are [physical record-bearing systems](claims/D2-observer-as-record-bearing-system.md), not merely people.

This is the point at which the analogy must be guarded.

`[NOT CLAIMED]` [Human belief does not create matter.](guardrails/G1-human-belief-does-not-create-matter.md) The relevant agreement is physical, not psychological.

A wall is not solid because humans agreed to call it solid. It is solid because its material structure, electromagnetic interactions, photons, pressure waves, your body, your nervous system, dust, tools, memories, and instruments converge on the same constraint. You can disagree with the wall socially, but your shoulder will get a rapid local proof. The collision is not an opinion.

In this sense, "consensus reality" is a dangerous phrase unless consensus means physical record-consistency, not social preference.

`[CORE CLAIM]` Classical objectivity is the high-finality limit of record redundancy.

`[KNOWN NEIGHBOR]` [This has neighbors in decoherence and quantum Darwinism](literature/N1-known-neighbors.md), where classical objectivity is associated with redundant environmental records.

The essay is not claiming to invent that family of ideas. Its contribution, if it has one, is to bring that record-redundancy picture into conversation with finality, distributed systems, relativity, and temporal experience.

## 5. Quantum States As Real But Not Yet Classical-Final

`[CONJECTURE]` [Quantum states may be real but not yet finalized as classical facts in a particular observer-environment context.](claims/Q1-quantum-under-finalization.md)

The caveat is doing real work.

"Not finalized" does not mean unreal. It does not mean vague. It does not mean waiting for a human mind. A quantum state can be mathematically precise, experimentally confirmed, and physically consequential without being a finalized classical record in a given basis.

`[DEFINITION]` Under-finalized means not yet stabilized as a classical record across the relevant observer-environment network.

A superposition is not merely ignorance. It is a real structure that has not yielded a single stable classical record in the relevant measurement context. An entangled state is not two independent finalized local facts hiding behind a curtain. It is a non-separable joint constraint whose local classical outcomes are not independently finalized in advance in the naive sense.

The analogy becomes:

| Quantum language | Finality language |
| --- | --- |
| Superposition | Real candidate structure not yet classically finalized in a given basis |
| Measurement record | A durable classical commitment |
| Decoherence | Loss of accessible interference through environmental record-spreading |
| Pointer basis | A record-stable basis selected by system-environment interaction |
| Entanglement | Non-separable constraint without independent local classical finality |
| Classical fact | A record-stable state robust enough for many observers to treat as settled |

`[NOT CLAIMED]` [Decoherence by itself does not solve every interpretation-level question about why one outcome is experienced.](guardrails/G2-not-a-replacement-theory.md)

That guardrail is essential. Decoherence explains why interference becomes inaccessible and why certain record bases become stable. It does not, all by itself, settle the whole measurement problem for every interpretation of quantum mechanics. The finality view should therefore be read as a claim about classical record formation, not as a magic collapse mechanism.

`[NOT CLAIMED]` This is not a return to naive local hidden variables.

Bell-type constraints rule out the simple story that particles merely carry prewritten local classical answers that measurement reveals. The viable finality claim is different:

`[CONJECTURE]` [Before measurement, the joint quantum constraint can be real while the local classical facts are not yet finalized.](claims/Q1-quantum-under-finalization.md)

Entanglement is especially delicate. It is tempting to compare entanglement to non-final consensus or correlated ledgers. That can be useful, but only if it preserves no-signalling.

`[NOT CLAIMED]` Entanglement is not usable faster-than-light communication.

In finality language, entanglement is better described as non-separable constraint without local controllable finality transfer. The correlations are real. The ability to use them as arbitrary superluminal messages is not.

`[POTENTIAL BYPRODUCT]` The phrase "under-finalized but real" may help public readers avoid a false binary between "fully classical fact" and "not real."

That may be one of the essay's strongest explanatory payoffs. Quantum states, ideas, memories, institutions, probabilities, and possible futures may all be real in different ways because they occupy different finality regimes. The world is not cleanly divided into real and unreal. It is stratified by how records stabilize.

## 6. Relativity As No Universal Commit Order

`[CONJECTURE]` [Relativity is naturally compatible with a universe where finality is local, causally bounded, and observer-indexed rather than global and instant.](claims/R1-relativity-no-global-commit-order.md)

This claim must not be overstated.

`[NOT CLAIMED]` [The finality view does not replace special relativity, general relativity, Minkowski geometry, Lorentz invariance, or proper time.](guardrails/G2-not-a-replacement-theory.md)

Proper time remains the invariant clock measure along a timelike worldline. The finality claim is narrower: an embedded observer experiences that measure through locally accumulated records, and spacelike-separated events lack a universal finalized order because no causal record relation connects them yet.

The distributed analogy is helpful here. In a network with propagation delays, no node has an instant God's-eye view. Some events are causally ordered: if A causes B, any valid history must respect A before B. Other events are independent: no single global order is forced until a protocol imposes one or their effects meet in a shared future.

Relativity gives spacetime a structurally similar lesson. There is no universal now. Events outside one another's light cones do not have an observer-independent temporal order in the same way causally connected events do.

`[ANALOGY]` A light cone is like a boundary on possible record-finality propagation.

The analogy is not exact. A light cone is geometry, not a computer network. But it marks the causal structure of what can affect what. If a record cannot reach you, it cannot become part of your local finalized history. If two events cannot exchange causal influence, their relative order is not globally settled for all observers.

`[CONJECTURE]` [Relativity of simultaneity is what finality looks like when no observer has access to a universal commit order.](claims/R1-relativity-no-global-commit-order.md)

The phrase "commit order" should not be taken literally as a cosmic blockchain. It means something more modest: there is no single physically available ordering relation that every observer can apply to spacelike-separated events. Each observer carries a locally consistent history. Shared reality is reconciled where records can meet inside a common causal future.

Time dilation also fits this language if handled carefully. A clock is a physical system that accumulates state transitions. Its proper time is not merely a log, but an invariant measure. Still, what an observer actually has access to is a record of ticks along a path. When two clocks reunite and compare, the difference in elapsed proper time is read through the difference in accumulated records.

`[CORE CLAIM]` The experienced side of time is inseparable from the accumulation and comparison of local records.

## 7. Black Holes As Finality-Domain Stress Tests

`[POTENTIAL BYPRODUCT]` [Black holes become a stress test for finality-domain boundaries.](claims/B1-black-holes-finality-boundaries.md)

The reason is simple: a horizon divides causal access. Once an event is inside the horizon, direct classical records from that event cannot propagate back to future null infinity. Exterior observers cannot receive ordinary messages from the interior. The infalling observer, by contrast, may cross the horizon in finite proper time without encountering a locally dramatic wall at the horizon.

`[NOT CLAIMED]` The horizon does not make interior events unreal, and it is not a wall for the infalling observer.

The claim is about record accessibility.

`[CONJECTURE]` [A black-hole horizon is a boundary between finality domains: interior events may be locally final for infalling systems while remaining unavailable as direct classical records to exterior observers.](claims/B1-black-holes-finality-boundaries.md)

That is more precise than saying "the finality channel is cut." The direct classical export channel from the interior to the exterior is unavailable. Exterior observers can only work with records that remain outside, are encoded at or near the boundary, or are recovered indirectly through radiation if unitarity is preserved.

This reframes the information puzzle without pretending to solve it:

`[POTENTIAL BYPRODUCT]` [The black-hole information problem can be described as a question about whether and how interior record-finality is represented in exterior-accessible degrees of freedom.](claims/B1-black-holes-finality-boundaries.md)

If information is preserved, exterior-accessible records must in some sense carry enough structure to recover what would otherwise seem lost. If holographic ideas are right, the boundary description may encode the physically meaningful content of the bulk. If evaporation is unitary, Hawking radiation cannot be merely featureless thermal loss forever. These are technical claims beyond this essay's authority, but finality language gives a useful way to ask the question:

> What happens to records whose direct classical export path is blocked by a horizon?

Black holes matter here because they prevent sloppy global thinking. They force locality, observer-dependence, record access, and reconstruction into the foreground. If the finality view cannot speak carefully about black holes, it is not mature enough for public use.

## 8. The Toy Formalization

`[TEST]` [To move beyond metaphor, define a causal record graph.](tests/T1-record-graph-temporal-reconstruction.md)

Here is a toy model. It is not physics. It is a scaffold for asking better questions.

```text
Let G be a directed causal record graph.

Nodes are record-bearing systems.
Edges are record-transfer relations.

For a proposition p and observer-system O, define F_O(p) as a
degree of finality that increases with:

  R(p): independent redundant records accessible to O
  S(p): stability under later compatible observations
  C(p): cost of reversing or erasing correlated records
  L(p): causal reachability from p-relevant records to O

Experienced temporal order for O is reconstructed from:

  1. the causal partial order of record-transfer relations, and
  2. the growth or stabilization of F_O(p) for propositions O treats as settled.
```

This model is crude, but it has virtues. It defines finality without first saying "the moment when." It gives the hypothesis something to fail against. It distinguishes global truth from observer-accessible record finality. It makes relativity a constraint rather than an afterthought, because `L(p)` must respect causal reachability. It also gives quantum and thermodynamic objections somewhere to attach.

The model must also distinguish loss of local access from physical reversal. If an observer forgets a fact, the observer's access has changed; the physical record network may not have been unfinalized. Conversely, if enough correlated records are erased or made inaccessible, finality for that observer-network may genuinely decrease. A serious version of the model would need to track those cases separately.

`[TEST]` [The framework becomes more than metaphor only if it can reconstruct temporal ordering from record formation while preserving known physics.](TESTS.md)

Minimum compatibility constraints:

- It must not violate no-signalling.
- It must not replace proper time with social agreement.
- It must not treat decoherence as a complete solution to the measurement problem.
- It must not imply that human belief creates matter.
- It must not derive the thermodynamic arrow from handwaving.
- It must not require a hidden universal present.
- It must not claim black-hole interiors are unreal for infalling observers.

This is the discipline the idea needs.

These denials may feel repetitive, but they are load-bearing. The essay is trying to preserve a narrow, interesting bridge:

`[CORE CLAIM]` The world as experienced by embedded observers is a world of records becoming stable enough to constrain what comes next.

That claim does not need the more fragile claims. It does not need "mass is finality density." It does not need "gravity is consensus." It does not need a fully worked theory of consciousness. It does not need to defeat all no-go theorems by appeal to a mysterious higher layer.

The essay becomes stronger when it refuses those shortcuts.

## 10. Speculative Extension: Spacetime As Consensus Envelope

`[SPECULATIVE EXTENSION]` [Spacetime may be a consensus envelope for causally bounded finality.](claims/S1-spacetime-consensus-envelope.md)

This is the bold version. It should not be mistaken for the core claim.

The conservative version says: whatever spacetime is, embedded observers experience time through record finality. The radical version says: the structure we call spacetime may itself be the stable interface that allows many bounded observer-systems to maintain compatible histories.

In that radical picture, a deeper source structure may not be directly experienced as classical spacetime. Observer systems access it through limited protocols. Those protocols generate records, constraints, and finality. The shared 4D world is the stable interface in which many record-bearing systems can reconcile histories without requiring a universal now.

`[NOT CLAIMED]` [This does not defeat no-go theorems by declaration.](guardrails/G2-not-a-replacement-theory.md)

If a theorem applies under stated assumptions, it applies. The legitimate question is narrower:

`[TEST]` [Which assumptions belong to the rendered/classical interface, and which assumptions would have to be preserved by any deeper-source map?](open-problems/rendered-interface-assumptions.md)

That is a research question, not a rhetorical escape hatch. The burden is on the speculative extension to define the map between source, observer protocol, and classical record layer. Without that map, "deeper source" language is only a suggestive metaphor.

For public v2, this section should remain late and explicitly labeled. The essay does not need the reader to accept it. It only needs the reader to see why the core claim could point beyond itself.

## 11. Why This Might Be Useful Even If It Is Not New Physics

`[POTENTIAL BYPRODUCT]` The value of the finality frame may be conceptual unification before prediction.

It gathers several ideas under one question:

> What makes a state settled enough for later reality to depend on it?

In quantum foundations, that question points toward measurement records, decoherence, pointer bases, and classical objectivity. In relativity, it points toward causal access, local histories, and the absence of global simultaneity. In thermodynamics, it points toward irreversibility, entropy gradients, and the cost of reversing traces. In black-hole physics, it points toward horizon-bounded record accessibility and reconstruction.

`[KNOWN NEIGHBOR]` [None of those areas needs this essay to exist.](literature/N1-known-neighbors.md) The question is whether finality gives them a shared vocabulary without flattening their differences.

That is the honest pitch. The essay is not a theory of everything. It is a lens. A good lens does not replace the object it looks at. It makes certain structure easier to see.

## 12. The Research Program

`[TEST]` [The next step is to formalize finality in at least one bounded model.](TESTS.md)

A useful research program would proceed in layers:

1. Define record-bearing systems and causal record-transfer relations.
2. Define a finality measure that depends on redundancy, stability, reachability, and reversal cost.
3. Show how observer-relative temporal order can be reconstructed from the record graph.
4. Test the construction against simple relativistic cases: spacelike-separated events, reunited clocks, causal diamonds.
5. Test the construction against simple quantum cases: measurement records, decoherence, quantum eraser setups, entangled pairs with no-signalling preserved.
6. Test black-hole language only after the ordinary causal-access model is clean.

`[TEST]` [The idea fails if it cannot reconstruct temporal order without reintroducing primitive time under another name.](tests/T1-record-graph-temporal-reconstruction.md)

It also fails if it predicts superluminal signalling, denies proper time, treats human agreement as physical causation, or claims that record redundancy alone solves the measurement problem.

Those failure conditions are not weaknesses. They are signs that the idea is becoming crisp enough to be criticized.

## 13. Closing: The World Becoming Accountable To Itself

`[CORE CLAIM]` [The past is what has become hard to undo.](claims/C1-experienced-time-as-record-finality.md)

Not because humans believe it. Not because the universe keeps minutes in a cosmic notebook. Not because every event becomes instantly known everywhere. The past is hard to undo because records propagate, stabilize, constrain, and become woven into further records.

`[CORE CLAIM]` [Time, as experienced by embedded observers, is the order of that becoming-final.](claims/C1-experienced-time-as-record-finality.md)

Quantum states remind us that reality can be real before it is classical-final. Relativity reminds us that no observer owns a universal now. Black holes remind us that record access can be domain-bounded. Thermodynamics reminds us that stable records require physical conditions. Consciousness reminds us that finality is not only stored; it is lived.

The bold extension may or may not survive:

`[SPECULATIVE EXTENSION]` [Perhaps spacetime is the consensus envelope inside which bounded systems can share a reality.](claims/S1-spacetime-consensus-envelope.md)

But the core claim is already enough to work with:

`[CORE CLAIM]` We do not merely move through time. We inhabit a network of records becoming settled enough that the world can continue from them.

If time is finality, then the universe is not merely happening. It is becoming accountable to itself.
