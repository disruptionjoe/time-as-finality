### New Open Problems

- [Spacetime as Finality Colimit](open-problems/spacetime-as-finality-colimit.md): Five independent formalisms (topos theory, NCG, QFT path integral, stochastic geometry, Wolfram) converge on: classical spacetime is the colimit of observer-local finality domains, not their arena. Highest upside claim in the sprint.
- [Arrow of Time as Constructor Theorem](open-problems/arrow-of-time-as-constructor-theorem.md): Six approaches converge on: temporal asymmetry is a theorem from impossible transformations on finality, not a cosmological boundary condition.
- [Observer Closure Theorem](open-problems/observer-closure-theorem.md): Any embedded recorder under D1 necessarily generates a self-stabilizing fixed-point subgraph — observer-world loops are structurally inevitable. Formal scaffold for the phenomenal bridge.
- [Finality Gauge Theory and Gravity](open-problems/finality-gauge-theory-and-gravity.md): D1 as a connection on a bundle over the record graph; curvature as gravitational field. First step: gauge invariance audit of D1's four dimensions.
- [Finality as Anomaly Cancellation](open-problems/finality-as-anomaly-cancellation.md): Distinct-holder redundancy as 't Hooft anomaly matching condition — under-finalized states are anomalous (structurally forbidden in the IR), not just dynamically suppressed.
- [First-Person Finality and Complexity Separation](open-problems/first-person-finality-complexity-separation.md): Is first-person finality verification provably richer than third-person record-graph computation? If so, the phenomenal bridge gap is a formal theorem, not an epistemic gap.

Four ideas had strong multi-disciplinary convergence in the voting pass — independent disciplines using different formalisms arrived at the same structural claim:

1. **Spacetime from finality aggregation** — 5 formalisms, all saying spacetime is the output, not the arena.
2. **Phenomenal bridge as formal incompleteness** — 5 formalisms, all saying the gap admits a theorem rather than a discovery.
3. **Temporal direction from impossible transformations** — 6 formalisms, all deriving time's arrow without entropy.
4. **Distributed consensus = physical record formation** — 8+ approaches, the most multi-disciplinary convergence in the sprint.
