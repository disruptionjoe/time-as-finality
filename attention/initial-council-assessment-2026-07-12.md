completion, transition-system completion, law-sector completion, and provenance.
For the model to hang together, Time-as-Finality needs a theorem or generator

or capability is chosen.

What to do next: Run the T531 ordering-fraction measure-law contract first. It
is cheap, local, and directly targets the T528 failure axis without importing the
external Lorentzian reference law.

### Heterodox-Rigorous Professor

What to do next: Stop adding minor admission gates. Force one declared
finality-native law to face the repaired S1 suite and hostile controls, then
derive the implied C(R) menus from that law instead of declaring them.

## Convergence

### Coherence Gap

The independently recurring gap is the missing finality-native source law:
a generator, measure law, or typed dynamics that produces both record-finality
order and bounded-region capability structure without importing the target
spacetime law, declaring the operation table by hand, or letting full
completion absorb the result.

In larger-structure terms, the repo is circling a single model:

```text
local record dynamics
  -> finality-native measure or reach law
  -> bounded-region capability object C(R)
  -> finite-to-continuum order bridge
  -> observer-shadow physics-facing tests
```

Right now those arrows are represented by gates, calibrations, and absorbers,
not by one law.

### Local Minimum

The local minimum is gate accumulation without source-law generation.

The repo repeatedly builds a precise packet, routes it through hostile
absorbers, records a correct review-only verdict, then turns the remaining gap
into another admission gate. That is valuable hygiene, but it is no longer
moving the bigger model. The repeating pattern is:

```text
declared boundary or packet
  -> completion absorber fires
  -> stricter future gate
  -> no native law yet
```

This is visible in the C(R) line through T398, T401, T402, T403, T405, T406,
T445, T452-T460, T493-T494, and T529. It is visible in S1 through T464, T490,
T491, T526, T528, T530, and T531. It is visible in computational finality
through T419-T451.
