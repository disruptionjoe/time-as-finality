# B1: Black Holes As Finality-Domain Boundaries

## Claim

Black holes are stress tests for finality-domain boundaries because they limit direct classical record export from interior events to exterior observers.

## Class

Potential byproduct.

## Status

Open.

## What This Does Not Claim

- It does not claim black-hole interiors are unreal.
- It does not claim horizons are walls for infalling observers.
- It does not claim black holes are traversable transportation hubs or galaxy-to-galaxy render gates.
- It does not solve the black-hole information problem.
- It does not replace technical work on holography, complementarity, Page curves, or quantum extremal surfaces.

## Why It Might Be True

Horizons are causal-access boundaries. Interior events may be locally final for infalling systems while remaining unavailable as direct classical records to exterior observers. This makes black holes a useful stress case for observer-relative finality domains.

The credibility-preserving version is:

> Event horizons are extreme cases where causal accessibility determines what can become part of an exterior observer's settled record domain.

This is about direct classical record export. It does not deny exterior-accessible records such as accretion-disk emissions, gravitational effects, Hawking-radiation questions, or possible holographic encodings. It also does not imply that crossing a horizon is a usable route to another galaxy.

### Boundary Permeability Spectrum

Event horizons are best read as one extreme of a continuous boundary-permeability parameter rather than a category of their own. At the hard extreme, classical record export is one-way and capped (the horizon). Softer boundaries permit bidirectional record exchange with a change in record character. The heliopause is a worked soft case: interstellar cosmic rays cross inward and solar particles cross outward, and records change character as they cross. Stating soft versus hard as a measurable export asymmetry across the boundary, rather than as a binary label, keeps B1 honest. If every boundary turns out bidirectional at some scale with no privileged threshold, that is the informative result: permeability is continuous and should enter B1 as a parameter. See [Heliosphere As A Finality Domain](../explorations/heliosphere-as-finality-domain.md).

## How It Could Fail

- The language overstates what exterior observers can or cannot recover.
- It ignores holographic encoding or unitarity constraints.
- It treats causal inaccessibility as metaphysical nonexistence.
- It treats horizons as mystical render frontiers rather than causal-access boundaries.
- It implies black-hole travel, wormhole transit, or destination selection without a supported mechanism.
