# Q1D: Contextuality And No-Signalling Guardrail

## Claim

Quantum contextuality and no-signalling constrain any Time as Finality account:
local classical records may be valid in their own contexts while no single
global noncontextual assignment exists, and this must not become a hidden
variable or faster-than-light signalling claim.

## Class

Subclaim of Q1.

## Status

Guardrail only.

## Current Strongest Form

Q1D is a constraint on admissible language and models. It is not currently a
standalone prediction or novelty claim.

## Earned Content

- T21 gives finite CHSH-style contextuality witnesses: local finality sections
  can exist while global noncontextual assignment fails.
- T21's score comparison separates classical, quantum, and PR-box regimes and
  keeps no-signalling explicit.
- T64 and later detector artifacts preserve the no-signalling guardrail in
  local detector marginals.
- T170 adds a record-stage guardrail: local record-finality is constrained by
  local marginals, while joint correlation finality is a later causal-
  reconciliation record. It rejects signalling marginals, hidden-variable
  retrofits, premature correlation export, and PR-box-as-quantum readings.

## Not Earned

- No new Bell theorem.
- No new contextuality inequality.
- No local hidden-variable repair.
- No operational quantum prediction beyond existing contextuality/no-signalling
  structure.
- No detector-level account of when the local or reconciled record physically
  forms.

## Falsification Or Demotion Condition

Any Q1 use that treats later reconciled correlations as earlier local hidden
variables, permits local signalling through finality language, declares the
joint correlation final before causal comparison, or labels post-quantum
no-signalling structure as a quantum prediction fails this guardrail.

## Reinstatement Condition

Q1D becomes more than a guardrail only if a nonredundant theorem target is
named, with a result not already covered by standard contextuality,
decoherent-histories, or no-signalling frameworks.
