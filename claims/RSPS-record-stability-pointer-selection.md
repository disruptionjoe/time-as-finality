# RSPS: Record-Stability Pointer Selection

## Claim

Record-fidelity and objectivity terms can select the monitored/einselected
pointer basis in fixed-H system-environment toy models, while Born weights
remain external trace-rule data.

## Class

Quantum-boundary claim adjacent to Q1A.

## Status

Active boundary / null-model gate.

## Current Strongest Form

RSPS is useful only as a fixed-H boundary:

```text
decoherence / Quantum Darwinism / SBS own basis and objectivity;
the trace rule owns probabilities;
Time as Finality may keep only the observer-access and provenance audit.
```

T189 supports the modest claim that, across a finite robustness family,
record-fidelity scoring follows the monitored pointer axis and objectivity
degrades under ordinary noise, inaccessible fragments, and nonorthogonal
records. It does not show new measurement dynamics, collapse, Born weights,
Temporal Issuance source structure, or a Geometric Unity section selector.

## Earned Content

- T189 tests 38 fixtures spanning environment size, branch weights, imperfect
  copying, fragment access, nonorthogonal records, monitored-axis rotation,
  provenance sharing, and weakened agreement terms.
- Pointer-basis selection persists across the finite perturbations where
  records remain distinguishable.
- Objectivity degrades in the expected neighbor-owned ways: noise, missing
  access, and nonorthogonal fragment states reduce or remove record evidence.
- Raw redundancy remains unsafe when fragments share provenance roots; audited
  redundancy carries the Q1A discipline.
- The result is compatible with, and largely absorbed by, decoherence, Quantum
  Darwinism, Spectrum Broadcast Structure, and provenance-aware Q1A accounting.

## Not Earned

- No Born-rule derivation.
- No collapse mechanism.
- No replacement for standard quantum mechanics.
- No Q1A upgrade beyond access/provenance bookkeeping.
- No Temporal Issuance source-side support.
- No Geometric Unity section-selection support.
- No new objectivity criterion beyond what SBS/strong Quantum Darwinism can
  import once fragment states, access, and provenance are declared.

## Reuse Rule

Any future TI, GU, or Q1A proposal using this lane must first pass the fixed-H
null gate:

```text
hold the Hamiltonian/coupling, pointer observable, fragment states,
observer-access subset, provenance partition, and trace-rule probabilities
fixed; then show a predeclared verdict split not importable by
decoherence / Quantum Darwinism / SBS / trace-rule accounting.
```

If that package absorbs the case, RSPS earns no physics-facing credit.
