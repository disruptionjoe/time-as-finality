# RSPS: Record-Stability Pointer Selection

## Claim

In fixed-H system-environment toy models, record-fidelity/objectivity terms can
select the monitored or einselected pointer basis across finite perturbations,
but they do not derive Born weights and do not supply source-side Temporal
Issuance or GU section-selection evidence.

## Class

Quantum boundary / null model.

## Status

Active boundary.

## Current Strongest Form

RSPS is a modest fixed-H null model:

```text
decoherence / Quantum Darwinism / SBS can own basis and objectivity;
the trace rule owns probabilities;
TaF only keeps the observer-access and provenance audit discipline.
```

T189 supports this boundary in a finite sweep. Record-fidelity scoring tracks
the monitored pointer axis across branch weights, environment sizes, copying
noise, observer access cuts, nonorthogonal record states, monitored-axis
rotation, provenance collapse, and weakened agreement terms. It never imports
Born weights.

## Earned Content

- T189 runs 38 finite fixtures.
- The sweep supports pointer-basis selection under fixed monitored-axis data.
- Record objectivity degrades under noise, inaccessible fragments, and
  nonorthogonal record states.
- Raw redundancy remains unsafe when provenance roots are shared.
- The result is explicitly compatible with the Q1A/SBS absorber: provenance
  auditing matters, but redundant classical records and basis selection are
  already neighboring-theory territory.

## Not Earned

- No Born-rule derivation.
- No collapse model.
- No new quantum measurement dynamics.
- No Q1A external-distinction upgrade.
- No Temporal Issuance source object.
- No GU section selector.
- No evidence that record-fidelity terms replace the trace rule.

## Falsification Or Demotion Condition

RSPS should be demoted or rewritten if the sweep's basis-selection result
depends on a hidden hard-coded pointer basis, if the score starts importing
Born weights, or if standard decoherence, Quantum Darwinism, and SBS no longer
absorb the fixed-H objectivity content.

## Reuse Rule

Any future TI, GU, or Q1A proposal that uses record-fidelity/objectivity
language must first pass this fixed-H null gate:

```text
Can ordinary decoherence / Quantum Darwinism / SBS explain the basis and
objectivity part while the trace rule supplies probabilities?
```

If yes, no source-side issuance, GU, or new Q1A physics credit is earned.
