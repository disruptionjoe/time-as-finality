**Date:** 2026-07-07. **Role:** judge phase of an adversarial explanation-competition.
**Question:** is "a FINITE OBSERVER doing real coarse-graining" the best (hardest-to-vary) explanation of the

**Guards (load-bearing).** This is METHOD, not evidence. No claim moves in any repo. From-memory physics is

The three theory-neutral fossils every account is scored against:

- **FOSSIL-1 (the arrow):** past fixed, records accumulate monotonically, thermodynamic and psychological/record

- **FOSSIL-2 (intersubjective classicality):** different observers agree on macroscopic facts; a shared objective
  past.
- **FOSSIL-3 (quantum -> classical):** definite classical outcomes emerge from quantum superposition.

## 1. The ranking (hardest-to-vary first)

Ranked by the composite Deutsch criterion: fewest *genuinely re-settable* dials + most neutral fossils
explained + **breaks rather than bends** under the flex test, weighted toward FOSSIL-1 because that is the
phenomenon the competition is actually about.

### The field-wide result that drives everything: all four flex on FOSSIL-1

The single most important finding, and the one that must be applied symmetrically: **coarse-graining,
equivalence-closure, eternalist 4D structure, and collapse dynamics are each time-symmetric or direction-neutral
by themselves.** To get a *one-way* arrow, every one of the four accounts re-imports a low-entropy boundary
condition (the Past Hypothesis) or an equivalent gradient. None of the four natively forces the *direction* of
the arrow. So on the deepest question, the field is tied and everyone spends the same one dial. What separates
the accounts is (a) whether they carry a genuine *break-test* somewhere on the arrow's sub-structure, and (b)
how they fare on FOSSIL-2 and FOSSIL-3.

## 2. Verdict: winner, and the honest boundaries of the win

**Winner of the ranking: the mainstream statistical-mechanics package (Past Hypothesis + decoherence /
quantum-Darwinism).** It explains all three neutral fossils, carries the only on-target break-test (the Landauer
record-thermo weld), and derives FOSSIL-2 from locality rather than positing it. Two honest caveats keep this
from being a clean, unqualified trophy:

1. **The top is underdetermined between Rank 1 and Rank 2.** They are within one dial (6 vs 7) with genuinely
   *different* unexplained fossils: Rank 1 cannot natively deliver the single definite outcome (FOSSIL-3
   definiteness); Rank 2 cannot natively deliver the Second Law / record-thermo alignment (FOSSIL-1 alignment).
   Rank 2 (objective collapse) is best read as a *specialization* of Rank 1 that completes its FOSSIL-3 residual
   at the price of an uncashed empirical bet and the most dials in the field. So: Rank 1 wins the ranking, but
   its FOSSIL-3 completion — Everett-branch, collapse, or left-open — is underdetermined among interpretations.

2. **The 3/4 boundary (home team vs block universe) is underdetermined.** Different unexplained fossils, within
   a dial. Neither is a contender for the win.

So the field is not a flat "underdetermined": there is a clear *top tier* (mainstream stat-mech + decoherence,
possibly completed by collapse) that out-explains the *bottom tier* (finite-observer, block universe). The
residual underdetermination is *within* each tier, not across them.

## 5. Mandatory self-serving audit

Was the hard-to-vary criterion applied evenly across all four, or bent to favor the home team? I found and
corrected the following:

**Asymmetry A — the "fewest dials => should win" trap (guarded, correctly).** The home team has the *fewest*
dials (5 vs 6/6/7). Applied naively, dial-count alone would hand it the win. Correction applied: the criterion is
fewest *genuinely-free, load-bearing* dials + fossil-coverage + break-not-bend, not raw count. The home team's
dials are the softest (admitted-underdetermined split; arrow-direction inherited from the rival's PH *plus* its
own), and its fossil-coverage is the most derivative. This correction cuts *against* the home team and is the
right call — raw dial-count is not the metric.

**Asymmetry B — the coarse-graining identity could be bent either way; I bent it neither way.** The pivotal fact
that the mainstream's macrostate-partition dial (DIAL-3) *is* the home team's observer/system gauge could be
exploited two ways: pro-home ("thermo is just us relabeled, so we can't lose") or anti-home ("we add dials on a
shared substrate"). Even-handed resolution: the shared substrate's observer-vs-system reading is genuinely
*underdetermined* — neither side may claim it — so the overlap is a wash; the home team's *distinctive* additions
(capability wall, C(R)) are what add free dials without new fossil-coverage. The home team loses on its extras,
not on the shared core. Named, not exploited.

**Asymmetry C — block universe was under-credited by a flat "easy-to-vary" label; corrected UP (against the home
team).** The audit rated block universe flatly "easy-to-vary." That undersells it: block owns the single
hardest-to-vary *commitment* in the field (the Lorentz-forced eternalist core) and is plausibly the field's
hardest-to-vary account *on FOSSIL-2*. Its true profile is *uneven* — hard-to-vary on ontology and FOSSIL-2,
limp on FOSSIL-1 — not flatly soft. Correcting this makes a *rival* stronger and tightens the 3/4 boundary to
"underdetermined," which is the anti-self-serving direction.

**Asymmetry D — the home team was held to a HARDER standard than block on the arrow, and that is correct, not a
bias to reverse.** Both home team and block flex on FOSSIL-1's direction. But the home team *claims* to derive
the arrow (monotone Knuth-Bendix closure), whereas block *honestly admits* it explains the arrow away. Holding
the home team's flex more damaging — because it contradicts its own thesis — is the correct asymmetry given "home
team on trial," not a bias to correct.

**Asymmetry E — single-process bias (structural, partially mitigated).** The entire competition is run by the
home team's own process, on home-team-authored docs, with home-team-authored steelmen; even the rival steelmen
were generated by the home process. This is an irreducible pro-home structural bias. Mitigations applied: (i) the
surplus (home-team's own construction) was scored as non-genuine and excluded from rival scoring; (ii) rivals
were built to their strongest independent forms and their *own* hard cores (Landauer weld, eternalist core,
collapse forcing) were credited even where they beat the home team; (iii) the verdict lands on "home team loses,"
which is the outcome the bias works against — a weak signal that the mitigation held. Residual bias cannot be
fully removed inside a single process and is flagged, not claimed-resolved.

**Net audit finding:** the primary failure mode (rigging the criterion to favor the home team) did not
materialize in the verdict — the home team loses. The corrections I made (B, C) moved credit *toward* rivals and
*away* from the home team, which is the correct direction for a home-team-on-trial competition. The one place the
home team is genuinely credited (cross-regime breadth) is real but was confined to the motivation layer and
explicitly denied fossil-scoring weight.
