## What the swing actually found

Two clean, honest results — one positive, one bounding.

**1. The wound is structural, not a physics artifact.** The T401 joint-record
completion kill reproduces *exactly* in a classical game: Pair 1's Shapley
separator dies to a single **proper-subset** coalition query `{0,3}` — the
game-theoretic twin of T411's single retained-Z measurement. Two utterly
different model classes (thermal quantum Darwinism; finite cooperative games)
exhibit the *same* kill by the *same* move. That materially strengthens the
persona pass's M1 ("finite closed ⇒ boundary is declarable/absorbable") from
"six personas expect it" toward "it recurs across model classes" — the scope
no-go theorem now has a second witness.

**2. The efficiency axiom constructs the R1 object the pass predicted — and
locates the entire remaining question in R2.** Pair 2 (efficiency-only) is two
games agreeing on **every** proper coalition, differing in Shapley value, with the
datum living only in the whole. Proper-subset joint-record completion cannot
absorb it. This is a *clean classical R1 object*, and it is more than T411
managed — T411's separator was itself proper-subset-localizable and got absorbed.
The catch is exactly the deliverable's R1/R2 split: `v(N)` is **declared**, so R2
is unpaid; the honesty leg fires. The homology thus **isolates the whole physical
question to one place**: is the value of the whole (`v(N)` / the constitution /
the total legitimate claim) *forced* or *declared*? — which is the program's
already-named central gap (why are agents bounded; the unbuilt Tier-2 card).

**The sharp boundary (Leg 8): the surviving quantum residue does not port.** A
no-marginal separator — information in *no* coalition, the genuine T411 β=0
correlation — cannot exist in a finite classical game, because the Shapley value
is *linear* in the characteristic function. So the classical/Shapley route and
the quantum/global-correlation route are **two different R1 constructions**: the
game gives a whole-coalition (efficiency) separator; the quantum fixture gives a
no-marginal (entanglement) separator. The homology is real at the level of the
*wound and the escape-hatch structure*, and it explicitly does **not** claim the
two surviving objects are the same object.

## Answering Joe's Nash/Shapley intuition directly

The "something between Nash at finite scale and Shapley at the infinite layers"
is, under this lens, the **ratification/aggregation gradient** — and it has three
named pieces:

- **Nash, finite scale = the declared layer.** Local best-response fixed points;
  multiple; coordinate-dependent; the imputation polytope (T412: dimension 4).
  This is the artifact/implementation layer — many legitimate-looking splits, no
  forced one.
- **Shapley = the axiom-forced legitimate value.** Unique because symmetry
  (automorphism-invariance), efficiency (the whole in every part), null-player,
  and additivity pin it. This is the "physical rather than declared" object — and
  T412 shows the localizing move that would declare it away is precisely a
  **symmetry violation**.
- **Aumann–Shapley, non-atomic = legitimacy asserted at the infinite layers.**
  In the continuum-of-players limit, Aumann's equivalence theorem (core =
  competitive equilibria = value) makes the legitimate allocation *unconditional*
  — the finite multiplicity collapses. This is the **asymptotic-gap escape hatch**
  the pass named (M1: a non-declarable boundary needs infinite structure). It is
  the same escape the transport rung was reaching for via a different door.

So Nash → Shapley → Aumann–Shapley is a concrete instance of the pass's
"declared → axiom-forced → physically-forced" ladder, and it lives in
established mathematics.
