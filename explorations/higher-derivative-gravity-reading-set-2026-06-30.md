## The reading set

1. **Turok et al.**, *The free boundary problem in general relativity*, arXiv:2606.18128 (2026) -- initial
   conditions derived from boundary stationarity. (Covered in the companion file.)
2. **Stelle**, *Renormalization of higher derivative quantum gravity*, Phys. Rev. D 16, 953 (1977) --
   curvature-squared gravity is renormalizable but ghost-cursed (non-unitary).
3. **Avramidi & Barvinsky**, *Asymptotic freedom in higher-derivative quantum gravity*, Phys. Lett. B 159,
   269 (1985) -- the same theory is asymptotically free; possible unitarity restoration via radiative
   corrections + Euclidean positivity.

Papers 2 and 3 form a matched higher-derivative-gravity pair.

## The cumulative arc (the real finding)

Three papers in, the fit is **uneven by design, and that is itself the signal**:

- **Turok was strong-for-TaF** (boundary-conditions-derived, finality-as-filter, the H1 gluing reframe).
- **Stelle and Avramidi-Barvinsky are strong-for-GU** and only medium/thin for TaF.

For GU the pair converges on one precise clarity: standard quadratic gravity **already** wins
renormalizability (Stelle) and asymptotic freedom (Avramidi-Barvinsky) **without GU's machinery**, so GU's
entire differentiating case rests on the residual -- the ghost/unitarity problem and the derivation of matter
content. That GU-facing obligation is recorded in the GU repo at
`gu-formalization/explorations/misc/external-quadratic-gravity-obligations-2026-06-30.md`.

a claim or adds a test.
