# Meta-Layer Analysis: Time as Finality × GU Formalization

## Status

Exploration, 2026-06-11. This sits one level above the existing claim-level crosswalk (`gu-formalization/explorations/time-as-finality-crosswalk/`, June 1): not "which TaF claims map to which GU claims," but *what shared structure the two research programs have, what the connecting meta-layer object actually is, and where the connection must be refused.* Cross-repo material stays in explorations on both sides; nothing here is canon for either repo.

## The Two Programs, One Sentence Each

- **GU Formalization:** a research map testing whether richer substrate-level structure can have smooth 4D observer-facing *shadows* that no-go theorems classify as impossible only within ordinary reduction classes. A *projection/ontology* question: what could the deeper structure be?
- **Time as Finality:** a formalization project testing whether observer-facing temporal order and classical objectivity can be reconstructed as the *stabilization of records* across causally bounded systems. An *access/epistemology* question: how does any observer-facing world become settled?

## Meta-Layer 1: The Method (strongest connection, fully load-bearing)

The two repos are the same epistemic machine instantiated twice: claim classes with stable IDs, guardrails as first-class artifacts, no-go/known-physics discipline, persona dialectics with synthesis protocols, canon/exploration separation with gated promotion, and falsification-explicitly-counted-as-success. GU's CANON.md posture ("clean falsification, narrowing, or demotion of a path is a successful contribution") and TaF's typed closure battery are the same philosophy at different maturity stages.

This means the most valuable shared asset is *neither repo's physics*: it is the domain-agnostic research method — which is exactly what the Stage-0 Research Advancement System packet (TaF `governance/`) describes. **TaF and GU are the two existing proving grounds of that system.** Any general extraction of the method should treat them as instance A and instance B, and differences in their conventions (GU's YAML status headers and six-axis specs; TaF's claim ledger and typed closure battery) are a ready-made comparison set for what the general system should standardize.

Practical consequence: method artifacts should flow freely between repos (lens libraries already do); *physics claims should not* (see Refusals).

## Meta-Layer 2: The Conceptual Connector — The Readout Protocol

Both programs deny that the experienced 4D world is fundamental, but they derive it from opposite ends:

```text
GU:   richer substrate --(projection)--> smooth 4D shadow      [who observes the shadow? unspecified]
TaF:  records --(stabilization)--> observer-relative finality  [records of what? unspecified]
```

Each program's unspecified slot is the other program's subject. GU needs an observer/readout story (a shadow with no account of access is just mathematics); TaF needs a substrate story only if it ever goes beyond bracketing it (its records are records *of* something). The June-1 crosswalk already found the connecting chain — `source substrate → observer protocol → record-bearing network → finality domain` — and the correct discipline: **projection and finality are not the same thing.** A mathematical shadow exists without delay; an observer-facing world must be recorded, stabilized, and accessed through a causal protocol.

The meta-layer object, made concrete, is a **dictionary between GU's six-axis specification protocol and TaF's primitives**:

| GU six-axis spec | TaF primitive/object | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| substrate | (deliberately bracketed) source layer | TaF's rendered-interface open problem demands exactly this slot be specified before deeper-layer claims |
| observer | D2 record-bearing system | TaF's most developed axis; GU's least |
| **pairing** | **coupling / mattering structure (M1)** | The convergence point — see below |

Two readings of this table:

1. **M1 lands on GU's pairing axis.** "What matters to the record holder" = the coupling/pairing structure = GU's axis 3. The mattering-first intuition (BACKLOG M1) is not TaF-internal speculation; it is the shared axis both programs independently require and neither has formalized. If M1-weak's T12 experiment works (different coupling profiles → different reconstructed temporal orders from one graph), it is simultaneously a TaF result and a worked toy example of GU's pairing axis.
2. **TaF supplies GU's certification layer.** The crosswalk's lattice-QFT persona had the key line: finality concerns *observer certification of an invariant, not the invariant's existence*. GU's invariants (index, anomaly) are mathematical facts; what a bounded observer can certify about them through which record channels is a TaF question — channel-indexed finality (WC-29) is the certification calculus GU's observer axis would need. The planned `signed-readout-record-graph-test` in GU explorations now has a real substrate: TaF's running T1 model exists as of t11.

## Meta-Layer 3: The Shared Anti-Pattern Both Repos Guard Against

Both programs are one rhetorical step from the same cliff: **using the derived-world move as an escape hatch.** GU's version: "the no-go theorem only applies to the shadow, the substrate is richer" — refused by canon claim 1 (no-go discipline: exact assumptions, known class exits). TaF's version: "the contradiction is only in the rendered interface, the deeper layer differs" — refused by the rendered-interface mechanism challenge. The shared dual: *the burden is always a specified map* (which assumptions the projection/protocol preserves, weakens, loses). That both repos independently built this guardrail is evidence the method layer is real.

## Refusals (where the connection must not go)

1. **No status contamination.** GU is reputationally radioactive in mainstream physics independent of its content. Linkage must be one-way: GU may cite TaF method and protocols; TaF public-facing surfaces must not depend on or cite GU claims (this was audit WC-7 and it stands — this file lives in explorations for that reason).
2. **No mutual credibility manufacture.** Two unproven programs citing each other create the appearance of support without evidence. Cross-references must always be method-level or explicitly labeled exploration.
3. **No escape-hatch trades.** TaF finality must never be used to discharge a GU proof obligation ("the anomaly is just not finalized for our observer network" is forbidden), and GU substrate ontology must never be imported as evidence for TaF claims ("records are records of the 14D substrate" is forbidden without the map).
4. **Projection ≠ finality**, permanently. The crosswalk's incompatibility finding is the load-bearing distinction; any document that blurs it should fail review.

## External Calibration Anchor: Disciplined Selection Filter (one-way, added 2026-06-30)

A reading set of external GR/QG papers (Tzanavaris-Boyle-Turok free-boundary 2026; Stelle 1977; Avramidi-Barvinsky 1985) was assessed against both repos via a council pass. The free-boundary paper is a fully-rigorous, mainstream instance of a **disciplined selection filter**: a variational condition that names exactly what it excludes (Kasner/BKL) and what it admits (conformally-regular FLRW), with an on-shell finiteness check. It is a useful external **benchmark** for what a selection filter must look like to count as rigorous — calibrating both GU's no-go exits and TaF's superselection/finality-filter claims (T10, T29).

**Refusal, load-bearing here:** this anchor attaches to GU and to TaF **independently and one-way**. It must never be routed GU -> paper -> TaF (that would triangulate three things into mutual support with zero evidence). The paper's filter is GR-internal and projection-level; mapping it to TaF finality or GU substrate is structure-mapping only, never ontology-merge. Projection ≠ finality, permanently. GU-facing obligations from this reading set live in the GU repo (`explorations/misc/external-quadratic-gravity-obligations-2026-06-30.md`); the TaF-facing notes live in `explorations/higher-derivative-gravity-reading-set-2026-06-30.md` and the two new open problems.
