# Cluster B — Physics & Geometry (Reverse Pass)

The deepest assembly candidates live here. Cross-cutting thesis the cluster is
asked to test: **finality, the physical boundary, "which inner product is
physical," and the arrow of time are one and the same object — a
choice-of-admissibility — and the E1/E2/E3 taxonomy types that choice by *which
resource fixes the cut*.** Each persona writes cold.

## 1. Mathematical Physicist

**Meta-synthesis.** The pile is one theorem wearing four hats. Fix a global
algebra A and a distinguished cut. There is always a *co-present separating
datum* (Declarability Lemma); making it "physical" means selecting an
admissibility structure that suppresses recovery of that datum. Three and only
three structures can do the suppression: a **limit** (state approached but never
attained → E1), a **complexity gate** (datum present but not computable in the
allotted resource → E2), a **superselection / conserved charge** (datum lives in
a sector the observable algebra cannot reach → E3). "Arrow of time" is then not a
fourth thing: it is the *statement that the admissibility structure is not
invertible*. So the missing meta-claim is: **the arrow of time inherits its type
from whichever resource is doing the suppressing** — thermodynamic arrow = E1,
one-way-function/eidostate arrow = E2, superselection arrow = E3. We have been
looking for "the" arrow; there are three, distinguished operationally.

**Hegelian.** Antithesis: this is just relabeling three known irreversibilities
(Boltzmann, Landauer-with-a-hardness-twist, superselection) with a common noun.
The honest negatives bite — E2 came back REDESIGN/pre-empted, E3 came back
disanalogy. If each hat is separately known and the union doesn't type-check
(T421), the "one theorem" is a category error. Synthesis: what survives is *not*
the unification but the **typing discipline**: the claim that these three are
mutually exclusive resource-signatures and that a given irreversibility has
exactly one type is itself a falsifiable, non-trivial statement even if no single
object unifies them.

**Steelman.** Concrete structural claim: define the *suppression functional*
S(A, cut) = the minimal resource (energy / circuit-depth / conserved charge)
whose expenditure inverts the cut. Claim: S is well-defined and its *type* (which
of the three resources is finite-and-minimal) is a **relabeling invariant** of
the arrow. The one move that makes it real: exhibit a single physical system
where the arrow's type *changes* (E1→E2) under a controlled parameter, proving
the types are physical regimes, not naming conventions.

## 3. Differential Geometer

**Meta-synthesis.** Admissibility = choice of metric on the section bundle. The
Declarability Lemma says the total space always carries the datum; "physical vs
declared" is which **reduction of structure group** you impose on the fibers. E1
is a *degeneration* of the metric (a limit where a direction's norm → 0/∞), E2 is
a metric that is smooth but *geodesically intractable* (present, non-navigable in
budget), E3 is a metric of *fixed indefinite signature* (a genuine Krein/pseudo-
Riemannian split that no admissible frame removes). The larger story we're
missing: **finality is a connection, not a scalar.** The arrow of time is the
*holonomy* of the admissibility connection around a closed observer loop — zero
holonomy = reversible = no arrow; nonzero = committed record.

**Hegelian.** Antithesis: calling irreversibility "holonomy" is exactly the
metaphor-overreach the poet persona warns of unless there's a real curvature
2-form. Where is it? Synthesis: the geometric content is *only* real if the E3
disanalogy (T421) is read correctly — operational-forced (limit degeneration,
E1-like) and metric-forced (fixed indefinite signature) are **different tensors**:
one is a degenerating positive metric, the other a non-degenerate indefinite one.
That distinction is genuine differential geometry, not rhyme. So holonomy survives
as a *program*, not a result.

**Steelman.** Claim: there is an admissibility connection ∇ on the bundle of
observer-accessible sections whose curvature F vanishes iff the cut is E0
(declared). E1 = F concentrated at a boundary limit (degenerate metric locus);
E3-metric = F valued in the indefinite grading. The one move: compute F for the
pseudo-Hermitian toy model and show its signature-type is the E-type — as a
worked geometric object, kept strictly as object-of-study.

## 4. Topologist / Sheaf Theorist

**Meta-synthesis.** The T421 disanalogy is the headline, and it is telling us
there are **two distinct cohomologies**, not one. (a) *Operational cohomology*:
the H^1 gluing obstruction of the global-correlation separator — a datum in the
whole, in no proper subset (item 10), whose nonvanishing = "no local witness for
finality/legitimacy." (b) *Metric cohomology*: the obstruction to globally
trivializing the indefinite grading (a Krein/superselection class). These pull
*opposite ways* on irreducibility precisely because they are classes in different
complexes with different coboundaries. The story we're missing: **legitimacy and
finality are H^1 sections of the operational sheaf; the E3 "symmetry no-go" is an
H^1 of the metric sheaf; conflating them is why the cross-repo adapter did not
type-check.** The novelty is not a unification — it is the *proof that no
unification exists because the two cohomologies are genuinely different functors*.

**Hegelian.** Antithesis: "two cohomologies" could be an ornate way of saying
"these two things are different," which we already knew from T421. Does the sheaf
language earn its keep? Synthesis: it earns it iff you can name the two *sites*
(the categories of opens) and show a section that is a coboundary in one and a
nontrivial class in the other — that is a real structural discriminator, and it
directly explains item 12's "cross-repo unification does not type-check" as a
*theorem about mismatched sites*, not a failure of effort.

**Steelman.** Claim: TaF-finality is a nonzero class in H^1(operational site) with
*no* image in the metric site; GU's generation-count E3 is a class in H^1(metric
site) with no image in the operational site. The map between sites is not a
morphism of sites (no continuous functor), which is *exactly* T421. The one move:
write the two Grothendieck sites down explicitly and prove the comparison functor
fails to exist. That converts "disanalogy" from a negative result into a positive
no-go theorem.

## 7. Quantum Foundations Researcher

**Meta-synthesis.** Yes — the whole program is secretly **one claim about the
Heisenberg cut.** "Which inner product is physical" (pseudo-Hermitian metric
operator), "where the measurement/observer boundary sits," "what counts as an
irreversible record (finality)," and "the direction of time" are *the same
choice*: the selection of an admissible observable algebra and its associated
positive functional. Decoherence/Quantum Darwinism already says the arrow and the
record are the *redundant, un-recoverable* part of the state relative to an
observer's accessible algebra. The missing meta-synthesis: **finality = the cut is
declared un-invertible relative to a resource**, and the *type of resource that
fixes the cut is the type of the arrow.** Einselection is E1-flavored (energetic/
thermodynamic branching); a hardness-of-unscrambling arrow is E2; a
superselection-sector arrow is E3. One observer-cut, three ways to make it stick.

**Hegelian.** Antithesis: the measurement cut is famously *movable* (von Neumann);
if it's a free choice, calling finality "the cut" makes finality subjective and
non-physical — the opposite of an arrow of time. And Quantum Darwinism already
supplies the objectivity story without any E-typing. Synthesis: the cut is
movable, but *the resource required to move it back is not free* — that expenditure
is the physical content. So finality is not "where you draw the cut" but "the
minimal resource to un-draw it," which is exactly the suppression functional. The
E-typing is what upgrades a subjective cut to an objective, typed arrow.

**Steelman.** Claim: for an observer algebra O ⊂ A, define finality of a record r
as the resource-cost of the smallest CP map returning r's pre-image inside O.
Arrow-of-time type = argmin-resource-type of that cost. The one move: show, in a
decoherence model, that the einselection basis is fixed by an E1 (energetic) cost
and that swapping to an engineered E2 (scrambling) cost yields a *different,
demonstrable* record-objectivity with the same formalism. Same cut, retyped arrow.

## 8. Quantum Information Theorist

**Meta-synthesis.** Recast everything as a **resource theory of the cut.** Free
operations = admissible channels (those respecting the observer algebra); the
monotone = irreducible finality. The Declarability Lemma is the statement that in
a *closed finite* system every channel is unitarily dilatable → nothing is truly
free → boundaries are declared. Forcing = restricting the free set by a resource:
energy (thermo-majorization, E1), computational hardness (a *complexity-bounded*
resource theory where the free set is poly-size circuits, E2), or symmetry
(resource theory of asymmetry / WAY theorem, E3). The larger story: **the arrow of
time is the monotone of whichever resource theory is operative, and the three
resource theories are the three E-types.** What we're missing is that these are not
one resource theory but a *lattice* of them, and finality is only well-defined
relative to a chosen point in the lattice.

**Hegelian.** Antithesis: complexity-bounded resource theories are notoriously
ill-behaved (the free set isn't closed under composition cleanly); and thermo-
majorization + asymmetry are already textbook. So E2 may not even *be* a proper
resource theory — which is exactly why the computational arrow came back REDESIGN.
Synthesis: the honest asymmetry among the three (E1, E3 are clean monotone
theories; E2 is a fragile pseudo-theory) is itself the finding — it predicts that
an **E2 arrow of time would be structurally different in kind**: unstable under
composition, observer-relative, non-monotone. That is a sharp, novel prediction,
not a rhyme.

**Steelman.** Claim: E1 and E3 arrows admit a strict monotone (entropy;
asymmetry measure); an E2 arrow admits at most a *computational* monotone that is
non-additive and collapses under an oracle. The one move: construct the WAY/
asymmetry monotone (E3) and the thermo-majorization monotone (E1) for the *same*
toy record and show they *disagree* on ordering — proving the arrow's type is
observable as a disagreement between two monotones, and E2's absence of a clean
monotone is its signature.

## 25. Philosopher of Physics

**Meta-synthesis.** The deep move is ontological deflation done *carefully*: the
program is discovering that a cluster of supposedly-absolute distinctions
(physical/declared, forced/free, real record/mere bookkeeping, past/future) are
**one relational distinction indexed by a resource**. This is the Declarability
Lemma promoted to metaphysics: *there is no view-from-nowhere boundary; every
boundary is either co-present (declared) or made to stick by a finite resource.*
The genuinely novel meta-synthesis is not "everything is relative" (that's cheap)
but the **typed** version: the boundary's *modality* (limit vs hardness vs
symmetry) is an objective, discoverable fact even though the boundary's location
is not. So the arrow of time is *modally* objective (it has an E-type) while being
*locationally* observer-relative. That threads the realism/anti-realism needle.

**Hegelian.** Antithesis: this risks being unfalsifiable — "some resource always
fixes the cut" can absorb any evidence. And the honest negatives show single-mode
novelty keeps getting pre-empted, suggesting the "profound reframe" is post-hoc.
Synthesis: falsifiability is recovered precisely by the **exclusivity claim** — an
arrow has *exactly one* type, and types make *different* empirical predictions
(monotone disagreement, oracle-collapse, superselection stability). If two types'
predictions were always identical, the reframe would be empty; the cluster's job
is to show they aren't.

**Steelman.** Claim: "arrow of time" refers rigidly to *the resource-type that
makes the local cut un-invertible*, and this type is an interpretation-independent
invariant (survives changing QM interpretation), whereas the cut's location is
interpretation-dependent. The one move: exhibit two interpretations (say Everett
vs relational) that agree on the E-type of a given arrow while disagreeing on cut
location — demonstrating type-objectivity is real, not verbal.

## 45. Fiber Bundle Specialist

**Meta-synthesis.** Model the observer as a *section* of a bundle whose fibers are
the admissible-menu classes. Declared boundary = the section is globally defined
(a trivialization exists); forced boundary = an **obstruction to the section
crossing a fiber**. E1 = the section runs off to a boundary/limit fiber
(non-compactness); E2 = the section exists but is not *constructible* by an
admissible (bounded) frame; E3 = a topological obstruction (nontrivial bundle, no
global section). Missing story: the tri-repo "one object, three shadows" is
literally **one bundle projected onto three base spaces**; the "missing thing at
the boundary" is the *obstruction class to a global section*, and it necessarily
lives at the boundary because that's where transition functions are glued.

**Hegelian.** Antithesis: bundles are so expressive they can be draped over
anything — this may be decorative. Synthesis: the non-decorative content is the
*distinction* between "no global section because non-compact" (E1) and "no global
section because nontrivial characteristic class" (E3). That is the fiber-bundle
face of the T421 disanalogy, and it is a real, computable difference (a limit vs a
Chern/Stiefel-Whitney-type class).

**Steelman.** Claim: TaF-finality = obstruction to a global admissible section,
and its E-type = *which kind* of obstruction (non-compactness vs characteristic
class vs non-constructibility). The one move: for the operational sheaf, compute
whether the obstruction is a limit-type or a class-type — settling whether TaF's
arrow is E1 or E3 *by geometry* rather than by analogy to GU.

## 46. Spin Geometry Expert

**Meta-synthesis (honoring the one-way rule — GU is adjacency, object of study).**
The physics face of "declared vs forced" is a **grading**: a Clifford/ghost-parity
Z_2 that splits the space into positive- and negative-norm sectors. "Embrace
ghosts, don't exclude them" (Turok-Bateman generalized Born rule) is the physical
picture of *declared* admissibility: the negative-norm datum is co-present in the
graded algebra, not thrown away — you *choose* which grading to call physical. So
the Declarability Lemma has a spinorial avatar: **the separating datum is the
ghost sector; a boundary is "forced" only when a Dirac-operator index or a fixed
Krein signature prevents you from gauging the ghost sector away.** What we're
missing: E3-metric-forcing is *exactly* a fixed spinorial grading (can't be
removed), while E1/E2 leave the grading trivial — giving spin geometry as the
natural home for the T421 metric-vs-operational split.

**Hegelian.** Antithesis: this is precisely where GU-contamination is most
tempting — importing ghost-parity as *evidence* for a TaF finality claim. The
one-way rule forbids it. Synthesis: kept clean, the spinorial picture is only an
*illustration* of the admissibility grading, not support; its legitimate yield is
a vocabulary (grading = declared choice; fixed index = forced) plus the honest
reminder that GU's generation-count E3 is a *metric* class and thus, per T421, not
transportable to TaF.

**Steelman.** Claim (object-of-study only): metric-forcing corresponds to a
nonzero Dirac index / fixed Krein signature that no admissible unitary removes;
operational-forcing does not. The one move that would be *illegitimate* here —
and must be refused — is using this to argue TaF's arrow is E3. The legitimate
move: use the grading only to sharpen the *definition* of metric-forcing for the
topologist's two-cohomology claim.

## 48. Gauge Theory Researcher

**Meta-synthesis.** Legitimacy/finality is a **gauge-theoretic global object**,
and the right trio is holonomy / anomaly inflow / edge modes. Local gauge = free
relabelings (declared boundaries, the Aumann-Shapley "no local representative");
the physical content is gauge-*invariant* and global: **holonomy** (finality =
nonzero Wilson loop of the admissibility connection), **edge modes** (the boundary
datum that must live on the cut to restore gauge invariance — literally "the
missing thing lives at the boundary," item 6), and **anomaly inflow** (a bulk
symmetry that cannot be realized on the boundary alone — the E3 no-go). Missing
story: **the E3 disanalogy is an anomaly-inflow statement** — the operational
current and the metric current have *different* anomaly coefficients, so a boundary
that cancels one leaves the other, which is why the two "E3"s pull opposite ways.

**Hegelian.** Antithesis: anomaly inflow requires an actual anomaly polynomial;
without one this is analogy. And gauge-invariance-as-legitimacy is suggestive but
the "gauge group" of the governance/observer setting is undefined. Synthesis: the
survivable core is the **edge-mode = boundary-datum** identification, which is
concrete (edge modes are forced by gauge invariance, exactly as the separating
datum is forced to the boundary) and directly realizes item 10's H^1 gluing
obstruction as an edge-mode counting.

**Steelman.** Claim: finality is the holonomy of the admissibility connection;
its E-type is the *mechanism* protecting the holonomy (mass gap/limit = E1,
computational inaccessibility = E2, anomaly = E3). The two-cohomology claim = two
anomaly coefficients (operational vs metric) that need not co-cancel. The one move:
write the two currents and show their anomaly coefficients differ — turning T421
into a computed anomaly mismatch, not a verbal disanalogy.

## 54. Experimentalist

**Meta-synthesis.** All of the above is idle unless there is **one observable that
distinguishes an E1 from an E2 from an E3 arrow of time.** Candidate: prepare the
*same* initial record and measure the **scaling of the resource needed to reverse
it** as you dial a control knob (system size N, or available compute, or a symmetry-
breaking field). Signatures: **E1** — recovery cost scales with a
thermodynamic/limit quantity and is *continuous* (recovery gets hard smoothly as
N→∞, finite at finite N; Loschmidt-echo-style). **E2** — recovery cost jumps
*super-polynomially* in a security parameter while the state is finite and
present; crucially it **collapses in the presence of an oracle / extra compute**
(the arrow is *computational*, not physical). **E3** — recovery is *strictly
impossible at every finite N* because a conserved charge/superselection forbids it;
no amount of energy or compute helps. So: **oracle-sensitivity separates E2;
finite-N impossibility separates E3; smooth limit-scaling separates E1.**

**Hegelian.** Antithesis: "resource to reverse" is hard to make model-independent;
Loschmidt echoes conflate E1 and E2 in practice, and true superselection is never
perfectly realized. So the three signatures may not be cleanly separable in any
real apparatus. Synthesis: the *discriminating axis* survives even if a single
clean experiment doesn't — **oracle/compute-sensitivity** is the sharpest wedge
because only E2 responds to added computation. That single knob (does more compute
change the arrow?) is a genuine, novel falsification test.

**Steelman.** Claim: the arrow's E-type is operationally defined by the *response
of reversal-cost to three independent knobs* (energy, compute, symmetry field);
the response-signature matrix is diagonal-ish (each type responds to one knob).
The one move: run the *compute knob* on a scrambling circuit — if reversal cost
drops when you add an oracle, the arrow is E2; if untouched, it's E1 or E3,
separated by finite-N impossibility. This is buildable today on a small quantum
simulator.

## Cluster Meta-Synthesis

The cluster converges on three assemblies. Ranked by real-structure-over-rhyme.

**Nomination 1 (strongest — genuinely novel, falsifiable): The typed-arrow /
suppression-functional claim.** *Backers: 1, 7, 8, 54 (with 25 supplying the
metaphysics).* Finality = the minimal resource whose expenditure inverts a
declared cut; the **arrow of time's type (E1/E2/E3) = the type of that resource**,
and the types are *mutually exclusive and empirically distinguishable*. The novel,
disciplined core is not a unification (which T421 forbids) but an **exclusivity +
observability** claim: an arrow has exactly one type, and **compute-sensitivity of
reversal cost** (54) is the wedge that isolates E2, with monotone-disagreement (8)
and finite-N impossibility separating E1/E3. *Real structure, not rhyme:* it makes
a buildable prediction (does adding an oracle change the arrow?) and predicts E2
arrows are *structurally deficient* (no clean monotone, oracle-collapsible) — a
sharp, non-obvious consequence.

**Nomination 2 (deepest — converts a negative into a theorem): Two cohomologies,
no comparison functor.** *Backers: 4, 48 (46/49 supply the metric-site vocabulary,
strictly as objects of study).* The T421 disanalogy is not a failure but a **no-go
theorem**: operational-finality is a nonzero class in H^1 of an *operational site*
with no image in the *metric site*, and GU's generation-count E3 is the mirror
image; the cross-repo adapter "does not type-check" *because the comparison functor
between the two sites provably does not exist*. Persona 48 gives it teeth as an
**anomaly-coefficient mismatch** (two currents, non-co-cancelling). *Real structure,
mostly — the load-bearing move (write the two sites / two currents explicitly and
prove no functor) is not yet done;* until then it is a rigorous conjecture, not a
theorem, but it is the most profound reframe available: it explains *why*
unification kept failing.

**Nomination 3 (unifying frame, most at risk of rhyme): Finality as holonomy of
the admissibility connection.** *Backers: 3, 45, 48.* Declared = flat/trivial;
forced = nonzero curvature/holonomy; the boundary datum = edge modes forced by
gauge invariance (concretely realizing "the missing thing lives at the boundary").
Elegant and it *names* the E-types by protection mechanism (gap/limit vs compute vs
anomaly). *Honest read: partly rhyme until the curvature 2-form is actually
exhibited* — but the **edge-mode = boundary-separator** identification (48) and the
**obstruction-to-a-global-section** picture (45) are concrete enough to be more than
metaphor and directly instantiate item 10's H^1 object.
