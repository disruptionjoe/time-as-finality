## Result

### Main Finding

The repo's strongest current branches are mostly about:

- what a declared observer can access;
- what counts as an admissible record under explicit conditions; and
- what formal structure survives bounded tests.

They are usually **not** claims about what exists simpliciter.

The main philosophical risk is not that the repo has already adopted a bad
ontology. The risk is that phrases like:

- `observer-relative`
- `finalized`
- `under-finalized`
- `hidden`
- `real`
- `loss`

can silently shift from:

```text
access-relative / protocol-relative / model-relative language
```

to:

```text
claims about what the world fundamentally is
```

without an explicit promotion step.

### Cross-Branch Guardrail

Unless a branch explicitly says otherwise, the default reading should be:

```text
observer-relative = indexed to a declared access profile
finalized         = admissible as a classical record under a declared task
hidden            = not available through the declared projection or boundary
real              = not denied by the model, but not thereby classically settled
loss              = source-target attribution or capability loss, not ontic destruction
```

That default protects the method while leaving the North Star free to stay
aggressive.

## Strongest Branch-Specific Guardrails

### 1. D1

Safe reading:

```text
D1 is an observer-indexed comparative profile over records.
```

Unsafe drift:

```text
D1 measures how real a thing is.
```

Why this matters:

- `D1` is explicitly weakened and comparative.
- Its current best support is mixed: holder redundancy has some executable
  physical traction; branch support and reversal cost remain formal-only in
  stronger physical readings.

Recommended paper-facing rule:

```text
Use D1 to compare admissible record situations, not to rank ontological status.
```

### 2. C1

Safe reading:

```text
Accessible stabilized records can reconstruct an observer-relative partial
temporal order.
```

Unsafe drift:


Why this matters:

- `C1` already states that the phenomenal bridge is open.
- The strongest current results are about partial-order reconstruction, descent,
  and canonicality conditions, not about lived succession as such.

Recommended paper-facing rule:

```text
Treat C1 as a reconstruction theorem family unless and until the phenomenal
bridge gets its own formal closure or impossibility result.
```

### 3. H7

## Compact Anti-Drift Rules

For future writing, the cleanest philosophy-of-physics guardrails are:

1. `Observer-relative` should default to an indexed access claim, not an
   ontological-relativity claim.
2. `Finalized` should default to an admissible classical-record verdict, not a
   metaphysical becoming event.
3. `Hidden` should default to projection or boundary inaccessibility, not
   nonexistence.
4. `Real but not finalized` should mean theory-real but not yet classically
   recorded for the declared observer/environment context.
5. `Loss` or `obstruction` should default to source-target formal bookkeeping
   unless a separate physical substrate theorem is named.
6. Any sentence that upgrades from bookkeeping/admissibility to ontology should
   have to say so explicitly.

## Blocker

The blocker is not missing philosophical vocabulary. The blocker is missing a
repo-wide convention that marks when a branch is speaking in one of these four
registers:

- epistemic access;
- record admissibility;
- ontology;
- physical existence.

Without that explicit register, the same sentence can slide between them during
essay writing or synthesis work.
