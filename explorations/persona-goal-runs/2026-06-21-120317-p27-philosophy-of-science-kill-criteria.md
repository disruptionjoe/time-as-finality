## Result

## Branch-Level Kill Criteria

### 1. Q1

### Current live content

`Q1` is already demoted from a single quantum conjecture to a navigation
umbrella over four child branches. The only non-null experimental route left
is `Q1B`, and even that route is externally blocked.

### Kill criterion

Kill `Q1` as an active branch if the repo reaches all of the following:

1. `Q1A` stays bookkeeping-only after future fixed-data attempts share the
   same audited access cut, provenance-aware partition, and accessible support
   count with neighboring frameworks.
2. `Q1B` fails the realism gate:
   no named lab workflow can freeze a valid `T136/T138` manifest before data
   collection with the required authority separation.
3. `Q1C` fails the platform gate:
   no concrete platform can freeze the full `R, A, H, V = g(H), L` tuple and
   survive the event-level null screens.
4. `Q1D` continues to contribute only guardrails and no nonredundant theorem
   target.

If those four conditions hold together, `Q1` should stop receiving autonomous
research time and remain only as an archived navigation label over older
quantum-facing work.

### What does not yet kill it

- Further absorption of one toy witness.
- More detector scaffolding without real rows.
- Another weak-measurement null result by itself.

Those narrow the branch, but the branch is only killed when the last live
escape routes become unrealistic in practice, not merely difficult.

## 2. H7

### Current live content

`H7` still earns a conditional constructor theorem and a strong reverse-edge /
resource-accounting audit discipline. Its physical-arrow reading is already
demoted.

### Kill criterion

Kill `H7` as an active physical research branch if the repo can justify this
strong closure statement:

```text
No realistic physical record substrate can instantiate a
physical_record_deletion reverse that remains constructor-impossible after
matching free-energy, capacity, sink/export, observer-boundary, provenance,
source-copy, reversible-control, and substrate-specific absorber data.
```

Operationally, that means:

1. serious substrate proposals can be named and frozen;
2. each one collapses under the `T145/T148/T152` plus `N8` absorber stack; and
3. no remaining candidate survives as anything more than resource accounting,
   boundary loss, provenance loss, or kinetic suppression.

At that point, `H7` should cease as a future physical-arrow route and remain
only as:

- the `T18` conditional constructor theorem; and
- the reverse-edge audit language for detecting overclaim.

### What does not yet kill it

- Showing that one more metastable or memory-protected substrate fails.
- Another argument that practical irreversibility is not enough.

Those are expected demotions inside the current gate. `H7` is killed only when
the gate itself has become exhaustively unrealistic, not when one candidate
fails it.

## 3. TF1

### Current live content

`TF1` survives only as a narrowed formal target. The strongest honest
survivor is the witness-obligation recast, not a prior-art-separated
obstruction theorem.

### Kill criterion

Kill `TF1` as an active novelty-seeking branch if the witness-obligation recast
fails on its own terms:

1. `WO(f, J)` cannot be derived canonically from the morphism, source fibers,
   and target judgment family.
2. Every tested `WO(f, J)` is definitionally the same as a neighbor object
   once provenance/effect/abstraction/lens/CSP packages receive the same data.
3. The recast gives no simplification in admissibility, hostile review, or
   comparison discipline over those neighboring accounts.

If that happens, `TF1` should stop being treated as a likely mathematical
separation target. The branch should be demoted to integration vocabulary or
notation for source-derived witness obligations and no longer framed as a
frontier for publishable mathematical independence.

### What does not yet kill it

- The negative result on the current finite family alone.
- The collapse of label-only `LossKernel`.
- Failure of one separation attempt.

Those results already happened and narrowed the branch honestly. `TF1` is only
killed when the stronger recast object also fails to become canonical or useful.

## 4. C1

### Current live content

`C1` has an earned finite core:
accessible stabilized records can reconstruct an observer-relative partial
temporal order under explicit descent conditions. The still-open frontier is
whether this remains a distinctive research line once dynamics and the
first-person bridge are taken seriously.

### Kill criterion

Kill `C1` as a distinct future branch if both of the following happen:

1. **Dynamics-collapse result**:
   in a dynamics-grounded model, record-stabilization order does no distinct
   work beyond ordinary causal reachability / provenance bookkeeping.
2. **Bridge-collapse result**:
   the first-person / accessible-witness frontier fails to produce a formal
   gap, because the relevant first-person verification task reduces to
   third-person record-graph computation or the gap objects collapse under the
   current restriction program.

If both happen, `C1` should no longer be pursued as "experienced time as
record finality." Its archival survivor would still be valuable:

- finite partial-order reconstruction theorems; and
- descent conditions for observer-relative event structures.

But those would then belong under a narrower record-order reconstruction line,
not a distinct time/experience program.

### What does not yet kill it

- The fact that `C1` does not prove phenomenal succession.
- One more finite counterexample to total-order rhetoric.
- The need for explicit descent data.

Those are already part of the branch's weakened status. `C1` is only killed as
a distinct research line if the remaining frontier collapses both
physically and phenomenologically.

## Cross-Branch Rule

The four branches share one practical decision rule:

```text
Do not kill a branch merely because it is weakened.
Kill it when its last remaining frontier is either absorbed in the same-data
sense, or requires an external realization that no realistic platform can even
freeze as a serious test object.
```

That rule preserves the repo's method:

- the North Star may stay ambitious;
- branch work must remain falsifiable; and
- archival survivors should be kept even when a frontier dies.

## Main Decision

The cleanest project-level kill criteria are:

1. `Q1` dies when all four child routes are either absorbed or operationally
   unrealizable.
2. `H7` dies as physics when no realistic substrate can even freeze, much less
   clear, the physical-deletion comparison object.
3. `TF1` dies as novelty when canonical witness obligations either cannot be
   derived or collapse without added review value.
4. `C1` dies as a distinct time/experience branch only if both its
   dynamics-side and phenomenal-bridge frontiers collapse.

## Blocker

The blocker is not missing branch-specific prose. The blocker is that the repo
often mixes three different outcomes:

- weakened but still live;
- dormant until an external handoff clears; and
- genuinely not worth further work.

Without separating those, a narrowed branch can keep generating motion even
after its live frontier is gone.
