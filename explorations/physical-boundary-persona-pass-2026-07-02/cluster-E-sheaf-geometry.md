# Cluster E — Sheaf / Category / Geometry

Owns the cohomological residue: the T411 survivor ("discriminating datum is a
global correlation present in NO proper subset of the reach") reframed as a
**failure of a local section to exist** — an H^1 / gluing obstruction. Central
task: does casting the survivor as a cohomology class let it escape
joint-record completion, or does it merely restate the wound more sharply?

## Persona 4 — Topologist / Sheaf Theorist

*(cluster lead on the cohomological line)*

### U1 — meaning of "physical rather than declared"

**Steelman.** Put a presheaf `F` of accessible descriptions over the reach:
to each subset `S ⊆ R` assign the states/statistics supported on `S`, with
restriction maps the marginalizations. State A and state B agree on every
*proper* subset (`res_S A = res_S B` for all `S ⊊ R`: worst proper-subset
marginal diff = 0.0) yet differ globally (full-joint diff = 0.124, verdicts
flip). That is precisely the statement that the local sections agree but do not
glue to equal global sections — the difference `A ⊟ B` is a **nonzero class in
H^1 of the reach's cover**, a Čech gluing obstruction. "Physical rather than
declared" is then given a mathematical meaning: the boundary carries a
cohomology class that **no choice of local (subset) description can trivialize**.
This is anti-relabel by construction — a support/light-cone functional is a
section of a *sheaf* (determined by its restrictions), so it is blind to an
H^1 class living in the difference of gluings.

**Strongest supporting argument.** The T411 numbers are literally a
non-gluing witness: agreement on all proper subsets + global disagreement is
the defining signature of a Čech 1-cocycle that is not a coboundary. A
light-cone bound (persona-independent asset from the brief) is a statement
about supports, i.e. about sections; it *cannot even express* an obstruction
class. So the surviving-the-wrong-absorber asset (LR relabel fails) is
explained, not accidental.

**Hidden assumptions.** (1) The cover of `R` by "proper subsets" is the
*right* cover and is held fixed. (2) The presheaf's restriction maps are the
physically forced ones (marginalization), not a declared truncation. (3) The
class is stable — not an artifact of one particular refinement.

**Vindicating computation.** Exhibit the cocycle explicitly: a Čech 1-cochain
on the proper-subset cover whose coboundary is zero on all pairwise overlaps
but which is not the coboundary of any global 0-cochain, and show its class is
invariant under refinement *within the declared reach*. Then show every
support functional annihilates it.

**Killing finding.** Enlarging the cover by one carrier (admit retained tier-1)
makes the class exact. If refinement kills it, it was a cover artifact.

**Confidence: 5/10.** The object is real; its independence from the declared
cover is exactly the contested point.

**Phase 2.**
- *Thesis.* The survivor is a nonzero H^1 gluing obstruction; "physical" =
  "carries a cohomology class no local description trivializes."
- *Antithesis (finite-closed-model + joint-record completion).* Čech
  cohomology is **computed relative to a cover you must supply**. In a finite
  closed model the cover is finite and refinable at will. Joint-record
  completion (the T401 kill, three-time killer) *is* cover refinement: admit
  the co-present retained tier-1 register (I(R:E1)=1.97 bits; one Z-measurement

  becomes a coboundary, H^1 → 0. The "proper subsets of the reach" were
  proper subsets of a *declared* reach; the equality region R is a declared
  strict subset of what is physically co-present. So the obstruction lives only
  relative to a declared cover, and dies under the physically-available refinement.
- *Synthesis (SHRINKS, does not dissolve).* What survives is **relative and
  honest**: *given a fixed cover, there is a genuine non-gluing certificate,
  anti-relabel and LR-invisible.* What does NOT survive is "physical rather
  than declared" — because cohomology **inherits** the cover question rather
  than answering it. The sheaf reframing's real contribution is to convert the
  vague wound into a sharp, testable one: **"is the cover physically forced?"**
  Cohomology cannot answer that from inside; it needs the cover handed to it by
  the *dynamics*. This passes the baton to U2 (a dynamically-forced cover =
  interaction-graph neighborhoods). Recorded shrinkage: the cohomological line
  is a sharper statement of the problem and a real anti-relabel asset, **not**
  an escape from joint-record completion.
