## 27 — Philosophy of Science Researcher

### U1 — meaning of "physical rather than declared"

- **Steelman:** The distinction is the classic observable/theoretical-term
  distinction sharpened for subsystems. A capability boundary is **physical iff
  it is fixed by the dynamical law** (which operators are local under the actual
  Hamiltonian / coupling graph) and **declared iff it is coordinate- or
  description-relative** (a chosen tensor factorization, a stipulated "retained"
  set). Operational test: physical boundaries are **invariant under the model's
  own relabeling group** (choice of subsystem decomposition compatible with the
  dynamics); declared ones move when you relabel.
- **Strongest supporting argument:** In physics the criterion for "real" is
  precisely invariance under the residual gauge/description freedom. A partition
  that survives every dynamics-compatible re-factorization is not bookkeeping.
- **Hidden assumptions:** That a finite closed model *has* a preferred
  factorization fixed by dynamics; that "local under the Hamiltonian" is
  uniquely defined; that budget is not smuggled in as description.
- **Vindicating theorem:** A no-go showing the reach set derived from the
  coupling graph + budget is invariant under all dynamics-compatible tensor
  re-factorizations, while the separating datum is not reproducible in any such
  re-factorization's R-marginal.
- **Killing finding:** Exhibit two dynamics-compatible factorizations that
  disagree on which side of the boundary the record lies → boundary is
  description-relative after all.
- **Confidence:** 5

**Hegelian.** *Thesis:* physical = fixed by dynamical law, invariant under
relabeling. *Antithesis (finite-closed-model):* in a finite closed unitary
model the global state is one vector; ANY subsystem boundary is a choice of
tensor factorization, and factorization is exactly the description freedom.
"Local under the Hamiltonian" narrows but does not eliminate the freedom, and
the three prior deaths show every equality region was a declared subset of the
co-present whole. *Synthesis (SHRUNK):* survives only as **dynamics-indexed
agent-relative reachability** — the boundary is physical *relative to a fixed
coupling graph and budget*, invariant under re-factorizations that preserve
those couplings, but NOT description-independent in the absolute sense the
program originally wanted. The invariance is real but conditional.

## 40 — Legal Scholar

### U1 — meaning of "physical rather than declared"

- **Steelman:** Sharpest framing in my vocabulary: **physical = jurisdiction
  fixed by territory/mechanism; declared = jurisdiction fixed by stipulated
  contract.** A boundary is **forced** iff it is a mechanism you *cannot contract
  around* — no agreement among the parties can relocate it. The "retained" set
  was a contract clause; a physical boundary is the coastline, not the treaty
  line.
- **Supporting argument:** Law already distinguishes constitutive limits
  (physically enforceable, e.g. you cannot contract to be in two places at once)
  from stipulated limits (choice of forum). Only the former binds absent
  consent. A capability wall is physical iff it binds even a maximally
  uncooperative, maximally resourced counterparty *within its actual powers*.
- **Hidden assumptions:** That the agent's "actual powers" (couplings, budget)
  are themselves non-negotiable givens and not another contract clause.
- **Vindicating theorem:** A separation that no admissible relabeling
  ("re-contracting" the partition) can dissolve, while the mechanism (coupling
  graph) is held fixed.
- **Killing finding:** Show the parties *can* contract around it — re-declare
  the partition and the wall moves → it was a forum-selection clause.
- **Confidence:** 5

**Hegelian.** *Thesis:* physical = cannot contract around the mechanism.
*Antithesis (finite-closed-model):* in a finite closed model the whole record is

pure stipulation — a forum-selection clause dressed as territory. The T401 kill
is exactly a court finding that the "boundary" was contractual: a single
Z-measurement on a retained, co-present register reaches across it.
*Synthesis (SHRUNK):* the only non-contractible content is the **budget as a
genuine incapacity** — if the agent literally lacks the coupling to reach the
register, that is territorial. But a *budget* is itself a stipulated endowment,
so the boundary is non-contractible only *given* an endowment. Physical =
mechanism-forced **relative to a fixed endowment**, not absolutely.

## 41 — Linguist

### U1 — meaning of "physical rather than declared"

- **Steelman:** This is a **use/mention and deixis** problem. "Declared" boundary
  = a *performative* whose reference is fixed by the speaker's coordinate frame
  (indexical: "here is R"). "Physical" boundary = a term whose reference is fixed
  by the world independent of the utterance. The program's risk is that
  "physical rather than declared" is the **next equivocation** in a series
  ("finality," "capability"): a single surface form covering two senses. The
  steelman that keeps it non-equivocal: physical boundaries have **rigid
  reference** (same referent across all admissible re-labelings / translations);
  declared ones are **indexical** (referent shifts with the frame).
- **Supporting argument:** Kripke rigidity gives a crisp test: translate the
  model into every dynamics-preserving vocabulary; a physical boundary keeps its
  extension, an indexical one does not.
- **Hidden assumptions:** That a dynamics-preserving translation class exists and
  is well-defined; that reference can be pinned without a frame.
- **Vindicating theorem:** Boundary extension invariant across the full
  translation class (all subsystem re-descriptions preserving the Hamiltonian).
- **Killing finding:** A translation preserving the dynamics that moves the
  boundary's extension → indexical, hence declared.
- **Confidence:** 4

**Hegelian.** *Thesis:* physical = rigid reference; declared = indexical.
*Antithesis (finite-closed-model):* in a finite closed model every subsystem
term is indexical — its referent is a chosen factorization, i.e. a frame. The
"retained set" is a pure indexical ("the ones I keep"). Three deaths = three
demonstrations that the boundary term shifted extension under re-description.
*Synthesis (SHRUNK):* "physical" earns rigid reference **only for the coupling
graph itself** (the interaction structure is frame-invariant); boundaries
*derived from* it inherit rigidity **only up to budget**, which is itself an
indexical ("within my time"). So the term is rigid on its dynamical core,
indexical on its budget envelope. It is NOT flatly equivocal — but it is
**two-part**, and prior claims conflated the rigid core with the indexical
envelope. That conflation IS the equivocation the Poet feared.

### U2 — transport rung

- **Steelman:** Transport gives the boundary term a **verb-derived, not
  noun-stipulated** reference: reach is defined by what propagation *does*, not
  by a label we *apply*. Grammatically this removes the indexical.
- **Supporting argument:** Deriving reference from process reduces indexical
  slippage — the referent is picked out by the dynamics' own predicate.
- **Hidden assumptions:** "Has not propagated here" is a stable predicate, not a
  covert tense/aspect indexical ("not yet").
- **Vindicating theorem:** Reach predicate has frame-invariant extension.
- **Killing finding:** The predicate is aspectual ("not-yet-reached"), so its
  extension moves with the time index → indexical returns.
- **Confidence:** 4

**Hegelian.** *Thesis:* verb-derived reach removes the indexical. *Antithesis
(joint-record completion):* the departed record is still *co-present* in the
global sentence; "within reach" quantified as "co-present and coupleable within
budget" makes reach an **aspectual** predicate — its extension is tensed by the
budget, and completion re-includes the departed term. *Synthesis:* transport
converts a noun-indexical ("the retained ones") into a verb-aspect ("the not-yet-
reached ones") — a real grammatical improvement, but aspect is still deictic.
Boundary rigidified in mood, not in reference.

### U3 — literature adjacency

- **Steelman:** Moving to HP vocabulary is a **register shift**; legitimate if it

  foreign form while smuggling foreign connotation).
- **Supporting argument:** Translation preserves truth-conditions; calque imports
  connotation ("horizon," "firewall") that was never earned in the source model.
- **Hidden assumptions:** A faithful translation of the recovery-cost content
  exists without the gravitational connotation.
- **Vindicating theorem:** Recovery-cost statement holds under literal
  translation into the finite-model vocabulary.
- **Killing finding:** Only the calque carries the boundary; the literal
  translation loses it.
- **Confidence:** 5

**Hegelian.** *Thesis:* translate HP faithfully. *Antithesis:* the seductive
move is the **calque** — importing "horizon/firewall" as loaded form.

### U4 — fallback
