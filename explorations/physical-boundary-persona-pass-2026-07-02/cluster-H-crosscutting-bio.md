## 29. Systems Biologist

### U1 — meaning of "physical rather than declared" (DISSIPATION LINE)

- **Steelman.** A boundary is physical when the record it bounds is maintained
  by *dissipation* — a non-equilibrium steady state (NESS). The distinction
  between "retained" and "departed" is not a declared partition when retention
  REQUIRES ongoing free-energy expenditure: a record you must actively pay to
  hold is physically retained; one you stop paying for *physically* departs
  (relaxes to equilibrium, information erased). "Forced" = you cannot hold the
  record without paying maintenance, and the departed record is one whose
  maintenance the agent cannot afford from inside R.
- **Strongest support.** Biological memory (phosphorylation states, proofreading,
  homeostatic set-points) is NESS-maintained; kinetic proofreading (Hopfield-
  Ninio) shows fidelity is *purchased* continuously with ATP — stop paying,
  fidelity decays. This makes "retained" a dynamical, dissipation-defined state,
  NOT a stipulated trace. It converts the declared partial-trace into a physical
  maintenance ledger. This is the strongest reply to decoherence-bookkeeping
  absorber #3: the trace is not hand-imposed if non-retention is a physically
  forced relaxation the agent cannot prevent from within R.
- **Hidden assumptions.** (i) The substrate has a genuine dissipative relaxation
  channel (open, not closed unitary!) so "stop paying → departs" is dynamical.
  (ii) Maintenance cost of the departed record exceeds R's affordable budget —
  an affordability floor, not just a positive price. (iii) The NESS is
  bistable/hysteretic enough that "retained" and "departed" are distinct basins.
- **Vindicating test.** A fixture where holding the discriminating record in R
  requires a maintenance power exceeding R's declared work budget, while R+ can
  afford it — so the A/B partition is set by *affordable dissipation*, not by a
