## Synthesis

The four specs share the same methodological pattern:

```text
bold open-problem intuition
  -> finite audit gate
  -> explicit failure/demotion criteria
  -> no claim promotion until implementation
```

T124 is the strongest near-term H7 guardrail. It converts "arrow from
finality" into a transformation-ledger question: for every apparent D1
increase, what blocks the reverse, and is the blocker constructor
impossibility, resource accounting, exported history, hidden environment,
coarse-graining, or an omitted return path?

T125 is the cleanest next step for the gauge branch. It does not ask whether
finality curvature is gravity. It asks whether boundary-indexed D1 profiles
can be transported coherently at all while preserving provenance of access
changes.

T126 inserts an intermediate geometry filter into the S1 line. A successful
finality colimit is not automatically spacetime-like; it first has to be a
canonical event-finality candidate and then pass finite causal-set diagnostics.

T127 is the most decisive LossKernel gate. Its default expected outcome is
demotion unless a same-neighbor-data witness can force distinct admissible
attribution verdicts solely through source-anchored witness obligations.

## Recommended Implementation Order

1. Implement T124 first if the goal is to protect H7 language after T128-style

   novelty/prior-art pressure.
3. Implement T125 before any further gauge, connection, curvature, or gravity
   prose.
4. Implement T126 before any stronger S1 or spacetime-colimit framing.
