# Quantum Consensus Finality: Top Five Steelman Synthesis And Persona Vote

## Status

Exploratory synthesis. This is not a claim update, test result, roadmap
commitment, Temporal Issuance claim movement, or physics evidence.

The input artifact ran 103 persona sections across Time as Finality, Temporal
Issuance, cross-repo OS/orchestration, and run-local consensus/no-consensus
extensions. This artifact compresses those responses into five meaningfully
different steelman arguments, then runs the same 103-persona inventory as a
quadratic-vote review panel.

## Voting Method

Per the Time as Finality persona-panel skill, each persona received 100 vote
points. A vote at intensity `i` costs `i^2`. Most personas spent two votes:
`8 + 6`, costing `64 + 36 = 100`. Some spent one high-conviction vote at
intensity `10`, costing `100`.

Rubric dimensions:

```text
N  novelty / profundity without ridiculousness
T  typability / mathematical precision
E  executable fixture readiness
R  absorber resilience / chance of useful residue after standard theory
X  cross-repo and cross-domain leverage
```

## Top Five Steelman Arguments

### S1 - Capability Non-Factorization Across Finalization

Core argument:

```text
Quantum advantage is a task-specific failure of capability to factor through
the record-finalization projection.
```

Typed shape:

```text
Q coherent process
pi_final : Q -> R
Cap_T,B,e : Q -> K

Question:
  Does Cap_T,B,e factor through pi_final?
```

Why it is strong: it turns the intuition into a native quantum-information and
Time-as-Finality audit. It does not claim faster information or new physics. It
asks whether same finalized record class can hide different future task
capability.

Why it is not ridiculous: known quantum separations already provide controls:
query complexity, contextuality, resource monotones, and circuit models.

First fixture: pick one known separation, define `pi_final` as destructive
measurement or record projection, and measure `Spread_Cap([r])`.

Demotion condition: if the witness is just ordinary QIT/resource theory with no
cross-domain transfer, label it translation residue.

### S2 - Holonomy / Contextuality Residue Lost By Records

Core argument:

```text
The quantum layer carries path-, phase-, context-, or cocycle-like structure
that endpoint-like finalized records may erase.
```

Typed shape:

```text
D       event / context DAG
F       local-context presheaf
A       transport or connection data
Hol(A)  task-relevant holonomy/contextual residue
R       finalized records
```

Why it is strong: it is the most geometric steelman. It connects holonomy,
Berry phase, contextuality, sheaf obstruction, and record projection without
claiming that consensus is physics.

Why it is not ridiculous: contextuality/sheaf frameworks and holonomy are real
mathematics already used near quantum theory.

First fixture: finite presheaf over measurement contexts where finalized local
records agree but global contextual capability differs.

Demotion condition: if Abramsky-Brandenburger contextuality, Berry phase, or
ordinary gauge theory fully owns the case, record absorber ownership and keep
only translation residue.

### S3 - Event-DAG Provenance And Local Finality

Core argument:

```text
The observed classical layer is better modeled as a local, provenance-rich
event DAG than as a global ledger or total order.
```

Typed shape:

```text
D        partial-order event DAG
prov(e)  ancestry / who-knew-what-when / record-carrier history
R_O      observer-local finalized records
Cap      future reconstruction or coordination capability
```

Why it is strong: it makes Hashgraph, vector clocks, actor traces, MMO
reconciliation, Git ancestry, and database provenance relevant in a bounded
way. Same payload can have different future capability because ancestry differs.

Why it is not ridiculous: it explicitly rejects a hidden universal ledger and
keeps locality, partial order, and observer access.

First fixture: same finalized payload with different provenance DAGs, then test
whether future reconstruction or merge capability differs.

Demotion condition: if database provenance or distributed-systems theory fully
explains it, keep it as a finite analogy and negative-control library.

### S4 - Metastable Probabilistic Finalization And Reversal Cost

Core argument:

```text
Finality is graded confidence and reversal cost, not a binary moment.
Avalanche-style metastability gives a finite model for thresholded record
stabilization.
```

Typed shape:

```text
sample rule
confidence counter
failure probability
reversal cost
record redundancy
rollback budget
```

Why it is strong: it turns "finalized loosely" into measurable quantities and
connects Avalanche, thermodynamics, settlement, decoherence, and distributed
simulation rollback.

Why it is not ridiculous: it is explicitly a finite model of record
stabilization, not a claim that matter votes.

First fixture: model repeated local sampling over record carriers and compare
`P_reversal`, `rollback_cost`, redundancy, and branch support.

Demotion condition: if it reduces to ordinary decoherence plus thermodynamic
cost plus protocol finality, record the collapse maps and stop there.

### S5 - Source / Shadow / Finality Effect Contract

Core argument:

```text
Finalization can erase coherent capability while creating classical platform
capability, but this is Project/Finalize/Lose unless a source-new Issue
witness defeats fixed-source absorbers.
```

Typed shape:

```text
Issue[S]     source-side new admissible structure
Project[O]   observer-facing shadow
Finalize[R]  stable, replayable record
Lose[K]      capability-relevant projection loss
```

Why it is strong: this is the least romantic and most protective version of the
whole idea. It lets the quantum/finality intuition live while preventing the
main category mistake: treating quantum advantage, measurement, or record loss
as source-side issuance.

Why it is not ridiculous: it matches the cross-repo contract already emerging
between Temporal Issuance and Time as Finality.

First fixture: fixed Hilbert space plus fixed observable algebra plus fixed
instrument/channel produces quantum capability and finalized records. Expected
verdict: `Project[O] + Finalize[R] + Lose[K]`, not `Issue[S]`.

Demotion condition: if no growing observable algebra, admissibility predicate,
or construction-space witness appears, do not promote source-side claims.

## Post-Vote Addendum: S6 - Sheafification As Finality Bridge

Status: proposed addition to the top-five synthesis, ready for persona re-vote.

This addendum incorporates the 2026-06-25 sheafification bridge proposal after
the original 103-persona vote. It does not rewrite the historical vote table.
The S1-S6 re-vote is recorded separately in
`explorations/quantum-consensus-finality-s6-revote-synthesis-2026-06-25.md`.

Steelman statement:

```text
The precise mathematical structure realizing the layered finality transition is
sheafification / effective descent over a site of local observer/context
fragments.
```

Layer mapping:

```text
Quantum metastable / avalanche layer:
  presheaf-like local data, including weak measurements, partial coherence,
  local probabilities, contextual residues, holonomy, indefinite causal
  structure, and non-factorizing capabilities.

Classical committed layer:
  sheaf-like globally glueable records whose sections can be accessed by
  independent environment fragments as stable, provenance-rich,
  Boolean-consistent Event-DAG-style facts.

Connecting bridge:
  the associated-sheaf reflector / sheafification unit, paired with the
  physical Darwinian redundancy process that supplies enough local overlap data
  for effective descent and observer objectivity.
```

Typed shape:

```text
(C, J)      site of local contexts, observer fragments, or causal neighborhoods
F           presheaf of local quantum / record data
aF          associated sheaf or effective descent completion
eta_F       unit map F -> i aF
Gamma(aF)   final record candidates / global sections
Cap         observer capability functor or task family
Loss_K      capability-relevant distinctions not preserved across eta_F
PO_rec      temporal/provenance partial order, if the site carries ancestry
```

Strictly typed version of the threshold:

```text
The Darwinian redundancy threshold is not the first moment F becomes
sheafifiable. In standard settings, the associated sheaf already exists.

The threshold is the point where local data has enough redundancy,
distinguishability, and overlap consistency for eta_F to support stable global
records for a declared observer capability.
```

Why it is strong:

- It gives S5 a categorical mechanism instead of leaving it as only an effect
  contract.
- It reframes S2 as obstruction to gluing rather than vague lost information.
- It gives S4 a local-to-global transition point: presheaf metastability to
  sheaf-like commitment.
- It gives S1 a sharper map: test whether `Cap(F)` factors through `Cap(aF)`.
- It makes S3 the expected output when the site contains causal/provenance
  morphisms.

Guardrails:

- For set-valued presheaves, loss should not be called a kernel or cokernel
  unless the target category supports those objects. The default loss object is
  the unit non-isomorphism / non-preservation profile of `eta_F`.
- Temporal order is not automatic from abstract sheafification. It becomes a
  theorem target only after the site includes causal/provenance structure or the
  construction proves a canonical ancestry relation.
- This is not new physics. Quantum Darwinism / Spectrum Broadcast Structure
  remains the physical objectivity absorber unless a witness survives the
  existing QD/SBS and fixed-H gates.

### Hegelian Analysis

Thesis, S4-dominant quantum layer:

```text
Finality begins as metastable probabilistic process: continuous environmental
sampling, graded confidence, reversible-in-principle coherent structure,
contextuality, holonomy, and no globally agreed temporal order.
```

Antithesis, S3-dominant classical layer:

```text
Finality appears as committed stable records: objective, provenance-rich,
Boolean-like Event-DAGs agreed upon by many observers. But this seems to demand
consistency, causality, and objectivity that the quantum-side local data does
not yet possess globally.
```

Synthesis, S6 bridge:

```text
Sheafification / effective descent resolves the contradiction by forcing local
data through gluing conditions. The quantum layer is preserved as local data,
canceled where its contextual residues cannot be globally glued, and elevated
into stable objective records.
```

The redundancy threshold is the dialectical turning point. Capability loss and
irreversibility are the price of consistency. A usable past appears when the
glued record object carries a stable ancestry relation. In this reading,
time-as-finality is not assumed at the bridge; it is the record-facing output of
local-to-global consistency under declared observer access.
