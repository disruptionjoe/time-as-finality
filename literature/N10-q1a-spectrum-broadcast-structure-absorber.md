## Absorber Shape

The relevant SBS target has the form:

```text
rho_{S E_1 ... E_n}
  = sum_i p_i |i><i|_S tensor rho_{E_1|i} tensor ... tensor rho_{E_n|i}
```

with conditional fragment states for different pointer values mutually
distinguishable, commonly represented by orthogonal support conditions such as:

```text
rho_{E_k|i} rho_{E_k|j} = 0 for i != j.
```

Read operationally, SBS says that multiple environment fragments carry
locally accessible, mutually agreeing classical information about the same
pointer observable, while the conditional product structure or strong
independence condition removes hidden inter-fragment dependence as an
objectivity resource.

That is very close to the intuitive part of Q1A. A Q1A witness is therefore
absorbed whenever its only surviving distinction is:

```text
raw fragment count is not enough;
independently accessible, mutually agreeing record fragments are needed.
```

SBS and strong Quantum Darwinism already own that physical objectivity burden.

## What This Improves

This closes a predictable expert objection. N7 compared Q1A to ordinary
Quantum Darwinism, decoherence, consistent histories, RQM, QBism, and Everett,
but an expert on quantum objectivity would also ask:

```text
Did you test the claim against Spectrum Broadcast Structure and strong
Quantum Darwinism?
```

The answer is now explicit. Q1A does not get credit for independently
supported redundant classical records until SBS and strong-QD data have been
granted to the neighbor.

## What This Weakens

This weakens any Q1A phrasing that treats D1 holder redundancy or
independence-corrected environmental records as a novel measurement-side
objectivity criterion.

The current Q1A survivor is narrower:

```text
an observer/access/provenance admissibility discipline over record evidence,
not a new quantum objectivity structure.
```

In particular, if the alleged D1 split disappears after the neighboring model
is allowed to declare:

- pointer observable;
- fragment partition;
- conditional fragment states;
- fragment distinguishability or support overlap;
- conditional independence or strong independence;
- observer-accessible fragment subset;

then Q1A is absorbed.

## Same-SBS-Data Reinstatement Gate

A future Q1A physics-facing witness must freeze all of the following before it
can claim residue against SBS or strong Quantum Darwinism:

1. The pointer observable or system measurement whose objectivity is at issue.
2. The full system-fragment state or an operationally equivalent experimental
   reconstruction target.
3. Conditional fragment states for the declared pointer values.
4. Fragment distinguishability or support-overlap bounds.
5. Conditional independence, strong independence, or the declared failure of
   that condition.
6. The observer-accessible fragment subset.
7. The provenance partition and any physical rule used to derive it.
8. The Q1A verdict map and support threshold.

Only after those data are fixed does a Q1A split count as non-absorbed.

The non-null target is:

```text
same SBS / strong-QD objectivity verdict
+ same observer-accessible fragment subset
+ same provenance-aware fragment partition
+ same audited accessible support count
but different Q1A finality verdict through a predeclared physical dimension
that the SBS / strong-QD neighbor cannot import without adding new physics.
```

No such witness is currently named in the repo.

## Demotion Rule

Demote a proposed Q1A objectivity witness to SBS/strong-QD absorption if:

- the only distinction is raw count versus independent fragment count;
- the evidence is ordinary fragment distinguishability;
- the split disappears after fragment partition and independence data are
  shared;
- the access cut is just a smaller fragment subset;
- provenance is only metadata for deciding the fragment partition;
- the D1 verdict factors through audited accessible support count;
- the witness changes branch/history availability, pointer data, or fragment
  state data rather than holding the neighboring objectivity target fixed.
