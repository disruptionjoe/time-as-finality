# N14: H7 Sector/Gauge Absorber

Status: primary sources checked 2026-06-22.

## Current Repo Verdict

Exact sector, superselection, and gauge-restriction proposals do not currently
reopen H7's physical-arrow route. After T168, their defensible role is
narrower:

```text
sector and gauge structure can be a serious stress test for H7's
physical_record_deletion gate, but it is not H7 evidence by default
```

The present literature supports four absorber readings before any H7 residue
is entertained:

1. exact superselection can be an ideal operation-class ban rather than a
   finite substrate-native deletion obstruction;
2. apparent sector locks can disappear once compensating reservoirs or
   reference-frame resources are admitted;
3. gauge-representative changes are not gauge-invariant physical deletion; or
4. bounded-region gauge sectors often encode boundary flux or edge-mode
   bookkeeping rather than a constructor-impossible record-deletion reverse.

## Absorber Shape

Sector and gauge language is dangerous for H7 because it can sound like
"deletion is impossible in principle" while actually moving between four
different objects:

```text
ideal operation ban
vs compensating-charge/resource accounting
vs gauge redundancy
vs boundary-sector bookkeeping
```

H7 asks a different question:

```text
does a physical_record_deletion reverse remain constructor-impossible after
conserved-quantity, reservoir, boundary, reference-frame, and control data are
matched?
```

That mismatch matters. A superselection statement can be scientifically real
and still fail H7 because:

- it forbids an operation only inside an artificially narrowed operation class;
- it can be lifted or relationally encoded once a reference frame or reservoir
  is included;
- it identifies only gauge redundancy rather than a physical deletion event; or
- it tracks boundary flux sectors that belong to subsystem bookkeeping, not to
  substrate-native deletion impossibility.

## What This Improves

T168 already gave the repo a finite executable screen. N14 makes that screen
source-backed rather than purely internal.

The key improvement is a sharper distinction:

```text
sector/gauge restriction != constructor-impossible physical deletion
```

That matters because exact-sector language is one of the easiest ways to
smuggle a paper-sounding impossibility claim into H7 without matching the
actual conserved-quantity, reference-frame, or boundary data.

## What This Weakens

This weakens a recurring reopening story:

```text
maybe superselection or gauge structure already gives the irreversibility H7
wants
```

The current literature does not support that upgrade. The cleanest known cases
either:

- work only inside a narrowed local operation class;
- move the obstruction into compensating reservoirs, flux transfer, or charge
  bookkeeping;
- dissolve once relational reference-frame resources are admitted; or
- describe boundary-sector structure rather than physical deletion.

All four are serious neighboring structures. None is yet the H7 object.

## Sector/Gauge Reinstatement Gate

A future H7 proposal drawing on sector or gauge physics must freeze all of the
following before it counts as more than a neighbor citation:

1. The encoded record token and the exact delete / reverse task.
2. The reverse-edge class:
   `physical_record_deletion`, not gauge relabeling, boundary-flux transfer,
   access denial, or sector-preserving operation-class restriction.
3. The allowed control class, including whether compensating reservoirs,
   ancillas, reference frames, charge exchange, edge modes, or boundary
   couplings are granted.
4. The fixed absorber vector:
   - conserved quantities and where they can flow;
   - reservoir or compensating sink state;
   - boundary flux / edge-mode data;
   - reference-frame or phase-standard resources;
   - observer/control boundary;
   - source-copy and reversible-handle status.
5. The sector/gauge-specific absorber data:
   - exact versus approximate symmetry status;
   - finite enforcement mechanism and leakage path;
   - gauge-invariant observables distinguishing the candidate states;
   - subsystem or bounded-region gluing assumptions.
6. A task-natural future-operation split that survives after those fields are
   matched.
7. A reason the reverse is impossible, not merely forbidden by ideal
   stipulation, local-sector bookkeeping, missing reference frame, or omitted
   reservoir channels.

No current sector/gauge source in this note clears that burden for H7.

## Demotion Rule

Demote a sector/gauge proposal to absorber-owned territory if:

- the headline obstruction is an exact operation-class ban with no finite
  enforcement audit;
- deletion becomes possible once compensating charges, reservoirs, or boundary
  flux transfer are admitted;
- the difference is pure gauge or representative choice rather than a
  gauge-invariant record distinction;
- the proposal needs a missing reference frame, phase standard, or relational
  ancilla to state the distinction operationally;
- the result is really bounded-region sector bookkeeping or edge-mode
  structure; or
- the supposed residue vanishes once the delete/reverse task is restated as
  conserved-quantity accounting, protected control, or boundary management.
