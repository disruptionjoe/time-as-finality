## Why it sits in this repo's literature

The open problem [rg-flow-as-multiscale-transport-analogy](../open-problems/rg-flow-as-multiscale-transport-analogy.md)
was opened from Avramidi-Barvinsky's asymptotic freedom of higher-derivative gravity as a calibration
template for the north-star "typed multiscale transport network." Mannheim's conformal (Weyl-squared)
gravity is the maximally symmetric member of the same higher-derivative family and adds the cleanest
version of the fixed-point picture:

- exact conformal symmetry means no intrinsic scale at all — the "fixed point" is not just where
  couplings stop flowing but where scale itself is absent;
- mass scales (and hence clocks, durations, and any notion of "a scale at which structure lives) exist
  only after dynamical symmetry breaking.

That is the sharpest available external example of "scale-invariant structure at the fixed point,
scale-dependent effective content away from it" — the shape the multiscale program is calibrating
against.
