## Why it sits in this repo's literature

Levin/Fields formalize *competency* as the observer-dependent capacity of a system to reach preferred
regions (attractors / goal states) in an arbitrary abstract space despite perturbations, measured
empirically by intervention (barriers, damage, novel prompts) — invariant across substrate and scale.
That is, structurally, TaF's capability object C(R): a region-indexed assignment of what can be enacted,
probed by intervention rather than read from statistics. Two specific alignments:

- **Competency-measured-by-perturbation (#19)** is TaF's own method (T392/T393 certify capability by
  what interventions supported on the region can and cannot do), arrived at by an independent research
  program on a completely different substrate. This is a candidate external witness for the
  region-indexed capability discriminator (`open-problems/region-indexed-capability-discriminator.md`).
- **Cognitive light cone (#4)** — the space-time (or abstract) boundary of what a system can measure,
  model, and act on — is a region-indexing of capability by construction; it is a calibration neighbor
  for how C(R) should depend on R.

## The dual role: neighbor AND absorber candidate

This cuts both ways and must be held both ways:

- **As calibration:** an independent-process formalization of capability-by-intervention strengthens the
  case that C(R) is a natural object, not a TaF idiosyncrasy.
- **As absorber:** if TaF's C(R) reduces without residue to Levin/Fields competency (or to its active-
  inference substrate — see `temporal-issuance/absorbers/active-inference-free-energy.md`), then C(R)
  is not novel and the capability-discriminator lane must show surplus over it. The named surplus
  candidate is the T395 result — a capability difference with *zero statistical trace* on the region —
  which competency-by-navigation does not obviously predict (navigation success is an observed statistic).
