# N3: Core Formalism Known Neighbors

Status: primary sources checked 2026-06-11.

This note positions the v0.1 formal result against the closest established
ingredients. It records overlap before novelty.

## Lamport: Causal Partial Order

Leslie Lamport's 1978 paper defines the "happened before" relation as a partial
order for distributed events and distinguishes that causal order from an
imposed total order generated by logical clocks.

Primary source:

- Leslie Lamport, "Time, Clocks, and the Ordering of Events in a Distributed
  System," *Communications of the ACM* 21(7), 1978.
  [Author PDF](https://lamport.azurewebsites.net/pubs/time-clocks.pdf) and
  [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1145/359545.359563).

**Overlap:** T1's refusal to order causally independent events is established
distributed-systems discipline, not a new result.

**Possible delta:** T1 attaches an observer-indexed record-stabilization
preorder to the causal graph and asks whether stabilization frontiers can
reconstruct a temporal partial order. This packaging is the contribution, if
it survives stronger models.

## Quantum Darwinism: Redundant Records And Objectivity

Ollivier, Poulin, and Zurek model the environment as a witness that
selectively proliferates information, allowing independent observers to infer
preferred observables from redundant environmental imprints. Zurek's later
review develops the relation between proliferated records and classical
robustness.

Primary sources:

- Harold Ollivier, David Poulin, and Wojciech H. Zurek, "Environment as a
  Witness: Selective Proliferation of Information and Emergence of
  Objectivity in a Quantum Universe," *Physical Review A* 72, 042113 (2005).
  [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/quant-ph/0408125) and
  [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevA.72.042113).
- Wojciech H. Zurek, "Quantum Darwinism," *Nature Physics* 5, 181-188 (2009).
  [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/0903.5082).

**Overlap:** redundancy, multiple observers, record proliferation, and the
connection between robust records and effective classical objectivity are
prior art.

**Possible delta:** D1 refuses to reduce finality to redundancy alone. It
keeps bounded accessibility, independent branch support, and reversal count
as separate preorder dimensions. T1 does not yet show that this extension
adds value in a quantum model.

## Hartle And Gell-Mann: IGUSes

Hartle treats past, present, and future as information-processing categories
of information gathering and utilizing systems rather than properties added
to four-dimensional spacetime. Hartle and Gell-Mann use IGUSes more broadly
in decoherent-histories quantum cosmology.

Primary sources:

- James B. Hartle, "The Physics of 'Now'," *American Journal of Physics* 73,
  101-109 (2005). [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/gr-qc/0403001) and
  [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1119/1.1783900).
- Murray Gell-Mann and James B. Hartle, "Equivalent Sets of Histories and
  Multiple Quasiclassical Realms," 1994.
  [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/gr-qc/9404013).

**Overlap:** non-anthropocentric information-processing observers and
observer-relative temporal categories are established ideas.

**Possible delta:** D2's trace-bearer/recorder/reconciler taxonomy is an
engineering decomposition for the executable model, not a replacement for
IGUS theory.

## Halliwell: Records Of Histories

Halliwell studies the connection between decoherent histories and records,
including imperfect records for mixed initial states.

Primary source:

- J. J. Halliwell, "Where Is the Information Stored When Histories
  Decohere?," *Physical Review D* 60, 105031 (1999).
  [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/quant-ph/9902008) and
  [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevD.60.105031).

**Overlap:** reconstructing or characterizing histories through records is
not unique to Time as Finality.

**Possible delta:** the present toy model asks a narrower
substrate-independent graph question. A future quantum version must state
exactly what it adds to records-based decoherent histories.

## Landauer: Logical And Physical Erasure

Landauer connects logically irreversible operations with physical
irreversibility and heat generation.

Primary source:

- Rolf Landauer, "Irreversibility and Heat Generation in the Computing
  Process," *IBM Journal of Research and Development* 5(3), 183-191 (1961).
  [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1147/rd.53.0183).

**Constraint on T5:** counting graph deletions is not a thermodynamic cost.
The v0.1 erasure-cost field is only a proxy that demonstrates possible
ordering divergence. A physical T5 requires a memory model, temperature, and
erasure protocol.

## Positioning Verdict

No primitive ingredient in v0.1 is novel by itself. The defensible research
object is the synthesis:

> an observer-indexed componentwise preorder over accessible record support,
> holder redundancy, causal branch independence, and graph reversal count,
> paired with stabilization-frontier reconstruction and explicit
> incomparability.

The current evidence supports publishing this only as a toy formal framework
and negative/limiting result. A claim of new physics would be unsupported.
