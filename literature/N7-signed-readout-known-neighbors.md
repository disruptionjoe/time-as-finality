## Quantum Measure Theory

Sorkin's quantum-measure framework classifies interference by higher-order
sum rules. Ordinary quantum theory permits second-order interference while
the third-order term vanishes.

- Rafael D. Sorkin, "Quantum Mechanics as Quantum Measure Theory,"
  *Modern Physics Letters A* 9, 3119-3127 (1994).
  [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/gr-qc/9401003).

**Relation to T13:** the toy readout `mu(S) = |sum_S w|^2` has nonzero `I2`
and symbolic cancellation of `I3`. T13 uses this only as a structural
positioning device. It does not derive quantum mechanics.

## Decoherent Histories And Records

Records are central in decoherent-histories approaches, where histories
become meaningful through correlations with records.

- J. J. Halliwell, "Records, Histories and Decoherence,"
  [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/quant-ph/9902008).

**Relation to T13:** T13 separates record finality from readout. This is
compatible with record-based quantum foundations, but it does not solve the
measurement problem.

## Quantum Darwinism

Quantum Darwinism explains classical objectivity through redundant
environmental records.

- Wojciech H. Zurek, "Quantum Darwinism," *Nature Physics* 5, 181-188
  (2009). [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1038/nphys1202).

**Relation to T13:** redundancy can stabilize records, but redundancy alone
does not encode the phase-sensitive readout used in T13.

## Wigner Negativity And Contextuality

Howard et al. show that contextuality, expressed through Wigner-function
negativity, is a resource for magic-state quantum computation.

- Mark Howard, Joel Wallman, Victor Veitch, and Joseph Emerson,
  "Contextuality Supplies the Magic for Quantum Computation," *Nature* 510,
  351-355 (2014). [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/1401.4174).

**Relation to T13:** the result reinforces the guardrail that phase-sensitive
or signed structure can carry information not present in ordinary positive
record counts. T13 does not model contextuality.
