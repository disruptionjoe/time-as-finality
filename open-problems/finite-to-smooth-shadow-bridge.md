# Finite-To-Smooth Shadow Bridge

## Status

Open bridge problem. This file is a research target extracted from the
North Star v0.8 62-persona steelman and Hegelian pass.

The problem is methodological: finite TaF witnesses have generated useful
mathematics, but the maximal North Star speaks in smooth geometric language.
No smooth physics-facing claim should lean on a finite witness unless a bridge
has been declared and tested.

## Problem

Which finite TaF objects, if any, can be safely compared to smooth, measurable,
causal, sheaf, bundle, gauge, or spin-geometric objects?

The bridge must state what is preserved:

- observer shadow;
- visible equivalence;
- capability object;
- obstruction or witness obligation;
- access boundary;
- transport or composition law;
- gauge or representation equivalence;
- residue label.

## Candidate Finite Sources

Candidate finite objects include:

- D1 restriction systems;
- typed transport networks;
- LossKernel / witness-obligation fixtures;
- causal record graphs;
- detector provenance DAGs;
- accessible-witness gap objects;
- consensus finality protocols.

## Candidate Smooth Or Structured Targets

Candidate targets include:

- sheaves or presheaves over causal regions;
- bundles over an observed base;
- connection or transport data;
- measurable fields of access profiles;
- local observable algebras;
- causal diamonds or domains of dependence;
- resource-theoretic state spaces;
- spin or spin-c lift data.

## Bridge Types

Every proposed bridge must name one of these or define a new one precisely:

```text
discretization map
inverse or direct limit
nerve / simplicial realization
sheafification
coarse-graining functor
continuum scaling limit
measurable-field construction
finite cover approximation
category of models with comparison functor
```

## Acceptance Conditions

A proposed bridge is credible only if:

1. source and target objects are typed independently;
2. the preserved capability object is declared before the example;
3. the bridge preserves the relevant visible equivalence or explains its
   transformation;
4. gauge, coordinate, relabeling, and representation redundancies are
   quotient-tested on the target side;
5. at least one positive control and one hostile control are included;
6. the result states whether the bridge preserves, weakens, or destroys the
   finite residue.

## Failure Modes

The bridge fails if:

- it maps finite labels to smooth words without a functor or comparison map;
- the smooth target adds the capability object by hand;
- the obstruction disappears under refinement with no accounted residue;
- gauge or coordinate choices create the apparent distinction;
- every example is only a visualization of finite bookkeeping.

## First Bounded Run

Pick one finite family, preferably a typed transport network or accessible
witness gap object.

Then attempt exactly one target:

```text
finite cover -> presheaf/sheaf over a causal or access-indexed base
```

The output should answer:

```text
Does the finite capability-spread or witness-obligation verdict survive
restriction, refinement, or sheafification?
```

## Claim Impact If Successful

A successful first bridge does not prove smooth physics. It permits a narrower
statement:

```text
At least one finite TaF witness has a declared route into a structured
geometric target without losing its typed capability verdict.
```

## Claim Impact If Refuted

If no bridge survives, the North Star should keep smooth-geometric language in
vision or source-vocabulary space only. Finite TaF witnesses would remain
useful, but they would not carry smooth geometry claims.
