# Integrated Finality Open Problems

## Resolved v0.1 Question

Can coupling profile, inherited expression, proof validation, protocol
confidence, finality profile, and signed readout coexist in one finite typed
pipeline without collapsing into one scalar notion of truth?

T14 answers: yes, as a bounded witness, if the stages remain typed.

## Open Questions

1. Does the integrated separation survive generated families of larger random
   record graphs?
2. What are the minimal breakpoints where coupling, expression, proof
   validation, and readout become mutually inconsistent?
3. Can proof costs, stale certificates, and epoch revocation be modeled
   without ideal proof functionality?
4. Can phase-bearing records arise from T9-style dynamics rather than being
   assigned by hand?
5. Which enriched profile object, if any, tracks enough information for
   signed readout without collapsing finality into readout?

## Guardrail

Do not use T14 to claim that consensus creates truth, proofs establish truth,
or finality derives quantum mechanics. T14 is an integration stress test.
