# Persistent Dynamical Reconciler Open Problems

## Resolved v0.1 Question

Can a finite local-update model represent a reconciler whose memory records,
access boundary, and equality decision arise inside the system dynamics rather
than being selected as a terminal observer window?

T17 answers: yes, in a bounded two-record latch-and-comparator system.

## Open Questions

1. Can the architecture be generated by search rather than designed directly?
2. Can records degrade, be copied, be overwritten, or be erased while the
   reconciler still distinguishes stored false from no record?
3. What is the weakest subsystem that deserves the name reconciler?
4. Can the generated boundary grow or shrink with coupling history rather
   than being fixed by the cell wiring?
5. Can explicit resource costs be attached to storing, preserving, comparing,
   and reversing records?
6. Does a larger generated record ecology preserve observer-relative
   finality profiles without collapsing into ordinary reachability?

## Guardrail

Do not use T17 to claim that consciousness has been modeled. T17 models a
minimal record-processing subsystem only.
