## The pointer

The Reeh-Schlieder theorem in algebraic QFT: the vacuum (and any bounded-energy
state) is cyclic and separating for every local algebra — operationally, there
are no perfectly localized records, and local operations on any finite region
have nonzero matrix elements to states differing arbitrarily far away.

## Why it bears on the discriminator

The primary open problem needs a capability boundary that is physical rather
than declared bookkeeping. Reeh-Schlieder is a candidate source of exactly the
needed structure, in either direction:

1. **Constraint direction.** If exact local records do not exist in QFT, then
   any capability assignment C(R) built on record custody inside a region is
   approximate by physical law, not by modeling choice. The declared-basis
   caveat that weakened T407 might be replaced by a physically forced
   approximation scale (localization tails), which is the difference between
   "bookkeeping boundary" and "physical boundary."
2. **Mechanism direction.** The split-property / type-III factor structure of
   local algebras (no pure states on a local algebra, intrinsic entanglement
   across any boundary) may supply the boundary-crossing menu the discriminator
   needs: operations available to R jointly with its complement that no
   R-supported statistics distinguish.

## Honest scope

This is a literature-shaped lead, not a result: nothing here is computed, and
the finite-witness house style does not directly model type-III algebras. A
first bounded step would be a finite proxy for localization tails (a record
whose support provably overlaps the boundary by construction) run against the
T392/T393 certificate toolkit. If the proxy is absorbed as ordinary noise
modeling, record that and drop the lead.
