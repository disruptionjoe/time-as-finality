# papers/candidates/

Papers that have **graduated out of `papers/drafts/`** and are staged for
publication, but are **not yet public**. A folder here means: Joe has explicitly
said he intends to publish this paper, and it has passed the light staging gate
below. It does **not** mean it has been submitted or posted.

Each candidate is a **fresh, purpose-written document** (written from the research
workings in `open-problems/`, `results/`, `technical-reports/`, etc. — those are
never moved here). A candidate lives in its own subfolder with a `STAGING-NOTES.md`.

## Lifecycle (the three stages)

1. **`papers/drafts/`** — work in progress. All drafts, notes, prompts, review
   passes, and previous versions live and stay here. Edit freely.
2. **`papers/candidates/`** (this folder) — a paper graduates here **only when Joe
   has explicitly said he wants to publish it.** The hardened carrier moves; its
   earlier draft versions stay in `papers/drafts/`. Treat as near-final;
   substantive changes should be deliberate.
3. **`papers/published/`** (sibling) — the public record, **only after Joe
   confirms it has actually been published** (arXiv id / DOI / live URL). Append-
   mostly.

A paper lives in exactly one stage at a time, so its folder always tells the truth
about where it stands.

## The light staging gate

Kept deliberately light (this is not the hostile-referee deep-dive — that happens
as a `drafts/` review pass, e.g. an adversarial deep-research run, *before*
staging). A draft enters this folder only after a one-pass check that:

1. **The title matches the earned core** — the headline claims only what is
   actually established, at its true grade; no overreach.
2. **No downgraded/absorbed wording leaks in** — evidence grades are explicit
   (finite-witness / computed / recorded / internally established, per the Canon
   Index and COMPLEXITY-LEDGER); nothing reads stronger than its evidence; an
   absorbed or re-scoped result is never presented as established.
3. **Every external citation resolves** — author, title, arXiv id / DOI correct.
4. **The single sharpest open issue is acknowledged in-text.**
5. **No overlap with another staged candidate** — if two drafts share a core, only
   the hardened carrier stages.

Two standing TaF guards also hold at the gate:

- **Single-process ceiling.** A candidate is capped at *internally established*
  until it is actually published/externally read. The text must not imply external
  validation it has not received. Publication (the `published/` step) is the
  external move; it is Joe's action.
- **Cross-repo one-way rule.** GU / temporal-issuance material may be discussed as
  stress-test or adjacency, **never cited as support** for a TaF claim.

Each candidate carries a `STAGING-NOTES.md` recording its scope, honest grade,
open items, the gate pass, and the deep-research/adversarial review it survived.
