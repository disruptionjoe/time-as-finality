## Abstract (one paragraph)

The Time-as-Finality program sought a *finality boundary* that is **physical
rather than declared** — a difference in what a bounded region can do that is
forced by the model's own structure, not by a bookkeeping choice. Across a dozen
finite-witness constructions the boundary was repeatedly absorbed, and this draft
argues the failures share one cause. In any **finite closed** model the
discriminating datum is always co-present (a Declarability Lemma); making the
boundary *physical* was shown to require leaving the single-instance regime via an
asymptotic limit, a hardness assumption, or a structural symmetry no-go (the
four-mode taxonomy E0/E1/E2/E3). A separate line then asked whether the surviving
"global datum with no local witness" (finality = legitimacy = the capability
separator) could be **canonically computed** — the same object from two independent
descriptions. Three constructions (T422–T424) show it cannot, on finite objects,
for one reason: **any finite invariant equal to the datum is a relabel of the local
structure, while any genuinely independent invariant computes different data —
independence and agreement are in tension.** The upshot is a single claim:
**physicality and canonicity are the same kind of thing — limit phenomena, not
finite-closed facts.** This is the capability/finality instantiation of a known
idealization principle (sharp structure requires a limit); its finality-specific
form, and the identification of "physically forced" with "canonically computable,"
is the new content, offered as perspective, not theorem.

## 1. The question

The program's lead object is the bounded-region capability measure `C(R, M)`: the
transformations enactable from a region `R` under a menu `M`. A **capability
boundary** separates two configurations that agree on every `R`-supported statistic
yet differ in an enactable/recoverable transformation. The load-bearing adjective
was **physical**: the separation should be *forced by the dynamics/structure*, not
a *declared* restriction on access. Twelve+ finite fixtures tried to earn it; each
was absorbed. This draft synthesizes why.

## 2. The finite-closed obstruction (the scope theorem)

Two results fix the terrain (`open-problems/finite-closed-capability-boundary-scope-theorem.md`,
after adversarial review):

- **Declarability Lemma (proven, elementary, algebraic form).** In a finite closed
  model, if two configurations agree on the observable algebra `⟨M_R⟩`, the
  separating datum lies *outside* `⟨M_R⟩` but is present in the global algebra —
  classically a function of the co-present complement, quantumly an `R`–`Rᶜ`
  correlation in no marginal (masking). The information to cross is **always
  co-present**; the informational boundary is **declared**, never a physical
  absence. (This is joint-record completion in general form.)

- **Forcing requires leaving the single instance (four modes).** A *physical*
  boundary is not an informational absence — it is an obstruction to recovering the
  co-present datum. In a finite closed single instance that recovery cost is finite,
  so a non-declared boundary must come from outside the single-instance regime, in
  one of three modes: **E1** an asymptotic/limit gap, **E2** a hardness assumption,
  or **E3** a structural-symmetry / conservation no-go. (E0 = declared, collapses.)
  Adversarial review corrected an over-strong universal framing and added E3; the
  surviving object is a **taxonomy**, adopted internally as the program's map
  (`technical-reports/capability-boundary-mode-taxonomy-REFERENCE.md`).

The theme — *sharp/physical structure is not a finite-closed single-instance fact*
— is the idealization principle of Kadanoff, Butterfield, and Landsman, here
specialized to capability boundaries (§6).

## 3. The surviving object and the canonicity question

What survived every absorber is not a boundary but an **object**: a datum *present
in the whole but in no proper part* — a "global section with no local witness" (an
H¹ gluing obstruction / a non-atomic value). A reverse meta-synthesis pass
(`explorations/meta-synthesis-reverse-pass-2026-07-02/`) found this same object
independently across registers: **finality** (time), **legitimacy** (institutions,
the Aumann–Shapley non-atomic value; List–Pettit's discursive dilemma), and the
**capability separator** (the T413/T422 no-proper-subset datum). It also sharpened
the taxonomy: **E1 and E3 are cohomologically cognate; E2 (computational hardness)
has no functorial/homotopy representative** — a claim that *retro-predicts* the
program's own negatives (the E2 arrow's failure; the E3 adapter disanalogy).

That raised the decisive question. If this object is real and cross-domain, can it
be **canonically computed** — exhibited as the *same finite datum* by two
*independent* descriptions, so its identity does not depend on a chosen category?
Canonicity is the difference between a genuine invariant and a coincidence.

## 4. The three walls (why finite canonicity fails)

Three finite-witness constructions answer: **no**, on finite objects, for one
reason.

- **T422 — representability span (GO-qualified, then bounded).** E1 and E3 realized
  as one Čech `H¹(C_n;A)` differing only in coefficient (ℤ winding vs ℤ/2 twist);
  E2's hardness verdict flips across a verified CRT isomorphism while the object's
  Čech invariants are preserved. Genuine content — but the E2-vs-{E1,E3} separation
  is **category-relative**: no single canonical category makes E1/E3 representable
  and E2 not. *Wall 1: category-relativity.*

- **T423 — value route (observer-game, REDESIGN).** A genuine List–Pettit discursive
  dilemma whose collective verdict is the global-no-local datum, with Aumann–Shapley
  value = the T413 separator. But the two "independent" descriptions (Shapley value
  and inconsistency-obstruction) are **one majority primitive read twice** — forced
  by **Shapley linearity** (`φ` linear in the game ⇒ exact agreement ⟺ same game ⟺
  shared primitive). *Wall 2: shared-primitive (value direction).*

- **T424 — index route (fiber-variation test, REDESIGN).** The one channel the
  linearity lemma cannot touch. Combinatorial-Hodge and signed-graph-frustration
  invariants **genuinely escape the relabel bar** (non-constant on the verdict's
  fibers — strictly more than T423 achieved) — but **neither equals the finality
  separator** across the class; the spectral-flow channel is null at n=3 (gapless
  triangle Dirac). *Wall 3: independence ⊥ agreement (index direction).*

Guard-the-guard controls pass in each; the walls are trustworthy, not artifacts.

## 5. The unified claim

The three walls are one wall, and it has a precise statement:

> **On a finite closed/combinatorial object, the global-no-local finality datum is
> finite-witnessed but not canonically computed. Any invariant that *equals* it is a
> function of the local/declared structure (a relabel — Shapley-linearity and its
> index-side twin); any genuinely *independent* invariant computes *different* data.
> Independence and agreement are in tension; you get one, not both. Escape requires
> a genuine continuum (an asymptotic limit, a Brillouin circle, a spin structure) —
> and *choosing* the continuum is exactly the hand-picked category of Wall 1.**

Set beside the scope theorem, the shape is identical. A **physical** boundary
(scope theorem) and a **canonical** invariant (T422–T424) are the same demand — that
a global datum be forced independently of a declared/local choice — and both fail on
finite objects for the same reason, and both are recovered only in a limit or under
an assumption. Hence:

> **Physicality and canonicity are limit phenomena, not finite-closed facts.**
> Finality — the whole program's object — is real at every finite scale but *sharp,
> physical, canonical* only in the limit (E1), under an assumption (E2), or via a
> symmetry no-go (E3). On a single finite witness it is always a relabel of the
> declared structure.

This closes the arc's loop: the M2 canonicity failure has the *same form* as the
scope theorem's physicality obstruction. It also gives the North Star ("time as
increasing finality") a precise, sobering shape — finality is an idealization that
lives in the limit, the way a phase transition does.

## 6. Honest positioning (what is and is not new)

**Pre-empted components (verified by arXiv fetch across the arc):**
- "Sharp structure needs a limit": Kadanoff's extended-singularity theorem
  (1002.2985); Butterfield "Less is Different" (1106.0702); Landsman (1305.4473).
- E3 / symmetry no-go: Wigner–Araki–Yanase (Kuramochi–Tajima 2208.13494); resource
  theory of asymmetry (Marvian–Spekkens); pseudo-Hermitian metric selection
  (Mostafazadeh, quant-ph/0508214).
- E2 / computation: computational vs information-theoretic indistinguishability
  (Goldwasser–Micali; Yao); a live counter-thesis embedding hardness as a
  thermodynamic coordinate (2501.15950).
- The value/index machinery: Shapley–Shubik power index; List–Pettit judgment
  aggregation; Harary signed-graph balance; combinatorial Hodge (HodgeRank);
  Livshits–Kimelfeld (Shapley value of an inconsistency, 2009.13819).

**The new content (offered as synthesis/perspective, not theorem):**
1. The **four-mode taxonomy of forcing** applied to capability/finality boundaries,
   with the **representability stratification** (E1,E3 cohomological; E2 not).
2. The **three finite walls** as computed finite-witness results with a shared
   structural cause (independence ⊥ agreement), which *retro-predicts* the program's
   own negatives.
3. The **identification of physically-forced with canonically-computable**, and the
   claim that both are **limit phenomena** — the finality-specific instantiation of
   the idealization principle.

**What it is not.** Not a proven theorem: the Declarability Lemma and the two
relabel-lemmas are proven on finite objects, but the *unification* (physicality ≡
canonicity; the whole thing as one limit phenomenon) is a structural analogy across
registers, not a natural isomorphism. Not novel physics or social choice — every
mechanism has an established home. Not externally established — single-process
ceiling; the supporting witnesses share one process, so their convergence is a
target. Honest tier: **a perspective with a proof-shaped finite-witness diagnosis.**

## 7. What it is good for, and the one genuine frontier

- **It retires the mode-by-mode chase.** Every finite swing at a single mode was
  pre-empted or absorbed; the durable object is the *structure relating the modes*,
  and its finite non-canonicity is now proof-shaped, not a run of bad luck.
- **It reframes the primary open problem** as candidate-provably out of finite
  scope, with named successors: the R1 relabel-proof separator (built) and the
  (E1)/(E2)/(E3) family/assumption/symmetry boundaries.
- **The one genuine remaining frontier is the limit itself** — the non-atomic
  (Aumann–Shapley) value, the continuum index, the thermodynamic limit. That is
  *analysis*, not finite fixtures, and outside the finite-witness house style; it is
  the honest next kind of work, not the next swing.
