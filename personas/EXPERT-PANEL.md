# Expert Persona Panel — Canonical Source

**This file is the canonical source for the numbered expert personas** used to
evaluate Time as Finality research. Other locations mirror or reference it:

- `personas/INDEX.md` — the persona *lens* registry (review postures); references
  this panel for the named experts.
- `agent-skills/time-as-finality-persona-panel/` — the panel skill; its
  `references/personas.md` is a **mirror** of this file and its
  `scripts/build_panel_prompt.py` reads this file directly.
- `workflows/registries/persona-clusters.md` — maps these numbered personas into
  discipline clusters for scoring.

Edit personas here. Do not edit the skill mirror by hand.

Use these personas as independent evaluators. Their role is to critique, extend,
falsify, and strengthen the mathematical and scientific program.

## Panel Evolution

The Expert Panel is intentionally expected to evolve over time.

New expert personas should be added whenever repeated research identifies missing
disciplinary perspectives or recurring blind spots.

Existing experts should rarely be removed. Instead, they may be deprecated,
merged, or superseded while preserving historical numbering and compatibility
where practical.

The objective is to preserve continuity while allowing the research program to
continually improve its coverage of the idea space.

## Standard Evaluation Pattern

Each persona should work independently before seeing other responses.

Response structure:

1. Summary of understanding
2. Strongest insight
3. Strongest criticism
4. Hidden assumptions
5. Rose (strength)
6. Bud (promising direction)
7. Thorn (risk)
8. Confidence (1-10)
9. Suggested experiment
10. Suggested theorem or mathematical direction
11. Suggested falsification test

Only after independent responses should a synthesis or voting phase occur.
Voting should be quadratic, where intensity of belief matters.
