## Four Reversal Quantities

The laboratory does not collapse these quantities.

| Quantity | Meaning | Rule 110 witness |
| --- | --- | --- |

| thermodynamic bound | `k_B T ln(2) H(X|Y)` | `2.1182e-21 J` average per update |

For the reversible witness, causal and graph costs are 1 while the
transition-map thermodynamic information-loss bound is zero. Therefore a
record trace does not require logical information loss.

## Complete 256-Rule Sweep

Width 5, three update layers, all initial states and all single-bit seed
interventions:

- 256 rules evaluated;
- 16 finite transition maps are injective;
- all 256 second-order lifts are injective;
- mean information loss is `1.057138 bits/update`;
- mean seed-trace survival fraction is `0.777832`;
- Pearson correlation between information loss and trace survival is
  `-0.723402`.

The correlation is substantial but not identity:

- Rules 1 and 128 have equal information loss but materially different trace
  survival.
- Rules 0 and 8 have equal trace survival but materially different
  information loss.

Logical information loss and record persistence are related properties of
the rule suite, but neither determines the other.

## Minimal Counterexamples

Search over rules and widths found:

1. **Width 1, reversible record without information loss.** A reversible
   second-order lift carries a one-cell counterfactual trace while its global
   transition map loses zero bits.
2. **Width 2, irreversible but inaccessible.** Rule 4 loses `0.5` bits per
   update under the uniform finite-state model. A seed leaves a global trace
   `10`, while the equal one-cell observer at cell 1 sees no record.
3. **Width 2, equal-access disagreement.** In the same Rule 4 witness, one
   one-cell observer sees the record and another does not.
4. **Profile collapse.** In a raw terminal binary trace, accessible support,
   spatial redundancy, and terminal intervention cost are the same Hamming
   count.

## Invariants And Divergences

### Survives Both Substrates

- local update induces a counterfactual sensitivity graph;
- seed influence can leave later record traces;
- bounded access makes record availability observer-relative;
- causal-step reachability orders influence without metric duration.

### Substrate-Specific

- injectivity and unique inversion;
- preimage multiplicity;
- information loss and the Landauer lower bound;
- persistence, extinction, and branching of traces.

### Does Not Emerge Automatically

- four independent D1 dimensions;
- semantic propositions or meaningful objects;
- an observer boundary;
- phenomenal temporal experience;
- metric time;
- computational irreducibility.
