# T160 Results: H7 Substrate-Family Screen

## Summary

The remaining H7 physical-arrow gate can now reject four broad substrate
families by default. Protected memories, driven dissipative records, exact
sector restrictions, and horizon-style inaccessibility do not reopen H7 unless
they survive the full post-T145/T148/T152/N8 absorber burden with a declared
`physical_record_deletion` reverse.

## Family Screen

| Family | Default absorber | Default verdict | Why null by default | Exception burden |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `protected_memory_family` | code-distance/syndrome/bath-reset accounting | `null_until_finite_control_impossibility` | Long lifetime, passive protection, or topological/code stability does not by itself show deletion impossibility; the proposal may still reduce to finite-rate decoding, reset, or reversible control once syndrome access and bath channels are restored. | Freeze code distance, syndrome access, bath/reset channels, allowed controls, and future-operation split; then show deletion remains impossible on a finite substrate. |
| `driven_dissipative_family` | pumping/bias/chemostat/free-energy accounting | `null_until_resource_matched` | Maintained order or directional current usually belongs to standard nonequilibrium or chemical-work bookkeeping, not an H7-specific deletion arrow. | Match pumping, fuel, sink/export, reservoir, and work variables; then show the deletion reverse remains impossible rather than merely costly or unsupported without drive. |
| `superselection_or_gauge_family` | ideal-sector restriction / exact symmetry ban | `null_until_finite_operational_reading` | Exact gauge or sector language often states an ideal restriction, not a finite substrate-native deletion impossibility. | Give a finite operational substrate, allowed controls, and reason the reverse is impossible without appealing only to exact ideal limits. |
| `horizon_or_access_family` | causal-access / observer-boundary accounting | `non_deletion_family` | Losing causal access to a record is not the same task as deleting the record. | Recast the proposal as actual physical deletion with matched access, sink, and control data, or else keep it outside H7. |

## Strongest Claim

H7's remaining physical-arrow route should be screened family-first, not
proposal-first. The default stance is negative:

```text
hard to erase, actively maintained, symmetry-protected, or inaccessible
records are not H7 evidence by default
```

until a proposal freezes its reverse-edge class, absorber vector, allowed
controls, and future-operation target and still shows finite substrate-native
constructor impossibility of physical deletion.

## What Improved

T160 converts the existing H7 demotion stack into a faster triage rule. Future
work can now reject broad substrate classes before spending time on detailed
toy models or prose around “practical irreversibility.”

## What Weakened

This weakens the remaining open-ended H7 intuition again. “Protected memory”
and related language no longer count as an interesting lead by themselves; they
are merely proposal labels until the frozen-accounting burden is met.

## Falsification Condition

T160 fails in H7's favor only if one of these substrate families supplies a
finite, physically typed example where:

- the reverse-edge class is genuinely `physical_record_deletion`;
- absorber variables are matched, including thermodynamic and information
  ledgers plus family-specific controls; and
- the reverse remains constructor-impossible for substrate-native reasons.

## H7 Update

Add T160 to H7: the most natural remaining substrate families are null by
default. Protected memories, driven dissipation, exact sector restrictions, and
horizon-style inaccessibility reopen H7 only with a finite matched-accounting
deletion witness.

## Claim Ledger Update

T160 sharpens H7's reinstatement gate from a single-substrate ask into a
family-level screen. Most natural “hard deletion” proposals are now demoted at
intake unless they declare `physical_record_deletion`, freeze the relevant
absorber vector, state the allowed controls, and still show finite
constructor-impossible deletion after full accounting.

## Open Blocker

The repo still lacks one named finite substrate with a deletion task that
survives both the generic stochastic-thermodynamic absorbers and the
family-specific absorber data.

## Suggested Next

Do not reopen H7 with generic stability or protection proposals. Either bring a
named finite substrate that clears the T160 intake screen, or spend effort on a
different route with live internal theorem leverage.
