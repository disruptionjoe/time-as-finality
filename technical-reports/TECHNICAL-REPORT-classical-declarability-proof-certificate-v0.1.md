## Setup

Let:

- `Omega = X_1 x ... x X_n` be a finite classical product configuration space.
- `R` be a subset of coordinates.
- `pi_R : Omega -> Omega_R` be the region projection.
- `A0` be the observable class generated by `pi_R`.
- `A1` include the full co-present finite code, equivalently the identity
  readout `id_Omega : Omega -> Omega`.
- `d : Omega -> V` be a total finite datum.

An `A0` boundary exists when there are `omega, omega' in Omega` such that:

```text
pi_R(omega) = pi_R(omega')
d(omega) != d(omega')
```

Equivalently, `d` does not factor through `pi_R`.

## Lemma

Every total finite classical datum `d : Omega -> V` is `A1`-measurable when
`A1` includes the full co-present code.

## Proof

Because `A1` includes `id_Omega`, every configuration is distinguishable at the
level of the full code. Define the finite lookup:

```text
L(id_Omega(omega)) = d(omega)
```

Then:

```text
d = L o id_Omega
```

So `d` factors through `A1`.

If `d` does not factor through `pi_R`, then `A0` is insufficient and the
boundary pair exists relative to the region. But the same datum still factors
through `A1`; the boundary is therefore declared relative to `A1`, not physical
relative to `A1`.

Thus no finite classical single-instance physical boundary exists under the
declared `A1` class. Family-level cost, asymptotic divergence, and hardness are
separate E1/E2 questions; quantum structural barriers are E3 questions and are
outside this proof.
