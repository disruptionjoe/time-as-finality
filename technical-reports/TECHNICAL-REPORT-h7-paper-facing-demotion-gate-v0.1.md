# Technical Report: H7 Paper-Facing Demotion Gate v0.1

## Claim Under Test

H7 has two separable readings:

```text
formal conditional reading:
  D1-monotone admissibility induces a finality direction

physical-arrow reading:
  finality structure itself supplies a physical or thermodynamic arrow
```

T148 asks whether the second reading is still allowed after T145.

## Result

It is not allowed.

H7 remains usable as a conditional constructor theorem and as a
resource/reverse-edge audit discipline. It should be demoted in paper-facing
text as a physical-arrow or thermodynamic-arrow claim.

## Current Strongest Claim

H7 is supported only as a conditional constructor/resource-accounting claim.
Current evidence blocks paper-facing physical-arrow language: closed
reversible, open stochastic, T1 record-graph, and fixed-accounting
physical-deletion screens all fail to produce a constructor-impossible
physical reverse.

## What This Improved

T148 converts the H7 status from a long chain of cautions into a reusable gate.
The project can still cite T18, but only with the admissibility/resource
conditions attached.

## What This Weakened

T148 weakens the public role of H7. It is no longer acceptable to present H7 as
partially supported evidence for a physical arrow just because the finite
constructor theorem exists.

## Falsification Condition

T148 fails in H7's favor if a physically typed record substrate clears the
fixed-accounting deletion gate:

1. matched free-energy / erased-bit floor;
2. matched capacity and sink/export state;
3. matched observer boundary and provenance state;
4. matched source-copy and reversible-control access;
5. a task-natural future-operation split; and
6. a physical deletion reverse that remains constructor-impossible after full
   accounting.
