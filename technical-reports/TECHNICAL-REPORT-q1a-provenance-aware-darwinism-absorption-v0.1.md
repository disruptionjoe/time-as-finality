# Technical Report: Q1A Provenance-Aware Darwinism Absorption v0.1

## Claim Under Test

T103 produced the strongest remaining internal Q1A witness. T104 asks whether
that witness still distinguishes itself from Quantum Darwinism once the
fragment partition is made provenance-aware instead of counting raw fragment
copies.

## Artifact

T104 reuses the T103 fixed-data witness family without changing the ordinary
quantum-side summaries:

- pointer basis;
- reduced coherence;
- fragment mutual-information profile;
- accessible raw redundancy;
- ordinary branch/history availability.

It then compares:

- raw accessible redundancy;
- provenance-aware redundancy over audited fragment classes;
- D1's independent accessible support and verdict.

## Result

The T103 witness is absorbed in the tested family.

```text
independent records      -> raw 3, provenance-aware 3, D1 finalized
copied archive duplicate -> raw 3, provenance-aware 2, D1 not_finalized
hidden partition         -> raw 3, provenance-aware withhold, D1 withhold
```

Once the provenance partition is shared, provenance-aware Quantum Darwinism and
D1 make the same verdict-critical distinction on these fixed-data cases.

## Current Strongest Claim

Q1A does not currently earn an external distinction from provenance-aware
Quantum Darwinism. Its surviving content is a discipline:

```text
do not treat raw fragment redundancy as evidence until the fragment partition
is physically audited.
```

That may be useful, but it is not new measurement dynamics or a new
interpretation on the present evidence.

## What This Improved

T104 turns the main external-collapse warning from T103 into an executable
kill test. The repo can now say exactly what survives and what does not:
current Q1A fixed-data support survives only as provenance-disciplined
redundancy accounting.

## What This Weakened Or Falsified

This weakens the remaining Q1A novelty claim. The tested witness no longer
supports even a narrow external distinction from Quantum Darwinism once both
sides share the same audited fragment partition.

## Falsification Condition

Q1A regains external distinctness only if a future fixed-data witness keeps the
same provenance-aware fragment partition and still forces a D1 verdict that
partitioned Quantum Darwinism cannot match, or if D1 derives the partition from
a genuinely extra physical rule rather than importing ordinary provenance
bookkeeping.
