# T101: Q1 Branch Adjudication

## Target Claims

Q1, D1, T2, T21, T22, T62, T66-T100.

## Setup

Classify the current Q1 evidence into four branch families:

- access-boundary record accounting;
- detector provenance admissibility;
- weak-measurement discriminator;
- contextuality/no-signalling guardrail.

Then ask whether the Q1 umbrella still deserves a single `partially_supported`
claim status in theorem-facing or paper-facing language.

## Success Criteria

- Each Q1 branch has an explicit current status.
- Each branch has a kill condition and reinstatement condition.
- The detector branch is treated as protocol admissibility, not empirical
  detector support.
- Weak measurement is reinstatement-only until a real platform supplies an
  independent pre-registered axis.
- Contextuality is kept as guardrail/literature discipline unless it names a
  nonredundant theorem target.

## Failure Criteria

- Q1 is allowed to remain one broad physics claim despite branch-specific
  evidence standards.
- Any branch is upgraded to new measurement dynamics without a new physical
  observable or real deployment evidence.
- Detector governance artifacts are counted as empirical support.
- Weak measurement is reopened without first naming the independent axis.

## Known Physics Constraints

- Preserve no-signalling.
- Do not treat decoherence as a complete measurement solution.
- Do not claim collapse, a Born-rule derivation, or a hidden-variable repair.
- Compare access-boundary content against decoherence, Quantum Darwinism,
  consistent histories, relational quantum mechanics, QBism, and many-worlds
  before calling it a physics discriminator.

## Contribution Needed

Split Q1 into explicit subclaims or demote it to a roadmap umbrella before any
paper-facing summary treats it as one partially supported conjecture.
