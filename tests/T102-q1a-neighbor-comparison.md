# T102: Q1A Neighbor Comparison Gate

## Target Claims

Q1A, Q1, T2, T22, T62, T64, T101.

## Setup

Compare the surviving Q1A branch against the nearest quantum-foundations
neighbors already named in the repo:

- decoherence;
- Quantum Darwinism;
- consistent histories;
- relational quantum mechanics;
- QBism;
- many-worlds / Everett.

For each neighbor, state:

- the shared content;
- the candidate TaF delta;
- the current verdict;
- the collapse condition.

Then define one gate for external use: what exact witness would be required
before Q1A can be presented as a distinction rather than a redescription.

## Success Criteria

- The comparison includes all six named neighbors.
- The current strongest Q1A claim is weakened to access-boundary and
  independence accounting unless a stricter gate is cleared.
- The gate requires standard quantum-side summaries to remain fixed while the
  D1 verdict changes.
- The result openly states that no current neighbor comparison yet earns a
  paper-facing distinction.

## Failure Criteria

- Q1A is still described as a distinct measurement theory or interpretation
  without a direct neighbor gate.
- The comparison hides behind vague "related work" language rather than
  naming collapse conditions.
- Any claimed distinction relies on changing the ordinary decoherence,
  redundancy, or branch/history summaries at the same time.

## Known Physics Constraints

- Do not claim collapse, a Born-rule derivation, or hidden variables.
- Preserve no-signalling and the current T101 branch split.
- Treat the current T2/T22/T62/T64 witnesses as partial only unless they hold
  fixed enough standard-neighbor summaries to force a clean distinction.

## Contribution Needed

Build one finite witness where decoherence, accessible raw redundancy, and
ordinary branch/history availability remain fixed while only the access cut or
independence partition changes the D1 verdict.
