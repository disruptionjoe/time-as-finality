# T104: Q1A Provenance-Aware Darwinism Absorption

## Route

Quantum measurement / classical records.

## Question

Does the surviving T103 fixed-data Q1A witness still distinguish itself from
Quantum Darwinism once the fragment partition is made provenance-aware?

## Motivation

T103 produced the strongest remaining internal Q1A witness, but it also named
its main collapse condition: if neighboring frameworks can adopt the same
provenance/independence partition without changing their physics, then Q1A is
bookkeeping rather than a new measurement-theory contribution.

T104 tests the nearest absorption threat directly instead of leaving it as a
future warning.

## Success Criteria

- Reuse the T103 fixed-data witness family rather than changing the
  quantum-side summaries.
- Compare three quantities for each fixed-data case:
  `raw_accessible_redundancy`, `provenance_aware_redundancy`, and the D1
  finality verdict.
- Show whether provenance-aware fragment partitioning reproduces the same
  verdict-critical distinction as D1.
- State clearly whether any surviving delta is physics, bookkeeping, or an
  admissibility rule.

## Failure Criteria

- The audit changes decoherence, fragment-information, raw redundancy, or
  branch/history availability while claiming absorption or non-absorption.
- It treats raw redundancy alone as provenance-aware redundancy.
- It advertises the result as new collapse dynamics, Born-rule content, or
  empirical quantum support.

## Claim Impact

If provenance-aware fragment partitioning reproduces the T103 verdicts, Q1A
should be weakened to a provenance-discipline layer over already formed
records. The remaining live question would then be whether any D1 dimension
other than partitioned redundancy produces a non-absorbed witness.

python -m unittest tests.test_q1a_provenance_absorption -v
