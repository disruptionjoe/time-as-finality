# T105: Q1A Accessible-Class Sufficiency

## Route

Quantum measurement / classical records.

## Question

After T104 absorbs the strongest fixed-data witness into provenance-aware
Quantum Darwinism, what verdict structure still survives in the current Q1A
branch?

## Motivation

The highest-value move is no longer to pile on more internal witnesses. It is
to determine whether the surviving fixed-data branch still contains any
nontrivial physical structure, or whether it collapses to a thresholded
classifier over audited accessible records.

That reduction matters because it sharply constrains what a future serious Q1A
upgrade would have to beat.

## Success Criteria

- Enumerate the current fixed-data witness family over accessible fragment cuts
  and provenance partitions.
- Keep the ordinary quantum-side summaries fixed while varying only access and
  partition structure.
- Test whether the D1 verdict is completely determined by the audited
  accessible provenance-class count.
- Show that raw accessible redundancy alone is not sufficient.
- Include a hidden-partition control where verdicts are withheld rather than
  inferred.

## Failure Criteria

- The audit quietly changes coherence, fragment-information summaries, or
  branch/history availability while claiming a reduction.
- It treats raw accessible fragment count as sufficient despite copied-record
  counterexamples.
- It advertises the result as new measurement dynamics, collapse content,
  Born-rule content, or empirical support.

## Claim Impact

If the reduction holds, the current Q1A branch should be described even more
narrowly: not merely bookkeeping over partitions, but a thresholded
record-accounting classifier over audited accessible provenance support.

python -m unittest tests.test_q1a_accessible_class_sufficiency -v
