# T107: Loss Relocation

## Route

Mathematical machinery / adoption elsewhere.

## Question

Can the intuition "lost structure reappears as constraint, debt, provenance,
obstruction, incentive, or geometry" be made precise without claiming a false
conservation law?

## Motivation

The strongest version of the new idea is not that all lost information is
conserved. It is that an information-losing projection can leave target-side
judgments dependent on source lifts. When that happens, the forgotten structure
has not vanished operationally; it has relocated into a reconstruction debt,
constraint surface, or admissibility obligation.

This directly extends the LossKernel program after T99. T99 showed that
label-only loss is too weak. T107 asks whether source-anchored witness
obligations can be derived from finite source fibers rather than declared as
metadata.

## Success Criteria

- Build finite projections with explicit source states, target states, and
  source-level judgment rules.
- Derive relocation classes from the preimage fiber of a target judgment.
- Produce at least one reconstruction-debt case with mixed source-lift
  verdicts.
- Produce at least one stable constraint-surface case with uniformly forbidden
  lifts.
- Produce at least one absorbed-freedom case showing why conservation language
  is too strong.
- Connect the result to the GU six-axis protocol only as a bridge discipline,
  not as imported physics.

## Failure Criteria

- The result merely attaches new labels to lost structure.
- Every case is forced to produce debt, hiding absorbed-loss controls.
- The model claims physical conservation, new quantum dynamics, or a general
  theorem beyond finite fixtures.
- The six-axis bridge is treated as evidence rather than a specification
  protocol.

## Claim Impact

If successful, T107 gives TF1 a sharper candidate semantics:

```text
Loss relocates when projected target judgments remain source-lift dependent.
```

This is still not prior-art separation from provenance, lenses, abstract
interpretation, or CSP explanations. It is a better object to compare against
them.
