# T11: Compositional Finality

## Target Claims

- [C2: Typed Compositional Finality](../claims/C2-typed-compositional-finality.md)
- [D1: Physical Finality Definition](../claims/D1-physical-finality-definition.md)
- [D2: Observer As Record-Bearing System](../claims/D2-observer-as-record-bearing-system.md)

## Setup

Represent record systems as recursively nested token bundles. Merge underlying
evidence by provenance-preserving union, then separately apply inherited
expression marks, observer access, finality profiling, and decision thresholds.

Compare transparent merge with lossy and provenance-preserving checkpoints.

## Success Criteria

- The evidence merge satisfies associative, commutative, and idempotent laws.
- The experiment states which later stages preserve or break those laws.
- Duplicate evidence cannot increase provenance support.
- Recursion works without a fixed semantic layer count.
- The epigenetic lens produces a testable difference beyond generic nesting.
- Minimal conflict, coarse-graining, and local-to-global counterexamples are
  reported.

## Failure Criteria

- A semilattice law is assumed for profiles because it holds for evidence.
- Coarse-graining is treated as neutral when it discards holder or branch data.
- "Epigenetic" is used without inherited context and reprogrammable expression.
- Recursive depth is hard-coded as a claim about the world.
- Local consistency is mistaken for global consistency.
- Stable agreement is mistaken for truth.

## Result

Status: **implemented; evidence composes, finality does not compose
universally**.

Across all 225 ordered pairs of the 15 non-empty states generated by four
independent supporting sources:

- transparent evidence merge was monotone in `100%` of pairs;
- the resolved value survived in `100%` of conflict-free pairs;
- the merged profile equaled the componentwise least upper bound in only
  `51.11%` of pairs.

Provenance checkpoints preserved source support in `100%` of states but
preserved holder and branch dimensions in only `26.67%`. Lossy checkpoints
preserved source support in only `26.67%`.

The implementation also found six minimal counterexamples, including
conflicting finalized records becoming unresolved and locally consistent
assignments failing to glue globally.

Inherited expression marks preserved stored record identity while changing
visible support from `1` to `0` and back to `1` after local reprogramming.
Neutral recursive grouping was invariant in all 500 trials; mark placement
changed expression in all 500 targeted comparisons.
