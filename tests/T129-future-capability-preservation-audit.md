# T129: Future Capability Preservation Audit

## Route

Mathematical machinery / adoption elsewhere, with detector, provenance,
maintenance, and reconstruction-debt pressure.

## Question

Are the repeated witnesses of

```text
same current state != same future capability
```

instances of one deeper object, a framing artifact, a repackaging of existing
theory, or a useful cross-domain audit pattern?

## Required Branch Review

Review:

- detector packet witness;
- Git history witness;
- reconstruction debt;
- provenance work;
- operation-right discussions;
- ASP;
- FOA;
- LossKernel;
- admissibility;
- maintenance-cost investigations.

For each, record the state held fixed, future capability changed, hidden
structure causing the difference, and whether it admits a uniform
representation.

## Candidate Explanations

Pressure-test witness availability, operation rights, reconstruction paths,
provenance structure, maintenance capacity, and access boundaries.

## Prior-Art Pressure

Assume absorption until separation is demonstrated against reachability
analysis, viability kernels, opportunity-set economics, capability theory,
mechanism design, affordance theory, active inference policy spaces,
reinforcement-learning action spaces, provenance systems, access-control
systems, and distributed-systems capability models.

## Success Criteria

- Produce a finite witness table across all required branches.
- Identify the smallest common structure if one exists.
- Identify strongest separation and absorption witnesses.
- Recommend reject, preserve, formalize, or promote.
- Do not create a new primitive unless forced.

## Claim Impact

No core claim upgrade. The expected useful result is a conservative audit
normal form, not a new ontology.
