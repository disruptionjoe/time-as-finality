# T135: Weak-Measurement Second-Meter Checkpoint

## Route

Quantum measurement / classical records.

## Question

Do the most relevant screened 2020-2025 calorimetric and thermal-detector
papers actually produce the simultaneous second-meter witness required to
reopen Q1C?

## Motivation

T130 already showed that the obvious calorimetric rescue did not clear the
gate. But the repo still needed one tighter checkpoint against newer-looking
literature so that "second meter" would not remain a vague future hope.

## Success Criteria

- A screened platform supplies a standard weak-measurement record and a
  simultaneous independent physical meter.
- The source names a pre-registered comparison where the ordinary monitored
  statistics are held fixed.
- The second meter alone changes the admissible TaF verdict.

## Failure Criteria

- The thermal or calorimetric channel replaces the standard readout chain.
- The calorimetric record is itself the ordinary trajectory record.
- The formalism changes the measurement task instead of giving a monitored-qubit
  same-statistics dual-meter witness.

## Claim Impact

Q1C remains dormant unless one concrete simultaneous fixed-statistics
dual-meter witness is found. Replacement readout and calorimeter-assisted
measurement formalisms do not count by themselves.

## Contribution Needed

Either find a real simultaneous monitored-qubit dual-meter protocol, or write
the stronger obstruction note that standard weak-measurement hardware is
unlikely to furnish one.
