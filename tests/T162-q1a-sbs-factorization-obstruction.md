# T162: Q1A SBS Factorization Obstruction

## Route

Quantum measurement / classical records.

## Question

After the N10 Spectrum Broadcast Structure absorber, can the current Q1A
already-formed-record verdict family still produce a same-SBS-data verdict
split?

## Motivation

N10 says SBS and strong Quantum Darwinism already formalize independently
accessible, mutually agreeing redundant records of a pointer observable. T147
says the current Q1A verdict family factors through partition visibility plus
audited accessible provenance-support count.

The highest-value move is therefore a negative one: turn the N10 absorber into
an executable obstruction and stop future runs from looking for a Q1A upgrade
inside the same closed bookkeeping family.

## Success Criteria

- Enumerate finite SBS-style objectivity cases with pointer agreement,
  observer-accessible fragments, provenance partitions, and strong-independence
  controls.
- Verify that the current D1 verdict factors through an SBS-importable closure
  key.
- Preserve the old Q1A guardrail that raw fragment count is not sufficient.
- Reject objectivity-failure controls and hidden-partition controls.
- State the exact condition needed to falsify the obstruction.

## Failure Criteria

- The result is presented as a no-go theorem for quantum measurement.
- The SBS absorber is treated as a TaF upgrade.
- Raw redundancy is allowed to replace audited support.
- A hidden partition is scored as finalized.

## Claim Impact

Q1A remains `bookkeeping_only`. In the current family, fixing SBS/strong-QD
objectivity data and the imported access/provenance support key fixes the D1
verdict. A future Q1A witness must either split the D1 verdict at the same SBS
closure key through a predeclared physical dimension, or derive the
partition/access rule from physical structure that SBS or strong Quantum
Darwinism cannot import for free.
