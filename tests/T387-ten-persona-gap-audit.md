# T387: Ten-Persona Gap Audit

## Target Claims

D1, R1, S1, PO1, and the T376-T386 relativity-substrate ladder:

```text
After deriving a bidirectional handshake conditionally from mutual local
attestability, are we missing any important gaps before pushing the claim
harder?
```

## Setup

Audit the chain through ten lenses:

- mathematical formalist,
- relativity physicist,
- distributed systems engineer,
- category/sheaf theorist,
- information theorist,
- gauge/coordinate auditor,
- adversarial/security reviewer,
- minimality/model-selection auditor,
- experimental/falsification designer,
- paper reviewer.

## Success Criteria

- Record exactly ten persona lenses.
- Identify whether any missing objects remain.
- Distinguish known open gaps from new or sharper gaps.
- Refuse a claim upgrade if the result is still conditional.
- Recommend the next highest-leverage goal.

## Failure Criteria

- Treat persona agreement as proof.
- Hide the conditional nature of T386.
- Collapse protocol mutuality into null geometry.
- Ignore security, gauge, higher-dimensional, or catalog-completeness risks.

## Known Constraints

This is an audit artifact, not a new derivation. Its job is prioritization and
gap discovery.

## Contribution Needed

Use the audit to select the next goal rather than upgrade the theorem claim.
