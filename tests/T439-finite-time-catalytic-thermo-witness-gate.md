# T439: Finite-Time Catalytic Thermodynamic Witness Gate

## Target Claims

- H7 physical-deletion / thermodynamic-arrow reinstatement gate.
- E1 thermodynamic-resource route in the adopted capability-boundary taxonomy.
- Method guideline action: thermodynamic resource witness with finite-time or
  catalytic constraints.

## Setup

T142 already separates reversible uncopy from blind Landauer-style reset. The N8
absorber map then requires future H7 work to grant stochastic-thermodynamic and
information-accounting variables before treating deletion, finite barriers,
feedback, catalysts, or nonequilibrium currents as finality evidence.

T439 turns that remaining burden into an executable admission gate. It evaluates
candidate thermodynamic-resource packets for a finite-time or catalytic
record-deletion witness. Admission is not H7 promotion and not a thermodynamic
arrow derivation; it only marks a packet shape as worth future review.

## Success Criteria

- Import the T142 guardrail: correlated uncopy has zero blind-erasure floor;
  blind reset pays the one-bit Landauer-style floor.
- Reject reversible uncopy, blind reset, finite kinetic barriers,
  nonequilibrium current-only packets, feedback packets without controller-memory
  ledgers, hidden sinks/export, untyped resource units, consumed or hidden
  catalysts, and task splits that vanish after ledgers are matched.
- Route exact ideal-limit or sector-lock packets to a separate E1/E3/idealization
  spec instead of admitting them as finite-time thermodynamic-resource witnesses.
- Admit only a predeclared full-accounting finite-time/catalytic packet as a
  future target, with no H7 promotion and no claim-ledger movement.

## Failure Criteria

The artifact fails if it:

- treats Landauer cost, finite barriers, or nonequilibrium currents as H7 proof;
- admits a packet with hidden catalyst, sink, source copy, controller memory, or
  reversible-control access;
- treats a changed catalyst state as matched thermodynamic accounting;
- claims a thermodynamic-arrow theorem, physics result, public posture, or claim
  promotion;
- edits the claim ledger, North Star, roadmap, or hard policy.

## Known Physics Constraints

This is a finite admission gate and synthetic positive-control shape. It does not
name or validate a real physical substrate, prove a stochastic-thermodynamic
bound, prove a catalytic resource theorem, or derive a physical arrow of time.

## Contribution Needed

A future positive H7 thermodynamic packet must bring a named record substrate,
finite-time protocol, fixed stochastic-thermodynamic ledgers, fixed information
ledgers, catalyst return policy, source-copy and reversible-control accounting,
and a task-natural future-operation split that remains after all absorber fields
are matched.
