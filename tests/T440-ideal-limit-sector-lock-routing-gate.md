# T440: Ideal-Limit Sector-Lock Routing Gate

## Target Claims

- H7 physical-deletion / thermodynamic-arrow reinstatement gate.
- E1 asymptotic/limit and E3 structural-symmetry modes in the adopted
  capability-boundary taxonomy.
- T439 follow-up route for exact ideal-limit or sector-lock packets.

## Setup

T439 rejects finite-time and catalytic thermodynamic packets that rely on
ordinary accounting gaps, then routes exact ideal-limit and sector-lock packets
to a separate E1/E3/idealization spec.

T440 implements that separate spec. It does not decide whether any real E1 or
E3 theorem exists. It only classifies packet shapes before they can be used as
evidence:

- E1 review requires a family-level limit packet with finite approximants, a
  predeclared limit invariant, and diverging recovery cost or nonlocality.
- E3 review requires an exact sector/symmetry no-go on physical states that
  survives absorber matching and A2 resource/reference-lift audit.
- H7-null idealizations include infinite barriers, exact sector axioms with no
  finite operational substrate, gauge relabeling, and finite control/kinetic
  restrictions.

## Success Criteria

- Reject or null-route infinite barriers, exact local sector bans, gauge
  relabelings, post-hoc idealization selectors, finite barriers, finite
  enforcement/leakage paths, and changed resource boundaries.
- Route compensating-reservoir and reference-frame completions to E0/resource
  completion rather than E3 or H7.
- Admit only a predeclared family-level packet as future E1 review.
- Admit only a predeclared exact-no-go packet that survives A2 resource-lift
  audit as future E3 review.
- Keep all admissions recorded-tier and synthetic-control-only.

## Failure Criteria

The artifact fails if it:

- treats an infinite barrier or exact sector axiom as H7 evidence;
- treats gauge representative change as physical record deletion;
- lets a single-instance packet pass as E1;
- lets an A1-relative obstruction pass as E3 without A2 resource-lift audit;
- treats a synthetic control as a real WAY theorem or quantum physics claim;
- edits the claim ledger, North Star, roadmap, or hard policy.

## Known Physics Constraints

This is a finite routing classifier and synthetic-control shape. It does not
prove a limit theorem, a stochastic-thermodynamic theorem, a resource-theory
theorem, a WAY theorem, or a physical arrow of time.

## Contribution Needed

Future E1 work must supply a family-level limit packet. Future E3 work must
supply an independently typed exact no-go witness that remains after A2
resource/reference-lift audit. Future H7 work must not cite ideal barriers,
sector axioms, or gauge relabelings unless the finite physical-deletion burden
is also frozen and survives the existing absorber stack.
