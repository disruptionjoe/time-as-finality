# T445: Region Capability Substrate-Law Big Swing

## Target Claims

- [Region-Indexed Capability Discriminator](../open-problems/region-indexed-capability-discriminator.md)
- T434 substrate-law admission gate
- T406 transition-system operation-unavailability gate
- T436 resource-lift guardrail posture

## Setup

Build a finite Z2 charge-conservation packet. Region `R` sees only the final
record payload, local charge sector, local provenance/control fields, and the
declared `R` operation menu. The main pair differs only in an outside-boundary
compensator sector:

```text
left:  admitted boundary compensator charge = 1
right: admitted boundary compensator charge = 0
```

The revision task has charge delta `1`. The predeclared substrate law is:

```text
revise_final_verdict is admitted iff
  revision_charge_delta + admitted_compensator_charge = 0 mod 2
```

The artifact checks that all `R`-supported observations and interventions match,
then asks whether the boundary menu changes revision capability without merely
restating the transition table.

## Success Criteria

- The main pair has identical `R` visible projections, observational statistics,
  and all tested `R`-supported intervention signatures.
- The `R`-only menu does not split revision capability.
- The boundary menu splits `can_revise_final_verdict`.
- T434 admits the main packet as a predeclared conservation-law-forced transition
  candidate.
- A transition-table restatement, post-hoc law, and hidden-label law are rejected.
- The model records whether explicit law-sector completion absorbs the result.
- The result remains recorded-tier with no claim-ledger, roadmap, public posture,
  North Star, or shared-context movement.

## Failure Criteria

The artifact fails if:

- `R`-supported statistics or interventions distinguish the main pair;
- the boundary split is caused by a hidden label, post-hoc selector, changed
  accounting field, or transition-table restatement;
- T434 does not admit the main law packet;
- a transition-table restatement is admitted;
- law-sector completion fails to restore factorization but the artifact still
  refuses to flag that as a stronger review burden;
- the artifact claims a real substrate law, physics theorem, WAY theorem,
  region-indexed discriminator success, claim promotion, or public posture move.

## Known Physics Constraints

This is a finite substrate-law pressure test. The Z2 rule is an executable law
packet for admission testing, not evidence for a real physical conservation law.
It does not prove WAY, does not supply a quantum E3 result, does not revive a
GU/TaF adapter, and does not promote any claim.

## Contribution Needed

Use T445 as a calibration result: the post-T434 burden can be cleared without
transition-table underdescription, but a stronger future packet must explain why
law-sector completion or reference-resource lift is physically non-admissible
rather than merely outside `R`.
