## Setup

T455 preserves T454 only as a finite integrated review target. The mechanical
packet survives, but stronger Direction-A posture remains blocked because:

- law-sector description still explains the boundary resource;
- cyclic, consumed, and ideal reference policies route away from the finite
  exact packet;
- no policy-independent region theorem is supplied beyond the integration.

T456 turns those blockers into an executable admission gate for any future
stronger region packet.

## Success Criteria

- Current T454/T455 is rejected for stronger posture while preserving its
  recorded-tier review-target standing.
- The gate requires any future stronger packet to supply:
  - T455-style mechanical survival;
  - an exact witness tied to the named completion;
  - description nonfactorization for the boundary resource;
  - reference-policy invariance or independent preclusion for cyclic, consumed,
    and ideal reference variants;
  - a policy-independent region theorem beyond packet integration;
  - frozen operation/resource class;
  - A2 reference/resource audit;
  - nearby negative control.
- Bare exact no-go, description-completion, reference-policy-fragile, and
  integration-without-theorem controls are rejected.
- Only a synthetic future theorem target is admitted, with no claim promotion.

## Failure Criteria

The gate fails if it:

- treats T454/T455 as a region-indexed discriminator success;
- treats the synthetic future target as a theorem already earned;
- ignores the description-completion absorber;
- ignores cyclic, consumed, or ideal reference controls;
- edits `CLAIM-LEDGER.md`, `TESTS.md`, `ROADMAP.md`, README, North Star files,
  public posture, hard policy, or cross-repo truth.

## Known Physics Constraints

T456 is a repo-local admission gate over finite toy packets. It is not a WAY
theorem, not a quantum physics claim, not a real substrate law, not cross-repo
support, and not claim-ledger movement.
