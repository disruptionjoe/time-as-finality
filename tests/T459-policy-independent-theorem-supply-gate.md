## Setup

T456 rejects current T454/T455 for stronger Direction-A posture because the
packet misses description nonfactorization, reference-policy invariance or
preclusion, and a policy-independent region theorem. T457 sharpens the
description blocker. T458 sharpens the reference-policy blocker. T459 focuses
on the remaining theorem-supply blocker.

The current integrated E3-region route cannot move by treating packet
integration itself as a theorem. A stronger packet must supply a theorem
declared before pair selection that is independent of the resource/reference
policy being screened, targets the named completion that would otherwise
restore factorization, makes that completion physically non-admissible, and
clears the T457 and T458 blockers together.

The gate fails if it:

- treats current T454/T455/T457/T458 as admitted for stronger Direction-A
  posture;
- treats packet integration as a policy-independent theorem;
- ignores T457 description completion or T458 reference-policy fragility;
- demotes a claim, changes the claim ledger, changes public posture, or edits
  hard policy;
- edits `CLAIM-LEDGER.md`, `TESTS.md`, `ROADMAP.md`, README, North Star files,
  public posture, hard policy, or cross-repo truth.

## Known Physics Constraints

is not a WAY theorem, not a quantum physics claim, not a real substrate law, not
cross-repo support, and not claim-ledger movement.
