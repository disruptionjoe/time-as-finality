# T461: E1 Local-Recovery Family Audition

## Target Claims

- E1 asymptotic / limit mode in the adopted capability-boundary taxonomy.
- H7 physical-deletion / thermodynamic-arrow reinstatement guardrails.
- T441 E1 family-limit packet gate.

## Setup

T441 admits only family-level E1 packets with finite approximants, a declared
approximant-to-limit map, stable task and operation class, fixed observer and
resource accounting, a predeclared finite-auditable invariant, convergence /
error controls, a negative control, and a recovery cost or nonlocality quantity
that diverges across the family.

T461 audits a concrete local-recovery control against that gate. In the main
finite family, the observer/control region is one endpoint of a path graph and
the record is the other endpoint at distance `n`. With nearest-neighbor local
operations of radius 1, endpoint recovery requires depth `n`, so fixed-depth
recovery eventually fails while the declared finite approximants remain
auditable.

## Success Criteria

- The nearest-neighbor endpoint-recovery family clears T441's structural E1
  packet form.
- The finite approximant profiles record endpoint distance, minimum local
  recovery depth, fixed-depth recoverability, and stable local operation radius.
- Single-instance, finite-barrier, post-hoc selector, drifting-task, hidden-relay,
  and E2-labeled controls fail or route through T441.
- An overread that treats the same local-depth family as physical record deletion
  is kept out of H7 unless it passes a separate physical-deletion absorber audit.
- No claim promotion, H7 reopening, E1 theorem, physics result, public posture,
  hard-policy movement, or cross-repo truth movement follows.

## Failure Criteria

The artifact fails if it:

- treats ordinary endpoint distance or local operation radius as H7 evidence;
- claims a limit theorem from finite approximants alone;
- lets a single finite barrier, post-hoc selector, hidden resource, or drifting
  task pass as an E1 packet;
- edits `CLAIM-LEDGER.md`, `TESTS.md`, `ROADMAP.md`, README, North Star files,
  public posture, hard policy, or cross-repo truth.

## Known Physics Constraints

T461 is a finite local-recovery control and admission audit. It is not a
thermodynamic theorem, not a stochastic-thermodynamic theorem, not a physical
record-deletion substrate, not H7 support, and not a physics claim.
