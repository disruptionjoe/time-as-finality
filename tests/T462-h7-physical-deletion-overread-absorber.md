# T462: H7 Physical-Deletion Overread Absorber

## Target Claims

- H7 physical-deletion / thermodynamic-arrow reinstatement guardrails.
- T145 physical-record-deletion fixed-accounting screen.
- T160 H7 substrate-family screen.
- T168 H7 sector-restriction screen.

## Setup

T461 built a concrete E1 local-recovery family: endpoint recovery depth grows
with endpoint distance under a fixed nearest-neighbor operation class. T461 is
a useful locality control, but it explicitly does not reopen H7 because the
split factors through ordinary locality data.

T462 converts the overread warning into an executable absorber audit. A packet
cannot count even as an H7 review target unless it freezes all of the following:

- reverse edge typed as `physical_record_deletion`;
- named physical substrate and frozen record encoding;
- write, delete, and reverse task frozen before comparison;
- task-natural future-operation split;
- full H7 absorber vector declared and matched;
- substrate-specific absorber data;
- allowed control class;
- finite operational substrate;
- constructor impossibility after full accounting;
- surviving future-operation split after matching;
- negative controls against idealization, hidden sink/export, hidden copy,
  access loss, and control denial.

## Success Criteria

- T461-style locality-depth divergence is absorbed as locality accounting, not
  physical deletion.
- Access loss, provenance loss, support-copy removal, finite barriers, hidden
  sink/export, changed thermodynamic ledgers, hidden source copies, withheld
  reversible controls, ideal/sector locks, and gauge relabeling all fail or
  route to the existing absorber stack.
- A substrate-incomplete deletion claim fails before review.
- A full-accounting packet whose reverse remains control-possible fails.
- A packet whose future-operation split vanishes after matching is absorbed.
- Only the synthetic full-burden packet is admitted as a future H7 review
  target.
- No H7 promotion, physical-deletion evidence, thermodynamic-arrow theorem,
  E1/E3 theorem, physics claim, public posture, hard-policy movement, claim
  movement, or cross-repo truth movement follows.

## Failure Criteria

The artifact fails if it:

- treats T461 locality-depth divergence as H7 evidence;
- lets access/provenance/support-copy loss pass as physical record deletion;
- treats finite barriers, hidden ledgers, ideal locks, sector rules, or gauge
  relabelings as deletion impossibility;
- admits a packet without the full H7 absorber vector and allowed control class;
- changes `CLAIM-LEDGER.md`, `TESTS.md`, `ROADMAP.md`, README, North Star files,
  public posture, hard policy, or cross-repo truth.

## Known Physics Constraints

T462 is an internal absorber audit and routing guard. It is not a thermodynamic
theorem, not a stochastic-thermodynamic theorem, not a physical substrate
result, not an E1/E3 theorem, not H7 evidence, and not a physics claim.
