observer-shadow category thread be closed against minor restarts while still
leaving a narrow path for genuinely new direct-preservation or new-family
evidence?

T476 consumes the T475 executable result and routes nine post-T475 proposals:

- capability-identification restart;

## Success Criteria

T476 succeeds if it:

- treats T475 as metadata-only review support;
- rejects minor variants of the current T470-T475 packet class;
- rejects semantic, absorber-completion, capability-identification, and
  fibration-label shortcuts;
- blocks claim-status and public-posture movement;
- admits only synthetic future targets with either independent direct
  preservation plus formal semantics or a new packet family with its own index,
  bridge, review, positive-control, and hostile-control burden;
- keeps all admitted future targets as review targets, not category evidence;
- leaves claim status, public posture, North Star, roadmap, README, hard policy,
  and cross-repo truth untouched.

## Failure Criteria

T476 fails if it:

- converts T475 review metadata into category or fibration evidence;
- treats repeated route labels as a new bridge;
- uses absorber completion as category evidence;
- identifies PO1 admissibility and LossKernel obligations as one capability
  object;
- moves claim status, public posture, roadmap, README, North Star, hard policy,
  or cross-repo truth;
- claims an observer-shadow category, indexed-category, or fibration theorem.
