## Question

After T487 shows that the T485 quotient is sufficient only for declared
reachability-task capabilities, can the current RG/multiscale
reachability-quotient thread be closed against minor restarts while preserving
narrow future review paths for genuinely new evidence?

## Setup

T488 consumes the committed T487 result and routes post-T487 proposals:

- reachability-quotient upgrade;
- role-profile internal-scale restart;
- path-latency relabel restart;
- relay-budget clock restart;
- component-size capacity restart;
- record-finality bridge shortcut;
- RG/conformal import restart;

## Success Criteria

T488 succeeds if it:

- treats T487 as reachability-task sufficiency only;
- rejects minor variants of the current T485-T487 quotient packet class;
- rejects path-latency, relay-budget, and component-size restarts because T487
  makes those capability spreads non-singleton over quotient-visible fibers;
- blocks finality, scale, clock, duration, RG/conformal, physics, claim-status,
  and public-posture shortcuts;
- admits only synthetic future review targets with an independent capability
  object, a domain-native morphism class, or a direct record-finality bridge
  theorem, each with positive and hostile controls;
- keeps admitted future targets as review targets, not evidence;
- leaves claim status, public posture, North Star, roadmap, README, hard policy,
  protected license, physics posture, and cross-repo truth untouched.

## Failure Criteria

T488 fails if it:

- converts reachability-task sufficiency into internal scale structure;
- converts quotient role profile into scale, clock, duration, finality, or
  scale-genesis evidence;
- admits path-latency, relay-budget, or component-size readings as determined
  by the reachability quotient;
- imports RG beta-function, conformal-gravity, or fixed-point mechanism as a
  TaF proof mechanism;
- moves claim status, public posture, roadmap, README, North Star, hard policy,
  protected license, or cross-repo truth.

## Known Physics Constraints

not evaluate RG beta functions, conformal-gravity phenomenology, unitarity,
rotation curves, asymptotic freedom, quantum gravity, or physical clocks.
