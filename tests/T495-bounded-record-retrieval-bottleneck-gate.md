# T495: Bounded-Record Retrieval Bottleneck Gate

## Target Claims

Finality-as-computation exploration note; C(R)-style bounded-record capability
audit; no claim-ledger target.

## Setup

Use a finite binary stream space of length five. Compare three visible
projections:

- full history;
- committed last-2 state;
- committed parity state.

Ask whether each projection determines the declared capability object:

- arbitrary indexed retrieval over the whole stream;
- retained suffix retrieval;
- parity query.

## Success Criteria

- Full history factors arbitrary indexed retrieval.
- Last-2 committed state does not factor arbitrary indexed retrieval.
- Last-2 committed state does factor retained suffix retrieval.
- Parity committed state factors parity but not arbitrary indexed retrieval.
- The only admitted reading is a finite retention-axis capability probe.

## Failure Criteria

- A bounded committed state accidentally determines arbitrary retrieval in the
  finite fixture.
- Positive controls fail for their declared native tasks.
- The gate is used to revive the naive attention/quantum copyability mapping.
- The gate is used as a physics mechanism, Standard Model, decoherence,
  complexity-theorem, claim-ledger, public-posture, or cross-repo result.

## Known Physics Constraints

This is not a physics result. It does not say unknown quantum states are
copyable, does not derive decoherence, and does not assert that physics is
literally an SSM/Transformer architecture.
