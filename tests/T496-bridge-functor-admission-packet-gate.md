# T496: Bridge-Functor Admission Packet Gate

## Target Claims

All-persona missing-analysis synthesis; bridge/functor restart admission
discipline; no claim-ledger target.

## Setup

Encode a finite set of candidate future packets against the packet form named
by the all-persona synthesis and the committed Bridge-Functor packet
report/template:

- native capability object, task family, observer/access boundary, completion
  rights, and visible projection;
- declared mature absorber checks;
- a domain-native law or theorem source;
- capability-spread evidence and positive controls;
- minimality condition and demotion path;
- closed-lineage citations plus required new keys for closed routes.

The gate includes current closed-route requirements for S1 finite colimit,
RG/reachability quotient, valid coarse-graining, typed-gap bridge, and
bounded-record retrieval surfaces.

## Success Criteria

- Complete typed packets are admitted only as review targets.
- Closed routes require their closing tests and one of their named new keys.
- Analogy-only packets without a domain-native law or theorem source are
  rejected.
- Packets missing `Cap`, tested absorbers, capability spread, positive
  controls, minimality, or demotion path are rejected.
- Claim, public-posture, external-publication, and cross-repo shortcuts are
  blocked.

## Failure Criteria

- Admission is treated as theorem, physics support, claim movement, public
  posture, hard policy, or cross-repo truth.
- A closed route can be reopened by a minor variant without a named new key.
- Analogy, synthesis, or vocabulary overlap substitutes for capability-spread
  evidence.
- Mature absorber checks can remain declared-only.

## Known Physics Constraints

This is not a physics result. It is a repo-local method gate for future typed
packets. Any physics-facing packet must still derive `Cap` from known physics,
declare access and allowed operations, and pass gauge/relabeling/completion
absorbers before earning anything stronger than review.

## Contribution Needed

Use this gate before reopening closed S1, RG/reachability, valid
coarse-graining, typed-gap, or bounded-retrieval routes. Executing an admitted
packet remains separate work.
