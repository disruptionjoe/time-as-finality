# T507: Finality Record-Redundancy Double Gate

## Target Claims

No claim-ledger target. This is a TaF-native finality discriminator gate for
the unnumbered finality records-vs-redundancy and ghost-parity physicality
probes.

## Setup

The finality discriminator reduced the mirror-sector record reading to an
admissible-operation question: a W- difference is redundancy when every
admissible physical operation preserves the W+/W- split, and is record-like
only if the declared operation algebra can recover W- into W+.

The ghost-parity physicality probe then added a second gate. Even if the mirror
sector is retained as physical through a positive full-space inner product, a
W+-restricted observer sees normalized W+ statistics differently under the
full-space Born rule. The mirror remains hidden only under the
self-normalized/projector Born convention.

T507 makes the paired burden executable on the same finite carrier:

```text
operation algebra gate:
  positivity-preserving / individual algebra -> redundancy
  full-Krein collective algebra -> recoverable mirror difference

normalization gate:
  full-space Born -> mirror leakage, not hidden
  self-normalized/projector Born -> hidden to W+ observer
```

## Success Criteria

- The standard positivity-preserving algebra recovers no W- difference and is
  recorded as redundancy.
- The full-Krein collective algebra recovers the W- difference.
- Full-space Born normalization leaks mirror weight into W+-restricted
  normalized statistics.
- Self-normalized/projector Born observation hides the W- difference from the
  same W+ observables.
- The hidden-record reading is admitted only as review material in the
  double-special corner: full-Krein retention plus self-normalized observation.
- Degenerate no-mirror-spread, untyped BRST assertion, claim/public-posture,
  external-publication, and cross-repo shortcuts are rejected or blocked.

## Failure Criteria

- Positivity-preserving operations recover the W- difference.
- Full-Krein collective operations cannot recover the W- difference.
- Full-space Born normalization does not leak mirror weight.
- Self-normalized observation does not hide the W- difference.
- The gate is used to assert BRST exactness, Krein quantization, hidden-record
  physics, source-action truth, mass-gap evidence, public posture, or cross-repo
  truth.

## Known Physics Constraints

This is a finite TaF review gate, not a physics result. It does not decide
whether the mirror sector is BRST-exact, BRST-nontrivial, physically retained,
dynamically realizable, or tied to any source action. Those require a typed
constraint/gauge structure supplied by the owning physics surface or a future
repo-local packet that names the external source as evidence rather than
instruction.
