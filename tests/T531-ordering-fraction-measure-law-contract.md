## Central Question

What must be true before a future S1-facing ordering-fraction measure-law packet
is allowed to execute as a review target, without screen-conditioning,
Lorentzian import, or S1/claim/canon/public-posture movement?

## Setup

T531 consumes T530's route and builds a pre-execution admission contract for the
only packet shape T530 admitted for future review:

- the law is declared before sampling;
- the observable descends from finality-domain data;
- the packet directly targets ordering fraction, T530's primary failure axis;
- independent naturality or neighboring-theory support is supplied;
- hostile controls are named and independent of the repaired-suite screen;
- the sample family is predeclared;
- later Lorentzian, locality, covariance, embedding, and continuum burdens are
  named before execution;
- no Lorentzian reference law is imported;
- no T528/T530 screen conditioning is used;
- no S1, claim, canon, public-posture, external-publication, or cross-repo
  movement is requested.

## Success Criteria

- T531 preserves T530's primary failure axis as ordering fraction.
- Exactly one packet shape is admitted as a pre-execution review contract.
- Post-hoc band fitting, Lorentzian-reference import, secondary-axis repair,
  missing-naturality packets, and posture-movement shortcuts are rejected or
  blocked.
- Every decision counts as no S1 evidence.
- The result changes no claim/canon/public posture.

## Failure Criteria

T531 fails if:

- it treats the contract as generator success;
- it admits a screen-conditioned or Lorentzian-imported law;
- it admits a packet that does not address ordering fraction;
- it admits a packet without independent naturality or hostile controls;
- it moves S1, claim status, Canon Index tiers, canon verdicts, public posture,
  external-publication state, or cross-repo truth.
