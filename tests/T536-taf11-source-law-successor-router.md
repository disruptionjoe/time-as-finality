# T536: TAF11 Source-Law Successor Router

## Target Claims

- TAF11: North Star source-law reassessment beyond the finite-generator route.
- [S1: Spacetime As Consensus Envelope](../claims/S1-spacetime-consensus-envelope.md)
- [T534: New Finality-Domain Law Pause Router](T534-new-finality-domain-law-pause-router.md)
- [T535: Real TAF10 Packet Screen](T535-real-taf10-packet-screen.md)

## Central Question

After T534 pauses the S1 finite-generator route and T535 finds no real C(R)
packet, what is the next honest Track-1 source-law move?

T536 chooses among successor shapes without letting a finishable branch outrank
the North Star merely because it is finishable.

## Candidate Route Shapes

T536 evaluates:

- a source-law family and falsifier packet;
- a shadow-protection theorem feeder packet;
- changing the target statistic before a law exists;
- waiting for a data-bearing C(R) packet as a Track-1 replacement;
- repeating spent receipt-product or record-window finite-generator shapes;
- moving S1, claims, canon, public posture, or external state from the pause.

## Success Criteria

- The computation is deterministic and exits 0.
- T534 is consumed as a pause state with no cleared new source law.
- T535 is consumed as no real TAF10 packet in hand.
- Exactly one next route is selected.
- The selected route requires new finality-domain law families, independent
  naturality, predeclared falsifiers, and no target import.
- Spent finite-generator shapes are rejected.
- Track 2 does not replace Track 1.
- No S1, claim-ledger, canon, public-posture, external-publication, or
  cross-repo truth movement occurs.

## Failure Criteria

T536 fails if:

- it repeats the two-channel, three-channel, or record-window candidate shapes;
- it imports Lorentzian coordinates as the law;
- it changes the target statistic before a law or falsifier earns that change;
- it treats missing C(R) data as a reason to stop Track 1;
- it demotes the North Star because the finite-generator route is hard;
- it moves S1, claim status, Canon Index tiers, canon verdicts, public posture,
  external-publication state, or cross-repo truth.

## Boundary

T536 is a deterministic work-selection router only. It does not establish a
source law, prove S1, or update any governance or public posture surface.
