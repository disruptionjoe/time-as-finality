# T552: Observerse Protocol-Stack Independent Transfer Gate

## Target Claims

- S1 guarded context
- TAF11 source-law reassessment beyond the finite-generator route
- T550/T551 frozen five-layer Observerse protocol-stack contract
- T551 bounded native finality stress survivor

## Central Question

Can the frozen T550/T551 Observerse protocol stack transfer to a structurally
different native finality fixture without becoming self-confirming validation,
source-law promotion, TAF4 movement, or TAF8 movement?

## Setup

T552 consumes T551 as transfer authority. The five frozen layers remain:

- issuance -> freeze;
- admissibility -> incoherence;
- sybil/finality -> capture;
- consensus -> fragment;
- governance -> ossification under near-term rules.

The transfer fixture is a handoff/partition/repair family, not the T551 bounded
fixture replayed with renamed labels. It has disjoint record ids, more records
than T551's full-stack survivor, multiple phases, left/right/both partition
structure, one mirror capture attempt, one contradictory merge repair, and late
novelty beyond the near-term rule horizon.

## Success Criteria

- T551 selected T552 and the five-layer contract remains unchanged.
- The transfer fixture is declared before outcome evaluation.
- The transfer fixture is record-disjoint, multi-phase, and partitioned
  differently from T551.
- Complete stack rescues the transfer fixture.
- Removing issuance, admissibility, sybil/finality, consensus, or near-term
  governance yields the predicted collapse mode.
- Full-horizon precomputed rules rescue without governance, so governance
  remains conditional rather than unconditional.
- Hostile controls block T551 replay, post-hoc layer choice, APRD retuning,
  target import, TAF4/source-law shortcuts, TAF8 overreading, and claim/canon/
  public-posture movement.
- A single next gate is named for a generalization-boundary test.

## Failure Criteria

T552 fails if:

- the layer set changes after outcome reading;
- the transfer fixture is merely T551 replayed with renamed labels;
- any frozen collapse/rescue prediction fails on the transfer fixture;
- governance is dropped unconditionally or promoted as unconditional;
- APRD is retuned or replayed as the stack rule;
- cross-repo truth, Lorentzian target structure, or outcome labels are imported;
- independent transfer is treated as source-law proof;
- TAF4, TAF8, source-law status, claim rows, Canon Index tiers, canon verdicts,
  public posture, external publication, or cross-repo truth is moved.

## Implementation Result

Status: implemented.

T552 finds that the frozen five-layer stack transfers to a structurally
different native finality fixture. Complete stack rescues; single-layer removals
still produce freeze, incoherence, capture, fragment, or near-term
ossification; and the full-horizon governance control rescues.

T552 selects `t553_observerse_protocol_stack_generalization_boundary_gate` as
the next TAF11 gate. It does not validate Observerse, establish a source law,
prove a shadow-protection theorem, move TAF4, execute TAF8, or change
claim/canon/public posture.

gate, but it does not supply evidence strong enough for claim-ledger, Canon
Index, source-law, public-posture, TAF4, or TAF8 movement.
