## Setup

Construct a minimal causal record graph with at least three observer-systems:

- two observers with overlapping causal histories;
- one remote source whose signals are received but not directly locally participated in;
- one causally inaccessible region or horizon-bounded event.

The model must distinguish:

- record access through received signals;
- direct local participation in an event or region;
- later reconciliation between observers whose causal histories overlap;
- no-access regions whose records cannot enter the observer's causal graph.

## Success Criteria

- The model shows how observers can maintain locally consistent histories without assuming a single global consensus state.
- The model distinguishes remote observation from direct participation.
- Photons, gravitational waves, neutrinos, or other received signals from remote galaxies count as causal record access.
- Event horizons are represented as limits on direct classical record export, not as claims of unreality.
- Shared finality strengthens only where causal histories overlap or records later become mutually accessible.

## Failure Criteria

- The model implies that remote galaxies are unrendered or unreal merely because observers cannot travel there.
- The model denies ordinary astronomical observation by treating received photons as non-records.
- The model treats event horizons as transportation hubs, render gates, or proof that black holes connect distant galaxies.
- The model requires a hidden global commit order.
- The model cannot state what is computed, who computes it, or what differs from standard causal-access language.

## Worked Example: Voyager Heliopause Crossings

The Voyager 1 and Voyager 2 heliopause crossings are a real-world instance of the three-region setup above. The solar-dominated interior, the interstellar medium beyond, and the boundary region around the heliopause map onto solar-domain-only records, interstellar-domain-only records, and an overlap region where both substrates contribute. Each spacecraft recorded an abrupt change in plasma density, magnetic-field character, and cosmic-ray or energetic-particle flux at its crossing, which is direct evidence that record-formation conditions change in character at the boundary rather than gradually.

`[citations verified]` Primary sources confirmed. Voyager 1 crossed the heliopause on 25 August 2012 at ~121.7 AU; Voyager 2 crossed on 5 November 2018 at ~119 AU. The abrupt transition character — not gradual — is supported by all instrument teams across both crossings.

Key papers:

- Stone et al. (2013), *Science* 341, 150–153, DOI: 10.1126/science.1236408 — Voyager 1 cosmic ray flux: >50% step increase in galactic cosmic ray nuclei; simultaneous drop in anomalous cosmic ray intensities.
- Burlaga, Ness, and Stone (2013), *Science* 341, 147–150, DOI: 10.1126/science.1235451 — Voyager 1 magnetic field: strength increased from ~0.2 to ~0.4 nT at boundary; direction did not rotate substantially.
- Gurnett et al. (2013), *Science* 341, 1489–1492, DOI: 10.1126/science.1241681 — Voyager 1 plasma density: electron density jumped from ~0.002 cm⁻³ (outer heliosphere) to ~0.08 cm⁻³ (interstellar medium), detected via plasma wave oscillations at ~2.6 kHz.
- Stone et al. (2019), *Nature Astronomy* 3, 1013–1018, DOI: 10.1038/s41550-019-0928-3 — Voyager 2 cosmic rays: sharp decrease in heliospheric ions and increase in galactic cosmic rays at crossing; no precursor flux tubes as seen by Voyager 1.
- Burlaga et al. (2019), *Nature Astronomy* 3, 1007–1012, DOI: 10.1038/s41550-019-0920-y — Voyager 2 magnetic field: stronger interstellar fields than Voyager 1 found; a magnetic barrier layer in the heliosheath adjacent to the heliopause.
- Richardson et al. (2019), *Nature Astronomy* 3, 1019–1023, DOI: 10.1038/s41550-019-0929-2 — Voyager 2 plasma: first direct plasma measurements at the HP; boundary layer ~1.5 AU wide before crossing; HP transition completed in less than one day.

This example targets [H-Domain and H-Soft-Boundary](../explorations/heliosphere-as-finality-domain.md), which use the heliosphere as a concrete finality domain and the heliopause as a soft boundary on the permeability spectrum.

## Known Physics Constraints

- Observational astronomy is already causal access through received records.
- Standard astrophysical black holes are not known to be traversable transportation networks.
- Relativity permits no universal present and no superluminal classical record transfer.
- Horizon language must remain compatible with black-hole information, holography, complementarity, and unitarity constraints where relevant.
