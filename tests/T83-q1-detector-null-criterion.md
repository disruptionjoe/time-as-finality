# T83: Q1 Detector Null Criterion

## Route

Quantum measurement / classical records.

## Question

When does the detector branch of Q1 add independent measurement content, and
when does it collapse to provenance bookkeeping over already formed records?

## Motivation

T66-T81 produced many useful boundary results but left the reader to assemble
their joint meaning. Without a compact null criterion, the detector branch is
too easy to overstate and too hard to falsify cleanly.

## Construction

Evaluate the detector branch against four null conditions:

- passive-statistics null: verdict reducible to calibrated outcomes,
  agreement, or other passive summaries;
- dashboard null: verdict reducible to coarse deployment summaries rather than
  event-level raw logs;
- post hoc partition null: verdict depends on a provenance partition fixed only
  after inspecting the desired classification;
- schema-overreach null: broad measured schema claimed in prose, but only a
  smaller subset is actually load-bearing in the executable witness family.

Then require one positive escape condition:

- a pre-registered raw-log protocol must produce a D1/provenance verdict that
  survives all four null reductions.

## Success Criteria

- The current detector branch is classified explicitly as null or non-null.
- The reason for that classification references T66-T81 rather than new
  rhetoric.
- At least one concrete future escape route is named.

## Failure Criteria

- The criterion still allows dashboard summaries or passive statistics to
  upgrade Q1.
- The criterion cannot distinguish a narrow admissibility gate from an
  independent detector claim.
- The criterion expands the detector branch without adding a new hostile case.

## Known Physics Constraints

- This is not a new detector dynamics model.
- No collapse, Born-rule, or no-signalling claim is added here.
- The artifact evaluates evidential content, not quantum ontology.

## Contribution Needed

Either produce a raw-log hostile family that escapes the null conditions, or
compress the detector branch so the repository no longer claims a broader
detector-side theory than the executable audits earn.
