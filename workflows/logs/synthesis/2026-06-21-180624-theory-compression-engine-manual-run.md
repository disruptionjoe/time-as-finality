## Mathematical Compression

Fix a source object `Y`, an observer/access boundary `O`, and a projection
`pi: Y -> X`. For a task `T`, horizon `h`, and boundary `B`, a projected record
`X` matters only insofar as a capability `Cap(T, h, B)` factors through it or has
a bounded residual spread under a declared comparison relation `R_K`.

When capability does not factor through `X`, the project asks whether a witness,
certificate, provenance object, or admissibility condition can repair the gap
without smuggling in hidden source access. When the repair is absorbed by a
native framework, the claim demotes. The lead TaF line is the finite
record-finality instantiation of this problem.

Minimal primitives:

- `Y`: source state or native object;
- `O`: observer/access boundary;
- `pi`: projection or forgetting map;
- `X`: visible record or shadow;
- `T, h, B`: task, horizon, and boundary;
- `Cap`: future operation or capability;
- `R_K`: native comparison relation;
- `W`: witness, certificate, or provenance object;
- `Adm`: admissibility predicate;
- `A`: absorber or rival framework;
- `D`: demotion rule.

## Philosophical Compression

The project separates vision from belief. The vision asks whether there is a
geometry whose shadows include physics-like observer worlds. The method allows
belief only when a typed projection, capability, witness obligation, and absorber
test survive. Research lines are local attempts to prove, weaken, absorb, or
kill parts of the vision.

## Intelligent-Outsider Compression

This repo asks whether what an observer can rely on later is determined by what
the observer can access now. It studies records, witnesses, and projections:
what gets preserved, what gets lost, what can be reconstructed, and when a
system is allowed to treat a record as final enough to act on.

## Shared Core

All three compressions retain the same core:

- observer-indexed projection;
- record sufficiency or insufficiency;
- future-operation availability;
- witness or certificate obligations;
- admissibility under a declared boundary;
- absorber tests and demotion.
