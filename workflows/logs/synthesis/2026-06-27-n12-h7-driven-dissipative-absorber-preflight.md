## Plain-English finding

If a record persists because a pump, bath, reset channel, decoder, or chemostat
keeps maintaining it, the maintenance machinery belongs in the accounting. That
is not yet H7 residue.

## Technical conclusion

Driven-dissipative systems are H7-adjacent but absorber-owned by default.
Current source-backed routes show stabilization, steady-state preparation,
autonomous correction, bistability, or phase selection under explicit drive and
environment structure. Those are not the same as a substrate-native
`physical_record_deletion` reverse that remains constructor-impossible after
matching:

- work and free-energy throughput;
- bath temperatures, reservoirs, and chemostats;
- housekeeping dissipation and currents;
- reset, decoder, and pump channels;
- sink/export history and source-copy correlations.

## Minimum next task

Require one named finite driven-dissipative substrate and one deletion task, with
drive, bath, reset, work, and control variables frozen before the audit runs.

## Stop condition

Stop the route if the claimed finality disappears when the pump, bath,
chemostat, autonomous correction, or control regime is included in the compared
state.
