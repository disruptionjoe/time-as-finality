## Fixed-H Null Boundary

The fixed-H null question is:

```text
Can ordinary decoherence / Quantum Darwinism / SBS explain the basis and
objectivity part while the trace rule supplies probabilities?
```

If yes, RSPS earns no source-side issuance, GU, Q1A, collapse, or Born-rule
credit. It remains a boundary showing what the neighbors can already absorb.

Required separation:

| Component | RSPS may discuss | RSPS must not claim |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Pointer basis | Record-fidelity/objectivity terms select the monitored axis in fixed-H finite sweeps. | A new preferred-basis mechanism beyond decoherence/QD/SBS. |
| Objectivity | Redundant, accessible, agreement-like records can be scored. | Novel quantum objectivity after SBS/strong-QD data are granted. |
| Probabilities | Born weights remain trace-rule data. | Born-rule derivation or replacement. |

Treat an RSPS-derived proposal as null, absorbed, or demoted if any of the
following occur:

- The score imports Born weights or treats record-fidelity as a probability
  rule.
- The proposal claims collapse dynamics.
- The proposal claims new pointer-basis physics without holding the standard
  decoherence/QD/SBS objectivity package fixed.
- The proposal withholds fragment partition, access subset, or provenance data
  from the neighboring framework.
- The alleged split disappears after the neighbor is granted the same
  monitored-axis, fragment, objectivity, access, and provenance data.

  support.

  record-fidelity sweep.
